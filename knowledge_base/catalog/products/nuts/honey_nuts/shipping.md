# Shipping
## Temperature Requirements

* **Sensitivity:** Honey Nuts (মধুময় বাদাম) are sensitive to sustained high temperatures. While the product is not strictly perishable or requiring refrigeration during transit, extreme heat exceeding 35°C (95°F) can cause the natural honey coating to liquefy, thin out, and settle at the bottom of the container. This can lead to clumping of the nuts and an uneven distribution of flavor.
* **Storage in Transit:** Consignments should be kept in cool, dry, and well-ventilated sections of transit vehicles. Avoid placing the product under direct sunlight or near vehicle heat sources (such as engine walls).
* **Seasonal Extensions:** During peak summer months in Bangladesh (April to September), domestic transit operators are advised to expedite deliveries or schedule dispatches during cooler parts of the day (evening/early morning) to mitigate heat exposure.

---

## Packaging Notes

* **Primary Container:** The product is packaged in food-grade, airtight plastic or glass jars sealed with an inner induction foil matrix. This seal prevents honey leakage, blocks ambient moisture, and preserves the crispness of the roasted nuts.
* **Fragility:** Glass jar variants are highly fragile and require individual bubble-wrap sleeve cushioning. Plastic jar variants, while impact-resistant, are prone to crushing under heavy top loads.
* **Master Carton Requirements:** Jars must be packed vertically inside corrugated master boxes. Use divider inserts (cardboard partitions) to eliminate jar-to-jar friction and impact during transit handling. Heavy stacking on top of Honey Nuts master cartons is prohibited.
* **Orientation Marking:** Outer cartons must clearly display "This Side Up" orientation arrows and "Fragile / Handle With Care" warning labels.

---

## Regulatory Considerations

* **Domestic Standards:** For distribution within Bangladesh, production batches adhere to food safety guidelines enforced by the Bangladesh Standards and Testing Institution (BSTI).
* **Labeling Compliance:** Labels on all variants (250g, 500g, 1kg) explicitly contain production dates, expiry timelines, batch numbers, net weights, and clear allergen warnings regarding tree nuts (almonds, cashews) and honey.
* **Sanitation Documentation:** Warehouses and transit hubs must possess up-to-date pest control certificates, as the naturally sweet honey aroma can attract insects or rodents if packaging integrity is breached.

---

## International Shipping

* **Agricultural & Food Controls:** Honey and raw/roasted tree nuts face strict import regulations in many destination countries (e.g., FDA in the United States, EFSA in the European Union, and custom protocols in Middle Eastern regions).
* **Documentation Required:** International dispatches generally require a Phytosanitary Certificate, a Certificate of Origin (Bangladesh), and a detailed Ingredients Statement specifying the exact heat treatments applied to the nuts.
* **Customs Restrictions:** Certain nations impose strict biosecurity bans or heavy tariffs on unprocessed or minimally processed honey products to protect local bee populations from diseases like American Foulbrood. Always verify the current import rules of the destination country before completing international bookings.
* **Transit Duration:** Air freight is highly recommended over sea freight for international orders to avoid prolonged exposure to changing humidity and temperature levels inside ocean containers.
