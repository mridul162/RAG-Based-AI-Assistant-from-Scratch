# Usage
# Honey Nuts (মধুময় বাদাম) Usage Guide

<section>

## Best uses

### Direct snacking

* Consume directly from the pack as a ready-to-eat snack.
* Ideal for:

  * Office breaks
  * Study sessions
  * Travel snacks
  * Evening tea-time
  * Midday energy boosts

### Healthy alternative to processed snacks

* Can replace highly processed snacks such as chips, candy, or sugary biscuits in moderate portions.
* Suitable for users looking for a more nutrient-dense snack option.

### Tea-time and guest serving

* Commonly served with:

  * Milk tea
  * Black tea
  * Coffee
  * Green tea
* Works well in snack platters during family gatherings or guest visits.

### Breakfast and dessert topping

* Adds crunch and sweetness to:

  * Yogurt bowls
  * Oatmeal
  * Smoothie bowls
  * Ice cream
  * Fruit salads
  * Custards and puddings

### Fitness and travel use

* Convenient portable snack for:

  * Gym bags
  * Road trips
  * Hiking
  * Long workdays
* Useful when quick energy and satiety are needed.

## Storage and handling

### Storage instructions

* Store in a cool, dry place.
* Keep away from direct sunlight and humidity.
* Use airtight containers after opening for best freshness.

### Shelf-life handling tips

* Use dry spoons or clean hands while serving.
* Avoid exposure to moisture to maintain crunchiness.

---

## What to avoid

### Avoid excessive intake

* Honey Nuts are energy-dense and calorie-rich.
* Large portions may contribute to excess calorie consumption.

### Avoid moisture exposure

* Humidity may soften the honey coating and reduce crispness.

### Avoid high heat storage

* Heat exposure may affect flavor, texture, and honey quality.

### Allergy caution

* Contains tree nuts and may not be suitable for individuals with nut allergies.

### Infant caution

* Products containing honey should not be given to infants under 12 months.

### Avoid improper storage

* Do not store near strong-smelling foods or damp environments, as nuts can absorb odors and moisture.
