# Storage
## Ideal Conditions (আদর্শ সংরক্ষণ পরিবেশ)

Sugarcane powder gur is highly hygroscopic (it readily absorbs moisture from the surrounding air). Because it is unrefined and contains natural molasses and mineral salts, improper storage will cause the powder to clump, harden, or spoil.

* **Humidity:** Keep relative humidity levels below 60%. High humidity is the primary cause of product degradation and clumping.
* **Temperature:** Store in a cool, dry place between 15°C and 25°C. Keep away from direct sunlight, gas stoves, ovens, or other heat sources, as elevated temperatures can cause the natural sugars to melt or become sticky.
* **Monsoon Protection:** During the Bangladeshi monsoon season (high humidity periods), extra care must be taken to ensure the storage container is completely sealed immediately after every single use.

---

## Container Recommendations (পাত্র নির্বাচনের নির্দেশনা)

The choice of storage container is critical to extending the shelf life of powdered jaggery.

* **Ideal Containers:**
* Air-tight glass jars with a silicone gasket or rubber seal.
* Food-grade, high-density polyethylene (HDPE) plastic containers with tightly screwing lids.


* **Avoid:**
* Metal containers (unless lined), as the natural moisture and mineral acids in jaggery can react with metal surfaces over time.
* Paper bags, open polythene packets, or loose containers that allow air exchange.


* **Handling Protocol:** Always use a completely clean, bone-dry stainless steel or wooden spoon to scoop out the powder. Introducing even a single drop of water or ambient steam from cooking into the container will initiate spoilage.

---

## Shelf Life (সংরক্ষণকাল)

When stored according to the recommended conditions, the product maintains its optimal flavor profile and physical properties within the timelines outlined below:

| Packaging Status | Expected Shelf Life | Storage Location | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Unopened / Original Sealed Pack** | 12 Months | Cool, dark pantry | Keep away from floor dampness. |
| **Opened (Standard Storage)** | 6 Months | Air-tight jar in pantry | Must be sealed tightly after every use. |
| **Opened (Refrigerated Storage)** | 12 Months | Refrigerator (4°C–8°C) | Recommended during peak monsoon months to prevent clumping. Bring container to room temperature before opening to avoid condensation. |

---

## Signs of Spoilage (নষ্ট হওয়ার লক্ষণসমূহ)

Customers should inspect the powder if it has been exposed to improper storage conditions. The following are definitive indicators of quality degradation or spoilage:

* **Sour or Fermented Odor:** A distinct alcoholic, sour, or vinegar-like smell indicates that ambient moisture has triggered yeast fermentation within the molasses.
* **Mold Growth:** The appearance of white, grey, or greenish fuzzy spots on the surface of the powder means water contamination has occurred, and the product must be discarded entirely.
* **Severe Liquefaction:** While minor clumping is normal and can be broken apart with a dry fork, if the powder turns into a dark, sticky liquid or gooey syrup at the bottom of the container, it has absorbed excessive moisture.
* **Insect Infestation:** Unsealed containers can attract ants, pantry pests, or fruit flies due to the high natural sugar concentration.
