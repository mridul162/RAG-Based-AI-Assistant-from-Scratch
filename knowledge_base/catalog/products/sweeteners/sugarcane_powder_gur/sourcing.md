# Sourcing
## Geographic Origin | ভৌগোলিক উৎস

Sugarcane Powder Gur (আখের পাউডার গুড়) is sourced from traditional sugarcane-growing regions of Bangladesh, particularly **Rajshahi, Faridpur, and Kushtia**. These areas have a long history of sugarcane cultivation due to their fertile alluvial soils, favorable climate, and established gur-making traditions.

The sugarcane grown in these regions is valued for its natural sweetness and juice yield, making it suitable for producing traditional cane jaggery (gur). Local farmers typically cultivate sugarcane using seasonal agricultural cycles that have been practiced for generations.

## Harvest Method | সংগ্রহ ও উৎপাদন পদ্ধতি

Sugarcane is harvested when the stalks reach maturity and contain maximum natural sugars. The harvested cane is typically processed soon after cutting to preserve freshness and juice quality.

### Traditional Production Process

1. Mature sugarcane stalks are harvested from the fields.
2. The cane is crushed to extract fresh juice.
3. The juice is filtered to remove coarse impurities.
4. The filtered juice is slowly boiled in large pans over controlled heat.
5. Water naturally evaporates as the juice thickens and concentrates.
6. The concentrated syrup develops into traditional cane gur.
7. The finished gur is cooled, dried, and processed into a fine powder form.
8. The powder is screened and packed for distribution.

This process relies primarily on evaporation and concentration rather than industrial sugar refining.

## Season & Timing | মৌসুম ও সংগ্রহকাল

Sugarcane Powder Gur is a **seasonal product** because its production depends on the annual sugarcane harvest.

### Typical Production Period

* Sugarcane harvesting generally occurs during the cooler months.
* Fresh gur production is most active during the winter season.
* Availability is often highest shortly after the harvesting and processing period.

Because gur is produced from freshly extracted sugarcane juice, the quality, flavor, color, and aroma may vary slightly between production batches and harvest seasons.

### Seasonal Characteristics

* Freshly produced gur often has a more pronounced aroma.
* Color may range from light golden brown to deeper brown depending on the harvest and processing conditions.
* Seasonal variations are normal and do not necessarily indicate quality differences.

### Handling and Packaging

After production, the powdered gur is typically:

* Allowed to cool and stabilize
* Processed into a fine granular texture
* Screened to improve consistency
* Packed in moisture-resistant packaging
* Stored in dry conditions before distribution

Proper packaging helps protect the product from humidity, which can cause clumping and affect shelf stability.

## Sustainability & Traditional Sourcing Notes | টেকসই ও ঐতিহ্যভিত্তিক সংগ্রহ

Sugarcane Powder Gur is closely connected to Bangladesh's traditional rural food economy. Production often supports:

* Small and medium-scale farming households
* Local agricultural communities
* Traditional gur-making skills and knowledge
* Seasonal rural employment opportunities

Because the product is derived directly from sugarcane juice and undergoes relatively limited processing compared to refined sugar, it remains closely linked to traditional agricultural production methods.

## Sourcing Summary | সংক্ষিপ্তসার

Sugarcane Powder Gur is sourced from the sugarcane-growing regions of Rajshahi, Faridpur, and Kushtia in Bangladesh. Freshly harvested sugarcane is crushed, its juice is concentrated through traditional boiling, and the resulting gur is processed into a convenient powder form. The product is seasonal, rooted in traditional farming practices, and moves through a relatively straightforward supply chain from farm to consumer while preserving the authentic character of traditional Bangladeshi cane gur.
