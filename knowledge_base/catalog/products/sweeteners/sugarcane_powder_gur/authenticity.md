# Authenticity Guide — আখের পাউডার গুড় (Sugarcane Powder Gur)

**Product ID:** `sugarcane_powder_gur`
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-11
**Category:** Sweeteners › Jaggery

> This guide helps buyers verify that they are receiving genuine, unadulterated Bangladeshi sugarcane powder gur — not synthetic substitutes, blended fillers, or mislabeled products.

---

## Visual Indicators — চাক্ষুষ পরিচয়

<section id="visual">

| Attribute | Authentic Product | Red Flag |
|---|---|---|
| **Color** | Warm golden-brown to deep amber; slight variation batch to batch due to natural sugarcane | Uniform bright yellow or stark white — indicates bleaching or synthetic sugar base |
| **Texture** | Fine, free-flowing powder with occasional very small soft clumps from natural moisture | Hard, crystalline granules identical to refined sugar; or chalky-white dusty appearance |
| **Clumping behavior** | Mild hygroscopic clumping in humid conditions — clumps break apart easily with finger pressure | Rock-hard, stone-like lumps that do not break easily suggest added starch or binding agents |
| **Particle consistency** | Mostly uniform fine grind; tiny darker specks (from cane fiber) are normal and a good sign | Perfectly uniform, zero specks — over-processed or synthetic base |
| **Residue on white paper** | Leaves a faint brown-amber smear | Leaves a pure white, odorless residue — likely refined sugar powder |

**Natural color variation is expected.** Gur from Rajshahi tends toward deeper amber; Faridpur and Kushtia batches may be slightly lighter depending on harvest season and juice concentration.

</section>

## Taste & Aroma Indicators — স্বাদ ও গন্ধের পরিচয়

<section id="sensory">

Genuine sugarcane powder gur has a layered flavor profile that refined or synthetic alternatives cannot replicate:

**Aroma (গন্ধ)**
- Rich, warm caramel-molasses scent when the bag is first opened
- Slight earthy, grassy undertone from raw sugarcane — this is authentic and desirable
- Aroma intensifies when dissolved in warm liquid (tea, milk)
- **Absent aroma or a sharp chemical sweetness = likely adulterated**

**Taste (স্বাদ)**
- Initial sweetness that is rounder and less sharp than white sugar
- A lingering caramel-molasses finish on the palate
- Mild mineral depth — not one-dimensional
- Dissolves smoothly in warm liquid without leaving a gritty or starchy film

**Dissolution test (গরম পানিতে পরীক্ষা)**
1. Add 1 teaspoon of powder to half a cup of warm water and stir.
2. Authentic gur dissolves fully into a clear amber-brown liquid with a pleasant molasses smell.
3. **Failure indicators:** milky/cloudy water (starch filler), white undissolved sediment (chalk or calcium carbonate), or colorless solution with strong sweetness (synthetic sweetener blend).

</section>

## Lab / Certification Checks — ল্যাব ও সনদ যাচাই

<section id="certification">

Bangladesh does not yet have a mandated certification specific to traditional gur, but the following checks are applicable:

| Check | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| **BSTI mark** | Look for Bangladesh Standards and Testing Institution (BSTI) compliance mark on packaging if commercially sold |
| **No artificial additive declaration** | Packaging should clearly state free from artificial colors, flavors, and preservatives |
| **Batch & harvest traceability** | Reputable suppliers provide the source region (Rajshahi / Faridpur / Kushtia) and approximate harvest season |
| **Moisture content** | Acceptable range: 3–8%. Above 10% accelerates spoilage and suggests improper processing or water addition to increase weight |
| **Sulphur dioxide (SO₂) test** | Authentic gur should test negative or near-zero for SO₂. Sulphur is used industrially to bleach and preserve adulterated gur |
| **Sucrose purity** | Pure sugarcane gur retains natural reducing sugars (glucose, fructose) alongside sucrose. A lab showing near-100% sucrose purity suggests refined sugar has been added |

**For bulk or institutional buyers:** Request a Certificate of Analysis (CoA) from the supplier covering moisture, sulphate, and reducing sugar content.

</section>

## Common Fakes to Watch For — ভেজাল পণ্য চেনার উপায়

<section id="adulteration">

The following adulteration practices are documented in the Bangladeshi and South Asian jaggery market:

**1. Refined Sugar Powder Blend (চিনি মেশানো)**
The most common fraud. Refined white sugar is ground fine and mixed into gur powder — sometimes at ratios as high as 50/50. This cuts cost significantly while appearing visually similar.
- *How to spot it:* Color appears lighter and more uniform than authentic gur; taste is sharper and one-dimensional; aroma is weak or absent.

**2. Starch or Flour Filler (ময়দা বা স্টার্চ মেশানো)**
Maida (refined flour) or corn starch is added as a cheap bulking agent.
- *How to spot it:* Dissolution test shows cloudiness or white sediment; texture may feel slightly sticky; taste has a flat, floury aftertaste.

**3. Artificial Color Addition (কৃত্রিম রং)**
Light-colored or refined-sugar-based powder is tinted with caramel color or synthetic brown food dye to mimic authentic gur color.
- *How to spot it:* Color is suspiciously uniform, too deep, or slightly reddish-orange rather than natural warm amber; aroma does not match the visual richness; a drop of the dissolved liquid on a white cloth may leave a dye stain after washing.

**4. Date Palm or Palm Gur Substitution (খেজুর/তাল গুড় দিয়ে প্রতারণা)**
Palm jaggery (khejur gur) or palmyra gur is sold as sugarcane gur. While both are natural products, they have different flavor profiles and sugarcane gur commands a premium.
- *How to spot it:* Date palm gur has a distinctly sharper, almost smoky aroma; color is often darker and more red-brown; taste has a date-fruit note absent in sugarcane gur.

**5. Chalk or Calcium Carbonate Addition (চুন মেশানো)**
Occasionally used to increase weight or adjust pH.
- *How to spot it:* Dissolution test shows white gritty sediment that does not dissolve; taste has a faint alkaline or chalky aftertaste. A few drops of vinegar on the powder will cause visible fizzing if chalk is present.

</section>

## Quick Field Verification Checklist — মাঠ পর্যায়ে যাচাই তালিকা

```
[ ] Color is warm golden-brown to deep amber (not white, not uniform bright yellow)
[ ] Faint natural molasses-caramel aroma present when bag is opened
[ ] Powder is fine and free-flowing; clumps break apart easily
[ ] Dissolves into clear amber liquid in warm water — no cloudiness or white sediment
[ ] Taste has round sweetness with molasses finish — not sharp like refined sugar
[ ] Tiny dark specks from cane fiber visible (positive indicator)
[ ] Packaging states source region (Rajshahi / Faridpur / Kushtia) and harvest season
[ ] No fizzing when a pinch is placed in a drop of vinegar (no chalk)
```
