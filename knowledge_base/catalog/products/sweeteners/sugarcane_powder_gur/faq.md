### 1. What exactly is Sugarcane Powder Gur? এটা কী?

Sugarcane Powder Gur (আখের পাউডার গুড়) is traditional Bangladeshi jaggery made by boiling fresh sugarcane juice in open pans until it solidifies, then grinding it into a fine powder. No chemicals, bleaching agents, or artificial additives are used in the process. The result is a natural sweetener that retains the minerals, trace nutrients, and flavor compounds that are stripped out during the refining of white sugar.

It is the same product as traditional solid patali or cube gur — just in a more convenient powdered form.

---

### 2. Is this the same as khejur gur (date palm jaggery)? খেজুর গুড়ের সাথে পার্থক্য কী?

No. These are two distinct products from two different sources.

- **Sugarcane Powder Gur** is made from sugarcane juice. It has a warm caramel-molasses flavor and is produced in regions like Rajshahi, Faridpur, and Kushtia.
- **Khejur Gur** is made from date palm sap, harvested only in winter (November–February), and has a distinctly smokier, more complex flavor with date-fruit notes.

Both are authentic traditional products, but they taste different and suit different recipes. This product is sugarcane-based only.

### 6. How should I store it? কীভাবে সংরক্ষণ করবো?

Sugarcane powder gur is hygroscopic — it absorbs moisture from the air, which causes clumping. Proper storage is important:

- Keep in an **airtight container** after opening
- Store in a **cool, dry place** away from direct sunlight and heat
- Avoid storing near the stove or in humid kitchen areas
- **Do not refrigerate** unless your storage area is unusually hot and humid — condensation inside the container accelerates clumping and spoilage

**Sealed (unopened):** 6–9 months from production date
**After opening:** Best used within 1–3 months

If mild clumping occurs, it does not indicate spoilage — break the clumps apart and use normally. Discard if you notice any off-smell, visible mold, or fermented odor.

### 7. Can I use it as a 1:1 replacement for white sugar? চিনির বদলে সমান পরিমাণে ব্যবহার করা যাবে?

For most everyday uses — tea, coffee, milk, smoothies, porridge — yes, a 1:1 substitution works well.

For **baking**, note that gur has a higher moisture content and a distinct flavor compared to refined sugar. In recipes where sugar structure matters (e.g., creamed butter cakes, meringues), a direct swap may affect texture and rise. For traditional Bangladeshi recipes like pitha, payesh, and halwa, gur is the intended sweetener and no adjustment is needed.

Start with a slightly smaller amount than the recipe calls for in sugar, taste, and adjust — gur's sweetness can be marginally more intense per gram depending on the batch.

### 8. What recipes is it best suited for? কোন রান্নায় সবচেয়ে ভালো?

The powder form is particularly convenient for:

- **Hot beverages** — cha (tea), dudh (milk), rong cha
- **Smoothies and lassi** — dissolves without needing to blend solids
- **Traditional sweets** — payesh, firni, mishti doi, halwa
- **Pitha** — chitoi, bhapa, patishapta fillings
- **Baking** — cookies, banana bread, muffins where a caramel note is welcome
- **Everyday cooking** — marinades, chutneys, dal tadka finishing

Its fine powder form means it dissolves faster than granulated sugar, making it especially practical for cold or lukewarm applications.

### 9. Is it healthier than refined white sugar? স্বাস্থ্যের জন্য কি বেশি উপকারী?

It is more nutritionally complete than refined white sugar. Gur retains naturally occurring minerals from sugarcane juice — primarily iron, calcium, potassium, and magnesium — which are stripped out during the industrial refining of white sugar. It also has a modestly lower glycemic index (approximately 50–55 versus 65–70 for white sugar).

However, it is still a sugar-based sweetener. People managing diabetes, blood sugar conditions, or caloric intake should treat it with the same caution as any sweetener and consult their doctor before making significant dietary changes. The mineral content per typical serving is real but modest — it is not a supplement.

The most accurate framing: it is a **less processed, more nutritionally intact** sweetener — not a health food in isolation.

### 11. How do I know if the product I received is genuine? আসল পণ্য কীভাবে চিনবো?

Key indicators of authentic product:

- Warm golden-brown to deep amber color — not white, not uniformly bright yellow
- Clear caramel-molasses aroma when the package is opened

- Taste is round and mildly complex — not the sharp, flat sweetness of refined sugar
- Tiny dark specks from cane fiber may be visible — this is normal and a positive sign

### 12. The powder has clumped in the bag. Is it still usable? জমে গেছে — ব্যবহার করা যাবে?

Yes, in most cases. Clumping is a natural behavior of gur powder caused by moisture absorption — it does not automatically mean the product has gone bad.

- If the clumps break apart easily with finger pressure and the product smells normal: **safe to use**
- If the clumps are hard as stone and the powder has a sour, fermented, or off smell: **discard**
- To prevent clumping going forward: transfer to an airtight container immediately after opening and store away from heat and humidity

### 13. Is this product suitable for diabetic customers? ডায়াবেটিক রোগীরা খেতে পারবেন?

This is not a product we can recommend as medically safe for diabetics. While sugarcane gur has a lower glycemic index than refined white sugar, it is still primarily sucrose and will raise blood glucose levels. The difference is a matter of degree, not kind.

Customers with diabetes or blood sugar management concerns should consult their physician or dietitian before incorporating any added sweetener — including gur — into their diet.
