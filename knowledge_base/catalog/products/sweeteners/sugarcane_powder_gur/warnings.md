# Warnings
## Allergen Information | অ্যালার্জি সম্পর্কিত তথ্য

Sugarcane Powder Gur is made from concentrated sugarcane juice and is not considered a major food allergen. However, consumers should be aware of the following considerations:

### Naturally Occurring Sensitivities

* Individuals with a known sensitivity or allergy to sugarcane should avoid this product.
* Rare individual reactions to sugarcane-derived products may occur.

### Processing and Handling Considerations

* Traditional gur production environments may not be allergen-controlled facilities.
* Cross-contact with environmental contaminants can occur during harvesting, processing, storage, or packaging if strict controls are not maintained.

### Signs of Possible Adverse Reaction

Discontinue use and seek medical advice if symptoms such as the following occur after consumption:

* Skin rash
* Itching
* Swelling
* Difficulty breathing
* Unusual digestive discomfort

## Who Should Avoid or Limit Consumption | কারা এড়িয়ে চলবেন বা সীমিত খাবেন

### People Managing Blood Sugar Levels

Sugarcane Powder Gur contains significant amounts of naturally occurring sugars and carbohydrates.

Individuals with:

* Diabetes mellitus
* Prediabetes
* Insulin resistance
* Other blood sugar management concerns

should monitor intake and follow guidance from their healthcare provider.

### Individuals on Sugar-Restricted Diets

People following medically prescribed low-sugar, reduced-carbohydrate, or calorie-controlled diets may need to limit consumption.

### People with Obesity or Weight-Management Goals

Like all caloric sweeteners, excessive intake may contribute to excess calorie consumption.

### Individuals with Certain Dental Concerns

Frequent consumption of sugary foods and beverages may contribute to tooth decay if good oral hygiene is not maintained.

### Infants and Young Children

Sugarcane Powder Gur is not known to carry the same infant safety concerns associated with raw honey. However:

* Added sugars are generally not recommended for infants under 12 months unless advised by a healthcare professional.
* Use age-appropriate feeding practices.

## Drug / Condition Interactions | ওষুধ ও স্বাস্থ্যগত অবস্থার সাথে সম্পর্ক

### Diabetes Medications

Because Sugarcane Powder Gur contains substantial amounts of sugar, consumption may affect blood glucose levels.

Individuals using:

* Insulin
* Oral diabetes medications
* Other blood sugar-lowering treatments

should account for the carbohydrate content when planning meals.

### Blood Sugar Monitoring

People managing diabetes or metabolic conditions should monitor their blood sugar response when introducing or increasing intake.

### Medical Conditions Requiring Controlled Sugar Intake

Consult a healthcare professional before regular use if you have:

* Diabetes
* Metabolic syndrome
* Certain liver conditions
* Conditions requiring strict carbohydrate management

### No Known Major Drug Interactions

There are currently no widely recognized direct drug interactions specific to traditional sugarcane jaggery. However, its sugar content may indirectly affect dietary management plans for certain medical conditions.

## Safe Usage Limits | নিরাপদ ব্যবহারের সীমা

### General Consumption Guidance

Sugarcane Powder Gur should be treated as an added sweetener and consumed in moderation.

### Avoid Excessive Intake

Excessive consumption may contribute to:

* Increased calorie intake
* Elevated blood sugar levels
* Weight gain over time
* Increased risk of dental cavities

## Storage Safety | সংরক্ষণ সংক্রান্ত সতর্কতা

### Protect From Moisture

Store in a cool, dry place and keep the container tightly sealed. Moisture can cause:

* Clumping
* Hardening
* Reduced shelf stability

### Use Clean, Dry Utensils

Always use a dry spoon when handling the product to minimize contamination and moisture exposure.

### Inspect Before Use

Do not consume if the product shows signs of:

* Mold growth
* Unusual odor
* Insect infestation
* Water damage
* Significant spoilage

## Important Note | গুরুত্বপূর্ণ তথ্য

Sugarcane Powder Gur is a traditional sweetener, not a medicine or dietary supplement. It should not be used to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease. While it contains small amounts of naturally occurring minerals, it remains a concentrated source of sugar and should be consumed as part of a balanced diet.
