## Overview

Sugarcane Patali Gur (আখের পাটালি গুর) is a traditional, unrefined jaggery made by boiling and solidifying fresh sugarcane juice into dense, molded blocks. Unlike refined white sugar, it retains the natural minerals, trace compounds, and flavor of the source cane — resulting in a sweetener that is both functional and distinctly flavorful. It is a staple in Bangladeshi households and a foundational ingredient in Bengali culinary tradition.

The name "patali" refers to its solid, molded form — typically shaped into discs or short cylinders — as opposed to liquid or granular jaggery. This form makes it easy to store, portion, and use in both cooking and direct consumption.

The flavor profile is notably different from both refined sugar (which is neutral) and date palm gur / khejur gur (which is more floral and seasonal). Sugarcane patali gur has a consistent, year-round availability and a deeper, more robust sweetness.

## Origin & Background

This product is sourced from the sugarcane-growing belts of Bangladesh — primarily **Rajshahi**, **Faridpur**, and **Kushtia** — regions with a long history of traditional gur production. Farmers and artisan producers in these areas follow generations-old methods: harvesting mature cane, extracting juice, slow-boiling it in large iron pans, and pouring the concentrated mass into molds to cool and harden.

The craft requires timing and experience; the final texture and color depend on how long the juice is reduced and at what temperature it is poured. Well-made patali gur holds its shape firmly but breaks cleanly, without crumbling or excessive stickiness.

## Why It Matters

Sugarcane patali gur serves a role that refined sugar simply cannot replicate in traditional Bengali recipes. It is the preferred sweetener for:

- **Payesh** (rice pudding) and **firni**
- **Pitha** (rice-flour cakes and dumplings)
- **Mishti** (Bengali sweets) and **sandesh**
- Everyday cooking where a caramel-depth sweetness is desired

Beyond taste, it is valued as a **chemical-free, minimally processed** alternative to white sugar — relevant to consumers seeking natural or "desi" sweeteners without artificial additives.

At **250 BDT/kg**, it offers an accessible price point for everyday household use, available year-round without seasonal restriction.
