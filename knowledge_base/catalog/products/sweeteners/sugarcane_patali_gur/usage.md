# Usage
# Usage Guide for Sugarcane Patali Gur (আখের পাটালি গুর)

<section>

## Best Uses

Sugarcane Patali Gur is a versatile traditional sweetener that can be used in both everyday cooking and special recipes. Its rich caramel-like flavor makes it particularly suitable for Bengali cuisine.

### Traditional Bengali Desserts

Common uses include:

* Payesh (rice pudding)
* Pitha (traditional rice cakes)
* Firni
* Semai (vermicelli desserts)
* Suji halwa
* Chira-based desserts

### Everyday Sweetening

Patali Gur can be used as a substitute for refined sugar in:

* Tea
* Milk
* Homemade drinks
* Porridge
* Oatmeal
* Yogurt

### Cooking Applications

Suitable for:

* Sweet curries
* Chutneys
* Dessert sauces
* Traditional sweets
* Homemade confectionery

### Baking Applications

Can be incorporated into:

* Cakes
* Muffins
* Cookies
* Sweet breads
* Energy bars

Its natural caramel notes often add deeper flavor than white sugar.

</section>

## Dosage / Serving Suggestions

### General Sweetening Guide

| Use Case              | Recommended Amount            |
| --------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| Tea or Coffee (1 cup) | 1–2 teaspoons (5–10g)         |
| Milk (1 glass)        | 1–2 teaspoons (5–10g)         |
| Oatmeal or Porridge   | 1–2 tablespoons (10–20g)      |
| Yogurt Bowl           | 1 tablespoon (10–15g)         |
| Payesh (4–6 servings) | 50–100g or according to taste |
| Pitha Preparation     | As required by recipe         |

### Sugar Replacement Guide

As a general rule:

* 100g refined sugar ≈ 90–110g Patali Gur

Actual amounts may vary depending on:

* Desired sweetness
* Moisture content of the gur
* Recipe requirements

### Daily Consumption

Patali Gur contains naturally occurring sugars and should be consumed in moderation as part of a balanced diet.

## Recipe Ideas

### Traditional Payesh

**Ingredients**

* Rice
* Milk
* Sugarcane Patali Gur
* Optional nuts and raisins

**Method**

1. Cook rice in milk until soft.
2. Allow the mixture to cool slightly.
3. Add grated or dissolved Patali Gur.
4. Stir thoroughly and serve warm or chilled.

**Tip:** Adding gur after cooking helps preserve its flavor and reduces the chance of milk curdling.

---

### Gur Tea

**Ingredients**

* Tea leaves
* Water
* Sugarcane Patali Gur

**Method**

1. Brew tea normally.
2. Add grated gur instead of sugar.
3. Stir until dissolved.

---

### Gur Oatmeal

**Ingredients**

* Oats
* Milk or water
* Grated Patali Gur
* Nuts or fruits

**Method**

1. Cook oats as usual.
2. Add gur near the end of cooking.
3. Mix well and serve.

---

### Pitha Filling

Patali Gur can be:

* Grated directly into coconut fillings.
* Melted and mixed with coconut.
* Combined with sesame seeds for traditional winter snacks.

---

### Homemade Sweet Sauce

1. Dissolve grated gur in a small amount of warm water.
2. Simmer briefly until smooth.
3. Use as a topping for pancakes, pitha, yogurt, or desserts.

## What to Avoid

### Do Not Use Excessive Heat for Extended Periods

Excessive heating may:

* Alter flavor
* Darken color further
* Reduce desirable aroma

Whenever possible, add gur toward the later stages of cooking.

### Avoid Adding Directly to Boiling Milk

In some recipes, acidic or impure jaggery can occasionally cause milk to curdle.

Best practice:

* Remove milk from high heat.
* Allow slight cooling.
* Add grated or dissolved gur gradually.

### Avoid Moisture Exposure

Do not:

* Store with wet utensils.
* Leave uncovered in humid conditions.
* Allow water to enter storage containers.

Moisture may encourage spoilage and texture changes.

### Avoid Overconsumption

Although less processed than refined sugar, Patali Gur remains a concentrated source of sugars.

Individuals who:

* Have diabetes,
* Monitor blood sugar levels,
* Follow medically prescribed diets,

should manage portions appropriately and consult a healthcare professional if needed.

### Do Not Use If Spoilage Is Present

Discard the product if you notice:

* Mold growth
* Fermentation smell
* Insect contamination
* Unusual sour odor
* Excessive moisture damage

## Quick Usage Summary

| Purpose      | Recommended Use                           |
| ------------ | ----------------------------------------- |
| Tea & Drinks | Dissolve grated gur as a sugar substitute |
| Payesh       | Add after cooking for best flavor         |
| Pitha        | Use in fillings and sweet mixtures        |
| Baking       | Replace refined sugar in many recipes     |
| Breakfast    | Mix into oats, yogurt, or porridge        |
| Snacks       | Pair with muri, chira, coconut, or nuts   |

**Key Tip:** Grating or breaking Patali Gur into small pieces before use makes it easier to dissolve, mix, and measure accurately in recipes.
