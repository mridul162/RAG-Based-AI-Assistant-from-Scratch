## Who Should Avoid or Limit | কে এড়িয়ে চলবেন

### ⚠️ Diabetes & Pre-Diabetes (ডায়াবেটিস)
- Sugarcane Patali Gur has a **high glycemic index (~84)** and is composed of **95–97% sugars**.
- It **is not a safe substitute for white sugar** for people managing blood glucose levels. The mineral content does not meaningfully offset the glycemic impact.
- Persons with Type 1, Type 2, or gestational diabetes should consult their physician or dietitian before consuming.

### ⚠️ Infants Under 1 Year (১ বছরের কম শিশু)
- Unlike honey, jaggery does **not carry a risk of botulism** in infants.
- However, the **WHO and national dietary guidelines do not recommend adding any sweeteners**, including jaggery, to the diet of children under 2 years of age.
- **Do not feed to infants under 1 year** without explicit medical guidance.

### ⚠️ Obesity & Weight Management (স্থূলতা)
- High caloric density (~383 kcal/100g) with almost entirely sugar-derived calories.
- Regular unrestricted use is not appropriate for calorie-restricted diets.

### ⚠️ Dental Health (দাঁতের স্বাস্থ্য)
- Like all fermentable sugars, jaggery contributes to dental caries. Individuals with active dental decay or on orthodontic treatment should limit frequency of consumption and maintain oral hygiene after use.

### ⚠️ Kidney Disease (কিডনি রোগ)
- Contains moderate-to-high levels of potassium (~300–400 mg/100g) and phosphorus.
- Individuals on a potassium- or phosphorus-restricted diet due to chronic kidney disease (CKD) should avoid or strictly limit intake. Consult a nephrologist or renal dietitian.

### ⚠️ Liver Disease (যকৃতের রোগ)
- High fructose and sucrose loads can burden the liver. Individuals with non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD), cirrhosis, or other liver conditions should limit intake.

### ⚠️ Hypoglycemia (নিম্ন রক্তশর্করা)
- May be used as a short-term quick energy source during hypoglycemic episodes, but this must be guided by a medical professional and is not a long-term management strategy.

## Drug & Condition Interactions | ওষুধ ও রোগের সাথে মিথস্ক্রিয়া

| Condition / Medication | Interaction | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| **Insulin / Oral hypoglycaemics** (e.g. Metformin, Glibenclamide) | May elevate blood glucose, requiring dose adjustment | Consult physician; monitor blood glucose closely |
| **Warfarin / Blood thinners** | No direct interaction known, but dietary sugar changes can affect INR indirectly | Maintain consistent dietary intake; inform prescribing physician |
| **Potassium-sparing diuretics** (e.g. Spironolactone) | Jaggery's potassium content (~300–400 mg/100g) may contribute to hyperkalaemia | Limit intake; seek dietary advice |
| **ACE Inhibitors** (e.g. Enalapril, Ramipril) | These drugs raise potassium retention; high-potassium foods add risk | Use caution; discuss with prescribing doctor |
| **Corticosteroids** | May amplify blood glucose spikes from high-sugar foods | Limit consumption during corticosteroid courses |
| **Iron supplementation** | Jaggery contains iron (~11 mg/100g); concurrent supplementation may cause over-correction in some cases | Inform physician of dietary jaggery intake |
| **PCOS (Polycystic Ovary Syndrome)** | Insulin resistance is common in PCOS; high-GI foods worsen metabolic markers | Limit intake; follow dietitian guidance |

> This table covers commonly relevant interactions. It is **not exhaustive**. Always disclose dietary changes to your healthcare provider if you are on any long-term medication.

## Safe Usage Limits | নিরাপদ ব্যবহারের পরিমাণ

| Population | Suggested Limit | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Healthy adults | **≤ 25–30g per day** (~1 small piece) | WHO free sugars guideline: <10% of total daily energy (~50g/day for 2000 kcal diet); jaggery counted as a free sugar |
| Children (2–12 years) | **≤ 10–15g per day** | Lower energy requirements; WHO recommends <5% free sugars for additional health benefit |
| Pregnant women | **≤ 20g per day** | Increased iron need may make moderate jaggery consumption beneficial, but total sugar intake should remain controlled; gestational diabetes risk |
| Diabetics | **Avoid or as directed by physician** | High GI; no safe threshold without medical supervision |
| Individuals with CKD | **As directed by renal dietitian** | Potassium and phosphorus restriction may apply |

> **Important:** The above limits apply to jaggery as part of total free sugar intake. Other sugar sources in the diet (tea, sweets, rice, fruit juice, processed foods) must be counted together. The WHO recommends keeping all free sugars below **10% of total daily energy intake** for healthy adults.

## General Handling & Storage Warnings | সংরক্ষণ ও ব্যবহারে সতর্কতা

- **Moisture exposure** can promote mold growth. Store in an airtight container in a cool, dry place. Do not use if mold is visible.
- **Insect contamination** is possible if stored improperly in open containers. Inspect before use.
- **Adulteration risk:** Some commercially available jaggery in Bangladesh may contain added chalk powder (calcium carbonate), synthetic colorants, or starch to increase weight or improve appearance. Purchase from verified sources. Our product is sourced from traditional producers without additives, but batch-level lab certification is not performed by default.
- **Hard texture:** Patali Gur is a solid block. Use a clean, dry knife or grater to break pieces. Avoid biting directly to prevent dental injury.
