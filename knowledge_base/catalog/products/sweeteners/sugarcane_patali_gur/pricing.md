# Pricing
## Price drivers

* **Raw Material Costs:** The foundational driver is the market price of fresh sugarcane crops in primary agricultural hubs like Rajshahi, Faridpur, and Kushtia. Fluctuations in seasonal harvests, fuel costs for transport, and crushed sugarcane juice yields directly dictate manufacturing overheads.
* **Artisanal Processing:** Traditional production involves crushing sugarcane, filtering the fresh juice, and boiling it in large open iron pans over open wood or mud stoves (*matir chula*). This labor-intensive manual boiling and manual molding into solid *patali* structures accounts for a significant portion of the cost.
* **Chemical-Free Premium:** Unlike low-grade market jaggery, our product does not rely on synthetic clearing agents (like hydros/sodium hydrosulfite), alum (*fitkiri*), artificial colorings, or flour stabilizers. Pure extraction commands an operational premium due to increased labor, stricter batch sorting, and lower overall yields per batch.
* **Moisture Loss:** During the dehydration and curing phases, significant moisture loss reduces mass. Packing purely concentrated, dense solid cakes means a high volume of raw sugarcane juice is compressed to deliver a single 1kg unit.

## Value justification

Our BDT 250 price point for the 1kg variant offers a balanced cost-to-benefit ratio anchored on health transparency and kitchen utility:

* **Zero Adulteration:** Eliminates health risks associated with the toxic chemical lighteners frequently added to generic open-market alternatives.
* **High Density and Sweetening Profile:** Because it contains no water-weight stretching or flour additives, a smaller quantity provides deep caramelized sweetening.
* **Culinary Stability:** The pure chemical-free nature ensures that when dissolved in hot milk for traditional recipes like *payesh*, *kheer*, or *pitha*, it minimizes the risk of enzymatic curdling common with low-grade jaggery.

## Cost vs alternatives

* **vs. Refined White Sugar (BDT 120 - BDT 140/kg):** White sugar is significantly cheaper because it is mass-processed via industrial automation. However, it undergoes heavy chemical refining and bleaching, stripping away all minerals. Sugarcane Patali Gur retains its natural iron, magnesium, potassium, and antioxidants, justifying its premium over empty-calorie white crystals.
* **vs. Date Palm Jaggery / Khejur Patali Gur (BDT 450 - BDT 600+/kg):** Date palm jaggery is highly seasonal (available almost exclusively in winter) and much rarer, driving its cost up to double or triple that of sugarcane jaggery. Sugarcane patali gur serves as an affordable, non-seasonal alternative that still delivers an authentic, deep caramel aesthetic and rich flavor profile to Bengali sweets year-round.
