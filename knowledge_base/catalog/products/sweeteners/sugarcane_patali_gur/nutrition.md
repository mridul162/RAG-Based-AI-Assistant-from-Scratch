| --------------------- | --------------------------- |
| Energy                | 370–390 kcal                |
| Carbohydrates         | 90–98 g                     |
| Total Sugars          | 85–95 g                     |
| Protein               | 0.2–0.5 g                   |
| Total Fat             | 0–0.2 g                     |
| Dietary Fiber         | 0–0.5 g                     |
| Moisture              | 2–8 g                       |
| Ash (Mineral Content) | 0.5–1.5 g                   |

### Estimated Glycemic Characteristics

| Parameter           | Typical Value            |
| ------------------- | ------------------------ |
| Glycemic Index (GI) | Approximately 70–84*     |
| Glycemic Load       | High                     |
| Sweetness Level     | Similar to refined sugar |

*GI values vary by production method and composition. Sugarcane jaggery is generally considered a high-glycemic sweetener and should be consumed in moderation.

## Key Micronutrients

Unlike refined white sugar, Sugarcane Patali Gur retains small amounts of naturally occurring minerals from sugarcane juice.

| Micronutrient | Typical Presence         |
| ------------- | ------------------------ |
| Iron          | Present in small amounts |
| Potassium     | Present                  |
| Calcium       | Present                  |
| Magnesium     | Present                  |
| Phosphorus    | Present                  |
| Manganese     | Trace amounts            |
| Zinc          | Trace amounts            |
| Copper        | Trace amounts            |

### Mineral Content Notes

* Mineral levels vary significantly between batches.
* Traditional processing methods may preserve more minerals than highly refined sweeteners.
* While jaggery contains minerals, it should not be relied upon as a primary source of micronutrients.

## Key Nutrients

### Natural Carbohydrates

* The primary component of Sugarcane Patali Gur is carbohydrate in the form of naturally occurring sugars from sugarcane juice.
* Provides a quick source of dietary energy.

### Naturally Occurring Minerals

* Contains small amounts of minerals retained during traditional concentration of sugarcane juice.
* Generally contains more mineral content than refined white sugar.

### Minimal Processing

* Produced by boiling and concentrating sugarcane juice rather than refining it into purified sugar crystals.
* Retains some of the natural compounds present in sugarcane juice.

### No Significant Fat or Protein

* Contains negligible amounts of fat.
* Contains negligible amounts of protein.
* Functions primarily as a sweetener and energy source rather than a protein- or fat-rich food.

### Compared with Refined Sugar

Sugarcane Patali Gur typically:

* Contains more naturally occurring minerals.
* Has a richer flavor profile.
* Undergoes less industrial processing.

However:

* Calorie content remains similar.
* Sugar content remains high.
* It should still be consumed in moderation.

### Compared with Date Palm Gur

Sugarcane Patali Gur:

* Has a stronger sugarcane and caramel flavor.
* Is usually available throughout more of the year.
* Is often more affordable than premium seasonal date palm gur.

Both products are traditional unrefined sweeteners and provide similar amounts of calories and sugars.

## Nutritional Considerations

* Sugarcane Patali Gur is a sweetener, not a nutritional supplement.
* Although it contains some minerals, it remains a concentrated source of sugar.
* Individuals with diabetes, insulin resistance, or those monitoring sugar intake should consume it cautiously and according to professional dietary advice.
* Portion size is an important factor when incorporating jaggery into a balanced diet.

</section>

<section>

## Batch Variation Notice

Because Sugarcane Patali Gur is an agricultural and traditionally processed product, nutritional composition can vary due to:

* Sugarcane variety.
* Soil and growing conditions.
* Region of production.
* Harvest season.
* Boiling and concentration methods.
* Moisture content.

Small differences in color, texture, sweetness, mineral content, and nutritional values are normal and expected.
