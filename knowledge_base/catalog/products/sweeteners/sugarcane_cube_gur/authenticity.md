**Product ID:** `sugarcane_cube_gur`
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-11
**Purpose:** Help buyers verify that the product they receive is genuine, unadulterated, traditional Bangladeshi sugarcane cube gur.

## Visual Indicators
### দৃশ্যমান পরিচিতি

<section id="visual">

Genuine sugarcane cube gur from regions like Rajshahi, Faridpur, and Kushtia has a distinct visual profile that sets it apart from adulterated or synthetic alternatives.

| Attribute | Authentic | Suspicious / Fake |
|---|---|---|
| **Color** | Deep amber to dark brown; may have slight variation batch to batch | Uniform bright yellow or stark white suggests added chemicals or excessive refining |
| **Surface texture** | Slightly rough, granular, or crystalline on the exterior | Perfectly smooth, glossy, or wet-looking surface |
| **Shape consistency** | Cubes or discs may have minor irregular edges — handcrafted variation is normal | Machine-perfect uniformity can indicate industrial processing |
| **Internal cross-section** | Dense, with visible fine crystal structure; may have small natural air pockets | Hollow, powdery, or gummy interior is a red flag |
| **Color under light** | Warm amber tones visible when held to light | Greenish or artificial orange tint is suspicious |
| **Moisture sheen** | Slight natural dryness on surface at room temperature | Sticky, wet, or weeping surface may indicate added glucose syrup |

> **Note:** Natural color variation across batches is expected and is actually a sign of authenticity. Sugarcane from Rajshahi and Kushtia can yield slightly different shades depending on the harvest season and cooking method.

</section>

## Taste & Aroma Indicators
### স্বাদ ও সুগন্ধের পরিচিতি

<section id="sensory">

Sensory evaluation is one of the most reliable methods for verifying authenticity. Perform this test with a small piece before using in bulk.

### Aroma

- **Authentic gur** releases a warm, smoky, caramel-molasses fragrance — earthy and slightly sweet, reminiscent of open-fire cooking.
- A faint sugarcane-field freshness may linger in well-preserved batches.
- **Adulterated gur** may smell neutral, chemically sweet (like refined sugar), or artificially fragrant — none of which are natural.

### Taste

- Dissolve a small piece on the tongue. Genuine cube gur has **layered sweetness** — initial mild caramel, followed by a deeper molasses note, sometimes with a very faint bitterness at the finish. This complexity is the hallmark of unrefined sugarcane processing.
- The sweetness should feel **rounded and full**, not sharp or one-dimensional like white sugar.
- **Adulterated or synthetic gur** tends to taste flat, overly sweet, or chemically sharp with no depth.

### Melt behavior

| Test | Authentic | Fake |
|---|---|---|
| **In hot water or tea** | Dissolves gradually; liquid turns deep amber-brown with a natural caramel aroma | Dissolves quickly but leaves a sugary film with no color or aroma development |
| **Between fingers** | Softens slightly with body heat; leaves a slightly sticky, brownish residue | Crumbles into white powder or does not soften |
| **In mouth** | Melts smoothly, no gritty chalk-like residue | Grittiness or a chemical aftertaste is a warning sign |

## Lab / Certification Checks
### পরীক্ষাগার ও সনদ যাচাই

<section id="certification">

For buyers purchasing in bulk or for commercial use, third-party verification adds an important layer of assurance.

### What to look for on the label or documentation

- **BSTI mark** — Bangladesh Standards and Testing Institution certification confirms the product has met national food safety standards.
- **No artificial color declaration** — Supplier should be able to confirm or document that no synthetic food coloring (e.g., tartrazine, sunset yellow) has been added.
- **Source region traceability** — Authentic product from Rajshahi, Faridpur, or Kushtia should have a verifiable supply chain. Ask the seller for sourcing documentation if buying in quantity.

### Lab tests that can verify authenticity

| Test | What It Detects |
|---|---|
| **Sucrose purity test** | Overly high sucrose concentration (>95%) suggests heavy refining or sugar syrup addition |
| **Mineral profile (ash content)** | Genuine gur retains iron, calcium, potassium; very low ash content indicates refined/adulterated product |
| **Heavy metal screen** | Rules out contamination from unclean processing equipment |
| **Artificial colorant panel** | Detects synthetic dyes sometimes used to mimic dark brown color |
| **Glucose/fructose ratio** | Abnormal ratios can indicate corn syrup or glucose syrup adulteration |

> These lab tests are recommended for commercial buyers, institutional buyers, or anyone sourcing large quantities. For household buyers, the visual and sensory checks above are sufficient for a first-pass authenticity check.

</section>

## Common Fakes to Watch For
### সাধারণ ভেজালের ধরন

<section id="adulteration">

Sugarcane gur adulteration is common in the Bangladeshi market, particularly outside harvest season. Here are the most frequently encountered methods and how to spot them:

### 1. Refined Sugar Syrup Addition

**What it is:** Cheap white or raw sugar dissolved and re-solidified with a small amount of real gur or caramel coloring.

**How to spot it:**
- Tastes flat and purely sweet with no molasses depth
- Dissolves too cleanly in water, leaving a colorless or very pale liquid
- Surface looks too clean and uniform for a handcrafted product
- Ash content will be near zero in a lab test

---

### 2. Artificial Coloring

**What it is:** White or pale jaggery blocks dyed dark brown or amber using food-grade (or sometimes non-food-grade) synthetic colorants.

**How to spot it:**
- Color rubs off on dry fingers — natural gur may transfer some residue but will not leave a distinct color stain
- Dissolve a piece in cold water; artificial dye will bleed out quickly in streaks, whereas natural gur produces a slow, even amber coloring
- Color appears too uniform and vibrant — natural gur has slight tonal variation

---

### 3. Glucose or Corn Syrup Addition

**What it is:** Glucose syrup blended in to increase weight and shelf life, giving the block a heavier, stickier feel.

**How to spot it:**
- Block feels unusually heavy for its size
- Surface may be slightly sticky or tacky even in cool, dry conditions
- In hot tea, leaves a slightly thick or syrupy texture rather than clean dissolution
- Taste is intensely sweet but without any complex or bitter finish

---

### 4. Chalk Powder or Calcium Carbonate Addition

**What it is:** Filler added to increase weight, common in loose or crumbled gur sold by weight.

**How to spot it:**
- Dissolve a piece in water — genuine gur leaves minimal sediment; chalk adulteration leaves a white, cloudy sediment
- Texture feels grainy or chalky between the fingers even before melting
- A simple acid test: a few drops of vinegar or lemon juice on a dissolved gur solution will produce fizzing bubbles if carbonate fillers are present

---

### 5. Khejur (Date Palm) Gur Substitution

**What it is:** Khejur (date palm) gur is a different product — lighter in color, thinner in flavor — sometimes passed off as sugarcane gur, especially in winter when khejur gur is abundant.

**How to spot it:**
- Khejur gur has a distinctly floral, almost fermented sweetness — noticeably different from the earthy molasses of sugarcane gur
- It is usually lighter in color (golden to light brown)
- It melts faster and is often softer at room temperature than sugarcane cube gur

> Khejur gur is itself a premium, authentic product — but mislabeling it as sugarcane gur is fraudulent. Always confirm which type you are purchasing.

</section>

## Quick Authenticity Checklist
### দ্রুত যাচাইয়ের তালিকা

Use this checklist when your product arrives:

- [ ] Color is deep amber to dark brown — not bright yellow or white
- [ ] Surface is slightly rough, not glossy or wet
- [ ] Smells of warm caramel and molasses, not neutral or artificially sweet
- [ ] Dissolves in hot water to produce a rich amber, aromatic liquid
- [ ] Dissolves fully with minimal white or chalky sediment
- [ ] No artificial color transfer on dry fingers
- [ ] Taste has layered sweetness — not flat or one-dimensional
- [ ] No gritty chalk-like residue after melting in the mouth
