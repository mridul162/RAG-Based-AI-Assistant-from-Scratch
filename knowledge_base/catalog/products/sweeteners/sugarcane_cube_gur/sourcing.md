# Sourcing
## Geographic Origin (ভৌগোলিক উৎস)

Sugarcane Cube Gur (আখের কিউব গুড়) is sourced from major sugarcane-growing regions of Bangladesh, particularly:

* Rajshahi
* Faridpur
* Kushtia

These regions have a long tradition of sugarcane cultivation due to their fertile alluvial soils, favorable climate, and established gur-making practices. The combination of suitable growing conditions and generations of local expertise contributes to the quality and flavor profile of traditional sugarcane jaggery.

Sugarcane grown in these areas is typically processed close to the cultivation sites, helping preserve freshness and reduce the time between harvest and production.

## Harvest Method (সংগ্রহ ও উৎপাদন পদ্ধতি)

Sugarcane Cube Gur is produced from freshly harvested mature sugarcane.

### Step 1: Sugarcane Harvesting

* Sugarcane is harvested when it reaches optimal maturity and sugar content.
* Farmers typically cut the cane manually using traditional harvesting methods.
* Harvested cane is transported promptly for juice extraction.

### Step 2: Juice Extraction

* Fresh sugarcane stalks are crushed to extract juice.
* The juice is filtered to remove plant fibers and visible impurities before boiling.

### Step 3: Concentration by Boiling

* The extracted juice is heated in large pans.
* Water gradually evaporates, concentrating the natural sugars.
* Producers continuously stir and skim the surface to maintain quality.

### Step 4: Molding into Cubes

* Once the syrup reaches the desired thickness, it is poured into molds or shaped into blocks.
* The gur is allowed to cool and solidify naturally.
* Finished cubes are then prepared for packaging and distribution.

This traditional process creates a minimally processed sweetener that retains the characteristic flavor, color, and aroma of sugarcane.

## Season & Timing (মৌসুম ও সময়কাল)

Sugarcane Cube Gur is a seasonal agricultural product because it depends on the annual sugarcane harvest.

### Typical Production Season

* Sugarcane harvesting generally peaks during the cooler months.
* Fresh gur production is most active during late autumn and winter.
* Availability is typically highest from approximately November through March, although exact timing varies by region and weather conditions.

### Seasonal Characteristics

Freshly produced seasonal gur is often valued for:

* Richer aroma
* Softer texture in some batches
* Distinct sugarcane flavor
* Traditional winter culinary use

Because production follows agricultural cycles, supply and pricing may fluctuate throughout the year.

## Supply Chain (সরবরাহ শৃঙ্খল)

### Farm to Consumer Journey

1. Sugarcane cultivation by local farmers.
2. Harvesting of mature sugarcane.
3. Local extraction of fresh sugarcane juice.
4. Traditional boiling and concentration.
5. Molding into cube or block form.
6. Quality inspection and sorting.
7. Packaging for retail distribution.
8. Delivery to consumers.

### Quality Preservation

To help maintain product quality:

* Sugarcane is processed soon after harvest whenever possible.
* Finished gur is stored in dry conditions to minimize moisture absorption.
* Packaging is designed to protect the product during transportation and storage.

### Sustainability & Ethical Considerations

Sugarcane Cube Gur is commonly produced through small-scale and community-based agricultural systems that support rural livelihoods in sugarcane-growing regions. Traditional gur production generally involves:

* Locally sourced agricultural raw materials
* Shorter farm-to-processing distances
* Support for regional farming communities
* Preservation of traditional food-making knowledge

Specific sustainability practices may vary by producer, harvest season, and sourcing location.
