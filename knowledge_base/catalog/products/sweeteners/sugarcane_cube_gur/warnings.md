# Warnings
## Allergen Information / অ্যালার্জেন সংক্রান্ত তথ্য

Pure sugarcane cube gur is inherently free from major common allergens like gluten, dairy, nuts, soy, and eggs. However, customers should be aware of the following cross-contamination and processing notes:

* **Cross-Contamination Risk:** This jaggery is produced using traditional, semi-automated artisanal methods in the regional open-air facilities of Rajshahi, Faridpur, and Kushtia. While the raw ingredient is purely sugarcane juice, it may be processed in environments that handle or package wheat, mustard seeds, or tree nuts. Highly sensitive individuals should proceed with caution.
* **Natural Sulfites:** Minimal naturally occurring sulfites may be present as a byproduct of the boiling and concentration process. No chemical sulfites are added as preservatives or bleaching agents.

---

## Who Should Avoid / কাদের জন্য এটি নিষেধ বা পরিহার্য

While healthier than refined sugar, sugarcane gur is not universally safe for everyone. The following groups must avoid or strictly limit intake:

* **Individuals with Diabetes:** Jaggery contains high concentrations of sucrose and fructose. It triggers rapid spikes in blood glucose levels and must not be used as a "diabetic-safe" alternative to white sugar without explicit medical supervision.
* **Infants Under 1 Year Old:** Although unrefined sugarcane jaggery does not carry the exact same *Clostridium botulinum* risk as raw honey, its traditional open-air processing means it is non-sterile. It should not be introduced to infants under 12 months due to their developing digestive and immune systems.
* **Individuals on Strict Low-Carb/Keto Diets:** Because it consists almost entirely of carbohydrates, it will instantly disrupt ketosis.

## Drug/Condition Interactions / ওষুধ ও শারীরিক অবস্থার সাথে প্রতিক্রিয়া

* **Anti-Hyperglycemic Medications (Insulin, Metformin, Glipizide):** Consuming sugarcane gur while taking blood sugar-lowering medications can severely counteract the efficacy of the drugs, leading to unexpected hyperglycemia (high blood sugar).
* **Chronic Inflammatory Conditions:** Sugarcane jaggery is unrefined but possesses a high glycemic load. Excessive intake can exacerbate systemic inflammation, affecting individuals dealing with chronic inflammatory disorders like rheumatoid arthritis or severe gut dysbiosis.
* **Acute Gastrointestinal Distress:** Traditional gur retains natural plant fibers and mineral salts. If consumed during active bouts of diarrhea or food poisoning, it can worsen abdominal cramping or loose stools due to its mild natural laxative properties.

## Safe Usage Limits / নিরাপদ ব্যবহারের মাত্রা

To maintain a balanced diet, consumers should treat sugarcane cube gur as a caloric sweetener rather than a health supplement.

| Demographic | Recommended Maximum Daily Intake | Operational Guidance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Healthy Adults** | **25g - 30g** (Approx. 2 to 3 small cubes) | Should fall within the WHO recommended limit for added sugars (less than 10% of total daily energy intake). |
| **Children (Ages 2-18)** | **15g - 20g** (Approx. 1.5 to 2 small cubes) | High intake can contribute to dental caries (cavities) and early metabolic imbalances. |
| **Individuals with Controlled Diabetes** | **0g** (Strict Avoidance preferred) | Seek direct advice from a registered dietitian or endocrinologist before any consumption. |
