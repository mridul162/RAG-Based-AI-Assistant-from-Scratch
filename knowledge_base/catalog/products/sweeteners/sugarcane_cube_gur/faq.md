### 1. What exactly is sugarcane cube gur? Is it the same as regular jaggery?
**আখের কিউব গুড় কী? এটা কি সাধারণ জ্যাগারি বা গুড়ের মতোই?**

Sugarcane cube gur (আখের কিউব গুড়) is unrefined jaggery made by boiling fresh sugarcane juice until it thickens, then molding it into cube or disc shapes. It is the same product category as jaggery, but the cube/block form (also called পাটালি গুড়) is more convenient to portion, store, and use compared to loose or semi-liquid gur. It is not the same as refined sugar — no chemicals, bleaching agents, or preservatives are used in authentic production.

### 5. How do I know the gur I receive is genuine and not adulterated?
**কীভাবে বুঝব যে গুড় আসল, ভেজাল নয়?**

Genuine sugarcane cube gur has a few reliable markers:

- **Color:** Deep amber to dark brown — not bright yellow or white
- **Aroma:** Warm, smoky caramel-molasses smell — not neutral or artificially sweet
- **Taste:** Layered sweetness with a natural molasses depth — not flat like refined sugar
- **Dissolution:** Melts in hot water to produce a rich, amber-colored liquid with minimal white sediment

For a full breakdown of visual, sensory, and lab-based authenticity checks — and a guide to common adulteration methods — refer to the **[authenticity.md](./authenticity.md)** document for this product.

### 6. Is sugarcane cube gur healthier than white sugar?
**আখের গুড় কি সাদা চিনির চেয়ে স্বাস্থ্যকর?**

Unrefined sugarcane gur retains natural minerals and micronutrients that are stripped out during the refining of white sugar — including **iron, calcium, potassium, and magnesium**, along with trace antioxidants. It also has a slightly lower glycemic impact than white sugar, though it is still high in natural sugars and calories.

**Important:** Gur is not a sugar-free or diabetic-friendly product. It should be consumed in moderation like any sweetener. It is not a medicine or supplement, and we do not make medical claims about this product. If you have a specific health condition, consult a doctor before making dietary changes.

### 7. Can people with diabetes eat sugarcane gur?
**ডায়াবেটিস রোগীরা কি আখের গুড় খেতে পারবেন?**

Sugarcane gur is still primarily sugar (sucrose) and will raise blood glucose levels. While it is less refined than white sugar and contains trace minerals, it is **not recommended as a safe sugar substitute for people with diabetes** without medical guidance. Please consult your physician or dietitian before including gur in a diabetic diet.

### 9. How should I store sugarcane cube gur at home?
**বাড়িতে গুড় কীভাবে সংরক্ষণ করব?**

Proper storage will preserve flavor and prevent spoilage:

- Store in an **airtight container** — exposure to air causes moisture absorption and surface mold
- Keep in a **cool, dry place** away from direct sunlight
- **Do not refrigerate** unless absolutely necessary — condensation inside the container can speed up deterioration
- If storing long-term (3+ months), wrapping individual cubes in parchment or food-grade wax paper before sealing helps preserve texture
- A properly stored cube gur can last **6 to 12 months** without significant quality loss

If you notice white surface bloom (a thin crystalline layer), that is usually natural sugar crystallization — not mold. Mold will appear as fuzzy, green, or black patches and has a distinct off-smell.

### 10. What is the shelf life of the product?
**পণ্যের মেয়াদ কতদিন?**

Under proper storage conditions (airtight, cool, dry), sugarcane cube gur maintains good quality for **up to 12 months**. The dense, low-moisture cube form has better shelf stability than semi-liquid or loose gur. However, flavor and aroma are best within the first 6 months of production. Always check for signs of mold or off-smell before use, regardless of the purchase date.

### 11. My gur arrived with some white powder or crystals on the surface. Is it spoiled?
**গুড়ের উপর সাদা গুঁড়া বা দানা দেখা যাচ্ছে — কি নষ্ট হয়ে গেছে?**

Not necessarily. A white crystalline coating on the surface of cube gur is usually **natural sugar crystallization** (efflorescence) — a harmless process that occurs when natural sugars in the gur migrate to the surface, especially with temperature fluctuations during transit or storage. The gur is still safe and flavorful to use.

To distinguish crystallization from mold:

| Sign | Crystallization | Mold |
|---|---|---|
| Appearance | Thin, dry, powdery white film | Fuzzy, green, black, or blue patches |
| Smell | Normal gur aroma | Musty, sour, or off-smell |
| Texture | Dry and granular | Soft, damp, or slimy |

If you see fuzzy patches or smell anything off, do not consume the product and contact us with your order details.

### 15. Can I use this gur as a 1:1 substitute for white sugar in recipes?
**রেসিপিতে সাদা চিনির বদলে সমান পরিমাণে গুড় ব্যবহার করা যাবে?**

You can substitute gur for white sugar, but a straight 1:1 swap may need slight adjustment because:

- Gur has a more intense, complex sweetness — you may want to start with **slightly less** (about 80–90% of the white sugar quantity) and adjust to taste
- Gur adds color to your dish — expect your payesh, tea, or baked goods to turn a warm amber-brown
- Gur contains trace moisture, which can affect texture in baking — especially in cookies or pastry dough
- The molasses flavor will be noticeable — this is desirable in traditional Bengali dishes but may be unexpected in neutral-flavored recipes

As a general starting point: **use 85g of gur for every 100g of white sugar**, then adjust based on the dish and your preference.
