# Usage
## Best uses

Pabnar Deshi Cow Ghee A2 is a premium traditional ghee ideal for everyday cooking and special preparations in Bangladeshi and South Asian cuisine. Its high smoke point (around 250°C/482°F) makes it excellent for frying, sautéing, and tempering. The rich, nutty aroma and granular texture enhance both savory and sweet dishes.

**Primary applications:**
- **Daily cooking:** Use as a primary cooking fat for curries, vegetables, and rice dishes instead of oil for superior flavor and aroma.
- **Tadka/tempering:** Finish dals, sabzis, and khichdi with a spoonful for authentic taste.
- **Baking & frying:** Suitable for deep frying snacks (singara, jilapi) and traditional sweets.
- **Traditional & medicinal:** Consumed warm with milk or honey in small amounts for digestive health and as per Ayurvedic practices (consult a practitioner for therapeutic use).
- **Finishing:** Drizzle over hot rice, roti, or paratha for enhanced taste and mouthfeel.

## Dosage / serving suggestions

- **General daily use:** 1–2 teaspoons (5–10g) per person per meal as cooking fat or topping.
- **For children:** ½–1 teaspoon mixed in warm milk or food.
- **Therapeutic/traditional:** 1 teaspoon with warm milk or honey in the morning (common in deshi ghee traditions).
- **Cooking portions:**
  - For 4 servings of curry or dal: 2–3 tablespoons.
  - For frying: Enough to submerge food items (recycle strained ghee after cooling).
  - Per 250g rice: 2–3 tablespoons for ghee rice.

Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Once opened, use within 3–6 months for best aroma and freshness. The product solidifies in cooler temperatures and melts easily when warmed — this is normal for pure deshi ghee.

## Recipe ideas

**Simple Ghee Rice (Pabnar Gawa Bhat):**
1. Cook 1 cup basmati rice.
2. Heat 2 tbsp Pabnar Deshi Cow Ghee.
3. Add cumin seeds, bay leaf, and whole spices.
4. Mix with cooked rice and garnish with fried onions.

**Classic Dal Tadka:**
- Prepare yellow dal as usual.
- Heat 1–2 tbsp ghee, add garlic, dry red chilies, cumin, and asafoetida.
- Pour over dal and serve with rice or roti.

**Traditional Bengali Sweets:**
- Use in semai payesh, halwa, or sandesh for rich flavor.
- Prepare ghee-soaked rosogolla syrup or laddoos.

**Everyday pairings:**
- Mix with warm milk and a pinch of turmeric or black pepper for a soothing drink.
- Spread lightly on paratha, luchi, or ruti.
- Use in khichuri or bhuna khichuri for festive meals.

## What to avoid

- **High-volume deep frying repeatedly:** Strain after use to remove residue and avoid bitterness.
- **Overheating beyond smoke point:** Though stable, avoid prolonged extreme heat to preserve nutrients.
- **Storage issues:** Do not refrigerate (it hardens excessively); keep in airtight glass or original container.
- **Allergies:** Avoid if you have dairy or milk protein sensitivity (though A2 ghee is often better tolerated).
- **Mixing with incompatible items:** Do not combine with very cold foods immediately after cooking.
- **Excessive consumption:** Like any fat, use in moderation. Those with specific health conditions (e.g., high cholesterol, digestive disorders) should consult a doctor.

This ghee is 100% pure and free from additives. Its quality is best experienced when used fresh and in traditional recipes.
