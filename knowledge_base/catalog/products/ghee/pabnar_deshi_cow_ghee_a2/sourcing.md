# Sourcing
## Geographic origin

<section>

**Pabnar Deshi Cow Ghee A2 (পাবনার দেশি গরুর ঘি A2)** originates from the Pabna region of Bangladesh, an area widely recognized for its long-established dairy farming traditions and high-quality milk production.

The milk used for this ghee is sourced from indigenous deshi cows raised in rural farming communities, including areas influenced by the fertile Padma River floodplains and char lands. These agricultural environments support natural grazing and mixed-feed farming systems that have historically contributed to the region’s reputation for premium dairy products.

</section>

## Harvest method

<section>

Unlike agricultural crops, ghee is a dairy product produced through a multi-stage milk processing method rather than harvesting.

### Step 1: Milk collection

* Fresh milk is collected from indigenous cows raised by local dairy farmers.
* Milk is typically gathered daily to maintain freshness and quality.
* Only milk meeting quality standards is selected for ghee production.

### Step 2: Butter preparation

* The milk is processed to obtain cream and butter using traditional dairy methods.
* Traditional producers may use cultured dairy processes that contribute to the characteristic aroma and flavor of gawa ghee.

### Step 3: Slow clarification

* Butter is heated slowly over controlled temperatures.
* Water naturally evaporates during cooking.
* Milk solids separate and gradually develop a rich aroma and golden color.
* The clarified fat is filtered to produce pure ghee.

### Step 4: Cooling and packaging

* The finished ghee is cooled under hygienic conditions.
* It is then packaged into retail containers for distribution and sale.

This slow-cooking process is a defining feature of traditional Pabnar gawa ghee and contributes to its distinctive texture, flavor, and aroma.

</section>

## Season & timing

<section>

Pabnar Deshi Cow Ghee A2 is **not a seasonal product** and is generally available throughout the year.

### Milk availability

* Dairy cows produce milk across all seasons, enabling continuous production.
* Output levels may vary depending on weather conditions, feed availability, and farm management practices.
* Seasonal fluctuations in milk supply can influence production volume and market pricing.

### Production cycle

* Milk collection occurs daily.
* Ghee production is carried out regularly based on milk availability and market demand.
* Properly prepared ghee has a relatively long storage life compared with fresh dairy products, allowing year-round availability.

</section>

## Supply chain

<section>

### Farm to consumer process

1. **Local dairy farms**

   * Indigenous cows are raised by rural farmers in the Pabna region.

2. **Milk collection**

   * Fresh milk is collected and transported for processing.

3. **Traditional ghee production**

   * Milk is converted into butter and clarified through slow cooking.

4. **Quality inspection**

   * The finished ghee is filtered and checked for appearance, aroma, and consistency.

5. **Packaging**

   * Ghee is packed into consumer-sized containers.

6. **Distribution**

   * Products are supplied to retailers, distributors, and direct customers.

7. **Consumer use**

   * End users purchase the product for cooking, baking, traditional dishes, and household consumption.

### Sustainability and ethical considerations

* Production supports local dairy farming communities in the Pabna region.
* Reliance on locally sourced milk helps strengthen regional agricultural livelihoods.
* Traditional small-scale dairy systems generally promote shorter supply chains between producers and consumers.
* Sustainable outcomes depend on responsible animal care, proper feeding practices, hygienic milk handling, and efficient resource management throughout the production process.

### Traceability focus

The product's identity is closely linked to:

* Pabna-origin milk sourcing.
* Indigenous deshi cow milk.
* Traditional gawa ghee production methods.
* Regional dairy heritage and craftsmanship.

</section>
