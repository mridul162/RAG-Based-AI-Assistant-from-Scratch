## Price Drivers

The pricing of Pabnar Deshi Cow Ghee A2 is primarily determined by four distinct supply chain and production factors:

### 1. High Input-to-Output Ratio

Producing pure deshi cow ghee requires a significant volume of raw milk. On average, it takes approximately **22 to 25 liters of pure deshi cow milk** to produce just 1 kilogram of premium ghee. The cost of raw milk directly dictates the baseline production cost.

### 2. Sourcing from Indigenous A2 Pabna Cows

Unlike commercial dairy farms that utilize high-yielding crossbred or foreign cows, this product is sourced strictly from indigenous Pabna breed cows grazing in the Padma river char areas. These cows yield substantially less milk per day, creating a highly constrained supply of authentic A2 beta-casein milk.

### 3. Traditional Slow-Cooking Process

Rather than utilizing industrial centrifugal separation and rapid heating, this ghee is manufactured using the authentic, labor-intensive slow-cooking method. The added time, manual monitoring, and traditional firewood or controlled heating fuel consumption add a processing premium to each batch.

### 4. Geographic Logistical Constraints

Milk collection from remote Padma river char (river island) areas requires dedicated cold-chain or rapid transit logistics to ensure freshness before processing. This specialized regional sourcing pipeline increases overhead compared to centralized city-adjacent dairy farms.

## Value Justification

While the upfront cost is higher than mass-produced commercial alternatives, the product provides measurable long-term value to health-conscious consumers:

* **Zero Adulteration Risk:** Commercial variants are frequently blended with vegetable oils (Vanaspati) or buffalo fat to lower costs. This product guarantees 100% pure milk fat, ensuring customers get exactly what they pay for.
* **Digestibility and Health Savings:** The exclusive presence of A2 beta-casein means consumers with A1 dairy sensitivities do not experience gastrointestinal discomfort, saving costs on specialized allergen-free alternatives or digestive supplements.
* **Concentrated Yield:** Due to the complete extraction of moisture and milk solids through traditional slow-cooking, a smaller quantity of this granular ghee is required to achieve the desired aroma and flavor in culinary applications compared to moisture-heavy commercial brands.

## Cost vs. Alternatives

### Commercial Ghee / Vegetable Fats (Vanaspati)

* **Price Difference:** Commercial alternatives can be 30% to 50% cheaper.
* **The Trade-off:** Cheaper alternatives introduce trans-fats, hydrogenated oils, or hydrogenated vegetable fats which degrade cardiovascular health. They lack the distinct natural granular texture and rich aroma inherent to authentic Pabna ghee.

### Standard Mixed-Milk Ghee

* **Price Difference:** Standard cow/buffalo mixed ghee costs roughly 15% to 20% less.
* **The Trade-off:** Standard options contain A1 beta-casein proteins, which can trigger bloating, inflammation, and discomfort in dairy-sensitive individuals. They also lack the verified regional pedigree of the Padma char-pastured cattle.
