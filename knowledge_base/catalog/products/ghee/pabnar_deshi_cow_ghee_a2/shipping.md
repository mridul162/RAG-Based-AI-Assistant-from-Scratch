# Shipping
## Temperature requirements

Pabnar Deshi Cow Ghee A2 is relatively stable due to its low moisture content and high smoke point but remains sensitive to high temperatures.

- **Ideal shipping temperature:** Below 30°C (86°F). Avoid exposure to direct sunlight or temperatures above 35°C for extended periods.
- **Summer/High-heat periods:** Use insulated packaging or expedited shipping to prevent melting, separation, or aroma degradation. The product will naturally solidify again upon cooling, but repeated melting can affect texture.
- **Storage after delivery:** Keep in a cool, dry place away from heat sources. Refrigeration is not required but can be used in very hot climates.

No seasonal restrictions apply as the product is available year-round.

## Packaging notes

- **Standard packaging:** Supplied in food-grade glass jars or metal tins (depending on variant size) with secure lids.
- **Shipping preparation:** Each jar/tin is individually bubble-wrapped or foam-protected. Multiple units are placed in sturdy outer cartons with sufficient cushioning to prevent breakage.
- **Fragility:** Glass jars are moderately fragile — handle with care during transit. Avoid dropping or stacking heavy items on top.
- **Leak-proofing:** Containers are sealed to minimize leakage even if partial melting occurs.

## Regulatory considerations

- **Local (Bangladesh) shipping:** No special restrictions. Standard courier and postal services are suitable.
- **Food safety:** Product complies with local food standards. Proper labeling with ingredients, manufacturing date, and expiry is included.
- **Customs declarations:** Must be declared as "edible fat / ghee" with accurate weight and value.

## International shipping

International shipping is possible but requires attention to destination country rules:

- **Documentation:** Commercial invoice, packing list, and certificate of origin may be required. Some countries need phytosanitary or health certificates for dairy-derived products.
- **Import restrictions:** Check destination regulations — many countries allow clarified butter/ghee but may impose duties, VAT, or quantity limits on personal vs commercial imports.
- **Transit time recommendation:** Use express air freight for longer distances to minimize heat exposure. Sea freight is not recommended unless temperature-controlled containers are used.
- **Potential issues:** Avoid shipping to countries with strict dairy import bans or during extreme weather seasons. Customers are responsible for any customs duties or delays.
- **Advice:** For best quality, prefer shorter shipping routes and monitor tracking closely.

For specific routes or large orders, contact support for tailored packing and carrier recommendations.
