# Usage

## Best Uses

Pabnar Pure Gawa Ghee can be used both as a cooking fat and as a finishing ingredient. Its rich aroma and concentrated flavor make it suitable for a wide range of traditional and modern recipes.

### Common Uses

* Mix with hot rice for a simple traditional meal.
* Add to khichuri, polao, biryani, and pulao for enhanced aroma and richness.
* Use for tempering (tadka) lentils and vegetable dishes.
* Spread lightly on paratha, roti, naan, or chapati.
* Incorporate into halwa, suji, payesh, firni, and other desserts.
* Use in baking recipes that call for butter or clarified butter.
* Drizzle over cooked dishes immediately before serving for maximum aroma.
* Use for sautéing spices, onions, and aromatics in traditional cooking.

### General Guidance

* Start with smaller quantities and adjust according to taste.
* Because the flavor is concentrated, a small amount is often sufficient.
* Use moderately as part of a balanced diet.

## Recipe Ideas

### Traditional Ghee Rice

**Ingredients**

* 1 bowl hot steamed rice
* 1–2 teaspoons Pabnar Pure Gawa Ghee
* Pinch of salt (optional)

**Method**

1. Add ghee to freshly cooked hot rice.
2. Mix thoroughly.
3. Serve immediately.

### Ghee Khichuri

**Ingredients**

* Cooked khichuri
* 1–2 tablespoons ghee

**Method**

1. Stir a portion of the ghee into the khichuri during the final cooking stage.
2. Add a small amount before serving for extra aroma.

### Ghee Tempered Dal

**Ingredients**

* Cooked lentils
* 1 tablespoon ghee
* Garlic, dried chili, cumin seeds

**Method**

1. Heat ghee gently.
2. Add spices and aromatics.
3. Pour the tempered mixture over cooked dal.

### Suji Halwa

**Ingredients**

* Semolina (suji)
* Ghee
* Sugar
* Water or milk

**Method**

1. Roast semolina in ghee until fragrant.
2. Add liquid and sugar.
3. Cook until thick and smooth.

### Paratha Finish

Apply a light layer of ghee to freshly cooked paratha for improved flavor, aroma, and texture.

## What to Avoid

1. **Avoid introducing moisture**

   Always use a clean, dry spoon. Water contamination can reduce shelf life and affect quality.

2. **Avoid prolonged exposure to heat and sunlight**

   Store in a cool, dry place with the lid tightly closed.

3. **Avoid overheating repeatedly**

   While ghee is suitable for high-heat cooking, repeated overheating may gradually affect flavor and aroma.

4. **Do not mix with lower-quality fats if authenticity is desired**

   Mixing with vegetable oils or other fats can dilute the traditional taste and characteristics of pure gawa ghee.

5. **Do not consume excessive amounts**

   Ghee is a concentrated source of fat and calories. Consumption should be balanced within overall dietary needs.

6. **Check suitability for dairy-sensitive individuals**

   Although most milk solids are removed during preparation, people with dairy allergies or specific medical dietary restrictions should consult an appropriate healthcare professional before consumption.
