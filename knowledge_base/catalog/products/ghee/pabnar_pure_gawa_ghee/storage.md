# Storage
## Ideal conditions

- **Temperature**: Store at cool room temperature (ideally 15–25°C / 59–77°F). Avoid direct heat sources, stoves, or areas with temperature fluctuations. Refrigeration is optional but recommended in hot/humid climates for extended freshness.
- **Humidity**: Keep in a low-humidity environment (<60% relative humidity). Moisture can lead to spoilage.
- **Light**: Store in a dark place or opaque container. Protect from direct sunlight to prevent oxidation and color degradation.
- **Ventilation**: Avoid exposure to strong odors (spices, onions, etc.) as ghee can absorb smells.

## Container recommendations

- Use the original airtight glass or metal container provided.
- If transferring, use clean, dry glass jars with tight-fitting lids (preferred over plastic for long-term storage).
- Ensure the container is completely dry before use—any moisture will reduce shelf life.
- Do not use reactive metal containers for long periods.

## Shelf life

- **Unopened**: 12–24 months from production date when stored properly. Traditional pure gawa ghee can last longer due to its low moisture content.
- **Opened**: 6–12 months at cool room temperature. Up to 18–24 months when refrigerated.
- Shelf life may vary with seasonal milk quality and exact production conditions. Always check for freshness before use.

## Signs of spoilage

- **Smell**: Rancid, sour, or off-putting odor (fresh ghee has a sweet, nutty, caramel-like aroma).
- **Appearance**: Discoloration (darkening or dulling of the golden color), grainy texture changes, or visible mold.
- **Taste**: Bitter or soapy flavor (test a small amount if smell seems suspicious).
- **Texture**: Excessive separation of liquids or unusual cloudiness (some natural granulation is normal).

**Note**: Pure deshi gawa ghee is highly stable due to the traditional Bilona method and absence of additives. When in doubt, discard. Store away from children and follow local food safety guidelines.
