# Aliases
## Common Names

These are the standard names used across general retail channels, product descriptions, and everyday conversation in both English and Bengali.

* **Pabnar Pure Gawa Ghee** – The primary English marketing and official label name.
* **পাবনার খাঁটি গাওয়া ঘি** – The primary Bengali name, explicitly denoting authenticity (*khati*) and regional origin (*pabnar*).
* **Gawa Ghee / গাওয়া ঘি** – The baseline product category identifier used in Bangladesh to differentiate cow's milk ghee from buffalo ghee or vegetable fats.
* **Pure Gawa Ghee / খাঁটি গাওয়া ঘি** – Used by consumers seeking unadulterated cow milk fat.
* **Khati Gheee / খাঁটি ঘি** – A generic but common shorthand expression for pure clarified butter.

## Regional Names

These names explicitly tie the product to its cultural, geographical, and linguistic origins within Bangladesh, reflecting regional variations or colloquial Banglish phonetics.

### Bengali (Bangla) Script

* **দেশি গাওয়া ঘি** – Denotes the use of indigenous, domestic cow milk (*deshi*).
* **পাবনার গাওয়া ঘি** – Emphasizes the product's origin from the historic dairy tracts of Pabna.
* **গরুর গাওয়া ঘি** – Explicitly specifies cow (*goru*) milk source to avoid confusion with buffalo milk variants.
* **পাবনার ঘি** – A simplified regional designation commonly used in local markets.

### Banglish / Romanized Bengali

* **pabnar khati gawa ghee** – Accurate phonetic representation used by customers typing with a standard QWERTY keyboard.
* **pabnar deshi gawa ghee** – Combines the region and the indigenous nature of the raw material.
* **gorur gawa ghee** – Transliteration of "cow's milk gawa ghee".
* **deshi gawa ghee** – Transliteration of "indigenous gawa ghee".

## Scientific / Trade Names

These technical and regulatory classifications ensure proper indexing in international databases, logistics systems, and scientific RAG queries.

* **Clarified Cow Milk Fat** – The technical and standard international trade description for pure ghee derived from cattle.
* **Anhydrous Milkfat (AMF)** – The international regulatory and food science trade terminology for milk fat products containing less than 0.5% moisture.
* **HS Code 0405.90.00** – The international Harmonized System code for "Fats and oils derived from milk; ghee".
* **Bilona Ghee** – Industry trade term representing the traditional method of boiling raw milk, curdling it, churning the curd to butter (*makhon*), and slow-cooking the butter to extract clean ghee.
