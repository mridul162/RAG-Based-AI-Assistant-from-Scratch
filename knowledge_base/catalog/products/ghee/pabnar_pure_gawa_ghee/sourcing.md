## Geographic Origin

Pabnar Pure Gawa Ghee originates from the Pabna region of Bangladesh, one of the country's most recognized dairy-producing areas. Pabna has a long-standing reputation for cattle rearing, milk production, and traditional dairy processing, making it a natural source for authentic deshi ghee.

The region's dairy heritage, availability of fresh cow's milk, and established ghee-making practices contribute to the distinctive quality associated with Pabnar gawa ghee.

## Harvest Method

Unlike agricultural products that are harvested from fields, gawa ghee is produced through a dairy-processing method that begins with fresh cow's milk.

### Typical Production Process

1. Fresh cow's milk is collected from local dairy farms.
2. Milk fat is separated through traditional dairy processing methods.
3. Butter or concentrated milk fat is prepared from the collected cream.
4. The butter is slowly heated over controlled temperatures.
5. Moisture evaporates while milk solids gradually develop the characteristic flavor and aroma.
6. The clarified golden fat is filtered and cooled.
7. The finished ghee is packed into clean, food-grade containers.

The slow-cooking process is a defining characteristic of traditional gawa ghee production and contributes to its aroma, texture, and golden appearance.

## Supply Chain

<section>

### Farm-to-Consumer Journey

**Step 1: Milk Collection**

* Fresh cow's milk is sourced from dairy-producing areas within and around Pabna.
* Milk is collected and prepared for processing.

**Step 2: Fat Extraction**

* Cream or milk fat is separated using traditional dairy practices.

**Step 3: Ghee Preparation**

* The milk fat is slowly cooked until clarified ghee is formed.
* The process develops the product's characteristic aroma, flavor, and color.

**Step 4: Quality Inspection**

* The finished ghee is visually checked for consistency, texture, aroma, and purity.
* Impurities and excess milk solids are removed through filtration.

**Step 5: Packaging**

* Ghee is packed into sealed containers to help preserve freshness and quality.

**Step 6: Distribution**

* Products are transported to retailers, distributors, and end customers.

## Sustainability & Ethical Sourcing Notes

* Sourced from a region with an established dairy farming tradition.
* Supports local dairy farmers and rural milk-producing communities.
* Traditional production methods generally rely on simple processing techniques rather than extensive industrial refinement.
* Packaging and transportation practices may vary by producer and distribution channel.
* Product quality is closely linked to responsible milk sourcing, proper animal care, and hygienic dairy-handling practices.

Because dairy sourcing practices can differ between individual farms and production batches, specific animal welfare, environmental, or sustainability certifications should only be claimed when independently verified.
