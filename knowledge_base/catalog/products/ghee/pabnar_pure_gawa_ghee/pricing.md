# Pricing
## Price Drivers

The pricing of Pabnar Pure Gawa Ghee is fundamentally driven by its traditional manufacturing process, localized sourcing, and strict quality control. Unlike industrial alternatives, several critical factors influence its cost structure:

* **Raw Milk Sourcing (Indigenous Cows):** The ghee is prepared exclusively from the milk of indigenous (*deshi*) Pabna cow breeds. These free-grazing cows yield significantly lower volumes of milk compared to crossbred or commercial dairy cattle, increasing the base cost of the raw material.
* **The Traditional Bilona Method:** Producing ghee through the authentic slow-cooking *Bilona* or *Gawa* method is highly labor-intensive and time-consuming. It requires curdling the milk, churning it manually or semi-mechanically to extract butter (*makhon*), and then clarifying it over a controlled, low-heat flame.
* **High Raw-to-Finished Yield Ratio:** It takes approximately 25 to 30 liters of pure milk to produce just 1 kilogram of authentic Gawa ghee. Consequently, any fluctuations in local raw milk prices directly impact the final product cost.
* **Purity Assurance Costs:** The product maintains a strict zero-adulteration policy. Costs are allocated toward eliminating vegetable fats (*dalda*), artificial colors, chemical preservatives, or synthetic aromatic agents.

## Value Justification

While the upfront cost is higher than mass-market options, the value proposition of Pabnar Pure Gawa Ghee rests on its uncompromised quality, health benefits, and authenticity:

* **Regional Rarity:** The Pabna region is historically renowned across Bangladesh for producing high-quality dairy due to its fertile riverine ecosystems (*bathan* grazing lands). This geographical indicator guarantees a distinct granular texture and natural aroma that cannot be replicated synthetically.
* **Nutritional Integrity:** Because it is free from hydrogenated oils and artificial additives, it retains its naturally occurring short- and medium-chain fatty acids, butyric acid, and fat-soluble vitamins (A, E, and K2).
* **Extended Shelf Life:** The precision of the traditional slow-cooking extraction process removes almost all moisture content, giving the ghee a naturally long shelf life without requiring chemical preservatives or refrigeration.
