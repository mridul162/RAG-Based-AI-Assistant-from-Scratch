# Shipping
## Temperature Requirements / তাপমাত্রা সংক্রান্ত প্রয়োজনীয়তা

* **Standard Transit:** Chia seeds are highly resilient and do not require cold-chain logistics or refrigerated transit during standard domestic shipping across Bangladesh.
* **Heat Vulnerability:** Avoid prolonged exposure to extreme heat (temperatures exceeding 40°C), such as leaving consignments in unventilated cargo areas or under direct sunlight for multiple days. Excessive heat can accelerate the oxidation of the seeds' natural Omega-3 fatty oils, risking premature rancidity.
* **Seasonal Awareness:** During the high-humidity monsoon season in Bangladesh, transit vehicles and distribution hubs must maintain dry cargo environments to prevent moisture accumulation.

---

## Packaging Notes / প্যাকেজিং নির্দেশনাবলী

* **Moisture and Spill Barrier:** Because chia seeds are exceptionally small and flow easily, they must be packed in thick, puncture-resistant plastic pouches or multi-layered laminate bags featuring an airtight ziplock or a heat-sealed top closure.
* **Secondary Containment:** For shipping delivery parcels, the primary product pouch must be placed inside a secondary shipping envelope or box. If a pouch punctures during transit, tiny chia seeds will leak rapidly, creating structural clean-up issues and product loss.
* **Fragility:** The product itself is non-fragile, but the integrity of the plastic packaging is highly vulnerable to sharp tools or crushing forces. Parcels should not be stacked beneath excessively heavy or sharp industrial goods.

---

## Regulatory Considerations / নিয়ন্ত্রক বা আইনি বিষয়াবলী

* **Domestic Transport:** No special domestic transit permits or certificates are required to move this product across different districts or divisions within Bangladesh.
* **Banned Substances:** Chia seeds are a pure agricultural food product. They are completely safe and contain no restricted or illegal chemical compounds under Bangladesh food safety regulations.

---

## International Shipping / আন্তর্জাতিক শিপিং নিয়মাবলী

* **Phytosanitary Certificates:** As raw, unprocessed agricultural seeds, international exports or imports generally require a Phytosanitary Certificate issued by the Ministry of Agriculture to verify that the seeds are free from regulated pests, soil contaminants, and plant diseases.
* **Customs Inspections:** Many destination countries apply strict biosecurity and quarantine protocols for raw seeds (e.g., Australia, the US, or EU nations) to ensure they cannot germinate or introduce alien species into the local ecosystem.
* **Customs Declaration:** Shipments must be clearly declared on international commercial invoices using the correct Harmonized System (HS) Code for chia seeds to ensure accurate duty calculations and clear clearance protocols:
* *HS Code:* `1207.99` (Other oil seeds and oleaginous fruits, whether or not broken).
