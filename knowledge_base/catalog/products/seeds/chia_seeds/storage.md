# Storage
## Ideal Conditions / আদর্শ সংরক্ষণের পরিবেশ

To maintain the high oil content (especially Omega-3 fatty acids) and prevent rancidity, store chia seeds under the following conditions:

* **Temperature:** Ambient room temperature or cooler (around 15°C to 25°C). Avoid storing near heat sources like stoves, ovens, or direct sunlight.
* **Humidity:** Low humidity is critical. High humidity triggers moisture absorption, which causes the seeds to clump and promotes mold growth. This is especially vital during the humid monsoon season in Bangladesh.
* **Light:** Keep in a dark place, such as a pantry or kitchen cupboard, as prolonged exposure to light can accelerate the oxidation of healthy fats.

---

## Container Recommendations / পাত্র ব্যবহারের নির্দেশনাবলী

* **Airtight Seal:** Always use an airtight container (glass jar, heavy-duty plastic container, or a securely sealed zip-lock bag) to lock out ambient moisture and pests.
* **Dry Utensils:** Never introduce wet spoons or damp hands into the container. Even a tiny amount of water can cause the seeds to form a gel locally inside the container, quickly leading to spoilage.
* **Long-Term Storage Option:** For bulk storage (e.g., keeping a 1kg pack over several months), storing the sealed airtight container inside the refrigerator or freezer will significantly extend freshness.

---

## Shelf Life / সংরক্ষণকাল

| Storage Location | Unopened Pack | Opened Pack |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Pantry / Kitchen Cupboard** | Up to 2 years from packaging | Up to 1 year (with proper airtight sealing) |
| **Refrigerator** | Up to 3 years | Up to 2 years |
| **Freezer** | 4+ years | Up to 3 years |

---

## Signs of Spoilage / নষ্ট হয়ে যাওয়ার লক্ষণসমূহ

Chia seeds naturally contain high levels of antioxidants, making them highly stable compared to other seeds. However, they can still degrade. Inspect for the following signs before use:

* **Rancid Odor:** A strong, sharp, sour, or paint-like smell indicates that the delicate Omega-3 fatty acids have oxidized. Good chia seeds should have a very mild, neutral, slightly nutty aroma.
* **Clumping or Webbing:** If seeds are stuck together in hard clumps or exhibit fine, web-like structures inside the container, moisture has penetrated, and mold or pests may be developing.
* **Bitter Taste:** If the seeds taste distinctly bitter or chemical-like instead of neutral, they are rancid and should be discarded.
* **Visible Mold:** Any fuzzy growth or discoloration indicates moisture damage. Throw away the entire batch immediately.
