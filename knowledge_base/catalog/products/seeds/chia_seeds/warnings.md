# Warnings
## Allergen Information (অ্যালার্জি সংক্রান্ত তথ্য)

<section>

### Seed Allergies

* Chia seeds are generally well tolerated by most people.
* However, individuals with known allergies to chia seeds or related seeds should avoid consumption.
* Rare allergic reactions may include:

  * Skin rash or itching
  * Swelling of the lips, tongue, or throat
  * Breathing difficulties
  * Gastrointestinal discomfort

### Cross-Reactivity

* Individuals with sensitivities to seeds such as sesame, mustard, or other edible seeds may wish to exercise caution when trying chia seeds for the first time.
* Cross-reactivity is uncommon but possible in sensitive individuals.

### First-Time Use

* If consuming chia seeds for the first time, start with a small amount and monitor for any adverse reactions.

</section>

## Who Should Avoid or Use With Caution (কারা সতর্কতার সাথে ব্যবহার করবেন)

<section>

### People with Swallowing Difficulties

* Chia seeds absorb significant amounts of liquid and expand into a gel-like texture.
* Consuming dry chia seeds immediately followed by liquid may increase the risk of swallowing difficulties in susceptible individuals.
* People with a history of esophageal narrowing, swallowing disorders, or choking risk should consume chia seeds only when properly soaked.

### Individuals with Digestive Sensitivities

* Chia seeds are high in dietary fiber.
* Sudden consumption of large amounts may cause:

  * Bloating
  * Gas
  * Abdominal discomfort
  * Changes in bowel habits

### Young Children

* Chia seeds can be consumed by children when age-appropriate and prepared properly.
* Small portions and adequate hydration are recommended.
* Very young children should be supervised during consumption to reduce choking risk.

### Individuals on Restricted Diets

* People following medically prescribed diets should consult a qualified healthcare professional before making significant dietary changes.

</section>

## Drug / Condition Interactions (ওষুধ ও স্বাস্থ্যগত অবস্থার সাথে সম্পর্ক)

<section>

### Blood Pressure Medications

* Chia seeds may support a heart-healthy dietary pattern.
* Individuals taking blood pressure medications should consult a healthcare professional before consuming large amounts regularly.

### Blood-Thinning (Anticoagulant) Medications

* Chia seeds naturally contain Omega-3 fatty acids.
* Individuals taking anticoagulants or antiplatelet medications should discuss significant increases in chia consumption with their healthcare provider.

### Diabetes Management

* The fiber content of chia seeds may influence how quickly carbohydrates are absorbed from meals.
* Individuals using diabetes medications should monitor dietary changes and follow medical advice regarding nutrition.

### Gastrointestinal Conditions

People with conditions such as:

* Severe inflammatory bowel disease
* Intestinal narrowing
* Chronic swallowing disorders

should seek professional medical guidance before consuming large amounts of chia seeds.

### Pregnancy and Breastfeeding

* Chia seeds are generally consumed as a food rather than a supplement.
* Pregnant or breastfeeding individuals should follow a balanced diet and consult their healthcare provider regarding significant dietary changes if needed.

</section>

## Safe Usage Limits (নিরাপদ গ্রহণের পরিমাণ)

<section>

### Recommended Daily Intake

| User Group         | Suggested Daily Amount                          |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Beginners          | 1 teaspoon (about 5g)                           |
| General Adult Use  | 1–2 tablespoons (10–20g)                        |
| Common Upper Range | Up to 25g (approximately 2 tablespoons) per day |

### Introduce Gradually

* Start with small amounts and increase slowly.
* This helps the digestive system adapt to the increased fiber intake.

### Hydration Is Important

* Chia seeds absorb many times their weight in water.
* Drink adequate fluids throughout the day when consuming chia seeds regularly.

### Soaking Recommendation

* Soaking chia seeds before consumption is often preferred for comfort and ease of digestion.
* Typical soaking time: 20–30 minutes or overnight.

### Excessive Consumption

Regularly consuming very large quantities may increase the likelihood of:

* Digestive discomfort
* Bloating
* Excessive fiber intake
* Reduced comfort due to inadequate hydration

</section>

## Storage & Safety Notes (সংরক্ষণ ও নিরাপত্তা নির্দেশনা)

<section>

### Proper Storage

* Store in a cool, dry place.
* Keep away from moisture, direct sunlight, and heat.
* Use an airtight container after opening.

### Check Before Use

Do not consume if:

* The product develops an unusual odor.
* Visible mold is present.
* Moisture contamination has occurred.
* Packaging is damaged or compromised.

### Food, Not Medicine

* Chia seeds are a nutritious food ingredient.
* They are not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease.
* Health-related decisions should be based on professional medical advice when appropriate.

</section>
