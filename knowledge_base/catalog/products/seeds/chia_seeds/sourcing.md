# Sourcing
## Geographic Origin (ভৌগোলিক উৎস)

<section>

Chia seeds are obtained from the plant *Salvia hispanica*, a flowering species native to Central America, particularly regions of present-day Mexico and Guatemala. Although the crop originated in the Americas, commercial cultivation has expanded globally due to increasing demand for nutrient-rich seeds.

Today, most food-grade chia seeds available in Bangladesh are imported from major chia-producing countries such as:

* Australia
* India
* Argentina
* Paraguay
* Bolivia
* Mexico

The exact country of origin may vary depending on the supplier and import batch. Regardless of source, quality chia seeds are selected based on cleanliness, maturity, color consistency, and moisture content.

### Typical Characteristics of Imported Chia Seeds

* Small black, gray, white, or mottled seeds
* Low moisture content for extended shelf life
* Cleaned and processed for food-grade consumption
* Suitable for soaking, cooking, baking, and beverage preparation

</section>

## Harvest Method (সংগ্রহ ও ফসল সংগ্রহ পদ্ধতি)

<section>

Chia is an annual crop grown primarily for its seeds. After planting, the crop matures over several months before harvest.

### Cultivation Process

1. Seeds are sown in well-drained agricultural fields.
2. Plants grow and produce flowering spikes.
3. Mature flowers develop seed heads containing thousands of tiny chia seeds.
4. Fields are harvested when seed moisture reaches an appropriate level.

### Harvesting

* Commercial farms typically use mechanical harvesting equipment.
* Harvesting is performed after the plants have fully matured and dried.
* The seed heads are collected and separated from plant material.

### Post-Harvest Processing

After harvesting, chia seeds undergo several quality-control steps:

* Cleaning to remove dust, stems, and foreign materials
* Screening and grading by size and quality
* Drying when necessary to achieve safe storage moisture levels
* Packaging for export and distribution

These processes help ensure a clean, stable, and food-safe product.

</section>

## Season & Timing (মৌসুম ও সংগ্রহের সময়)

<section>

Unlike many highly seasonal foods, chia seeds are generally available year-round in retail markets.

### Growing Season

* Planting and harvest periods vary by country and climate.
* Most producing regions harvest once per year, although timing differs between hemispheres.

### Availability

* Due to international cultivation and global trade, fresh harvests enter the supply chain throughout the year.
* Properly dried chia seeds have a relatively long shelf life, reducing dependence on a specific harvest season.

### Consumer Availability

* Available throughout the year in Bangladesh.
* Not considered a seasonal product from the consumer perspective.

</section>

## Supply Chain (সরবরাহ ব্যবস্থা)

<section>

### From Farm to Consumer

#### Step 1: Cultivation

Chia plants are grown on commercial farms in suitable agricultural regions.

#### Step 2: Harvesting

Mature seed heads are collected and processed after harvest.

#### Step 3: Cleaning & Quality Control

Seeds are cleaned, graded, and inspected for food-grade quality standards.

#### Step 4: Export & Import

Processed chia seeds are packaged and shipped to international markets, including Bangladesh.

#### Step 5: Local Packaging & Distribution

Importers, wholesalers, or food suppliers may repackage the seeds into consumer-friendly sizes such as 500g or 1kg packs.

#### Step 6: Retail Sale

The packaged product reaches consumers through grocery stores, health food retailers, online marketplaces, and specialty food suppliers.

</section>

## Quality & Sustainability Notes (গুণগত মান ও টেকসই সংগ্রহ)

<section>

### Quality Considerations

Premium chia seeds are generally selected based on:

* Clean appearance
* Low moisture content
* Minimal foreign matter
* Proper storage conditions
* Food-grade processing standards

### Storage During Distribution

To maintain freshness and quality, chia seeds should be:

* Protected from moisture
* Stored in cool, dry conditions
* Kept in sealed packaging during transportation and storage

### Sustainability Considerations

Chia is generally considered a relatively resource-efficient crop compared to some other specialty foods. Sustainability practices vary by producer and region, but responsible cultivation commonly focuses on:

* Efficient land use
* Soil health management
* Reduced post-harvest waste
* Compliance with local agricultural regulations

Because sourcing may vary between suppliers and import batches, specific sustainability certifications or farming practices should be verified on a case-by-case basis where applicable.

</section>
