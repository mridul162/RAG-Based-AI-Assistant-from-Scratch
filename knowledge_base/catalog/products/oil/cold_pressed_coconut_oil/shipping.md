# Shipping
## Temperature Requirements

* **State Transitions:** The oil solidifies below 24°C and liquefies above this threshold. In Bangladesh, transit conditions typically keep the product in a liquid state.
* **Thermal Exposure:** While the oil has high thermal stability, prolonged exposure to extreme heat (above 40°C) inside delivery vans or cargo holds should be avoided to prevent pressure buildup inside the container.
* **Leakage Management:** Because the product liquefies during typical Bangladeshi transit conditions, high-quality, airtight sealing is critical to prevent capillary leakage through the cap threads.

---

## Packaging Notes

* **Primary Containment:** All variants (250mL, 500mL, and 1L) feature induction-sealed caps to provide a leak-proof barrier against pressure changes during handling.
* **Fragility & Shock Absorption:** * Glass bottle variants require double-layer bubble wrapping and placement in corrugated dividers.
* Food-grade PET variants are wrapped in single-layer defensive bubble wrap to minimize scuffing and impact punctures.


* **Orientation Marking:** Shipments containing liquid oils must be clearly labeled with **"This Side Up"** directional arrows and **"Fragile - Liquid"** handling stickers on the external box.

---

## Regulatory Considerations

* **Documentation:** Consignment notes must clearly list the product category as **"Edible/Cosmetic Vegetable Oil"** along with its corresponding batch ID and manufacturing date.
* **Safety Classifications:** This product is non-hazardous, non-flammable under standard conditions, and does not fall under the hazardous materials classification for domestic road and air courier networks.

---

## International Shipping

| Factor | Requirement / Restriction |
| --- | --- |
| **HS Code** | Maintained under **HS Code 1513.11.00** (Virgin Coconut Oil and its fractions). |
| **Certifications Required** | Phytosanitary Certificate from the Ministry of Agriculture (Bangladesh), BSTI certification, and a comprehensive Material Safety Data Sheet (MSDS). |
| **Air Freight Restrictions** | Many international commercial air couriers classify bulk liquids under specific restricted categories. Shipments require explicit approval, specialized leak-proof secondary containment bags, and declaration of weight. |
| **Import Regulations** | Destination country customs (e.g., FDA in the USA, EFSA in Europe) frequently mandate strict biological testing proofs to guarantee the oil is free from aflatoxins and chemical residues. |
