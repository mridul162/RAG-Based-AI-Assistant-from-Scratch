# Sourcing

## Geographic origin

Cold Pressed Coconut Oil is produced from mature coconuts grown in the tropical coastal regions of Bangladesh, where coconut palms thrive in warm, humid climates and saline-tolerant soils. These areas are known for producing coconuts with naturally rich oil content and strong aroma. The product is sourced domestically within Bangladesh and processed locally to maintain freshness and traceability.

## Harvest method

The oil is made from fresh, fully matured coconuts selected for high-quality oil extraction.

1. Harvesting mature coconuts

   Coconuts are collected once they reach full maturity, when the meat contains the highest concentration of oil and flavor compounds.

2. Preparing the coconut meat

   The coconuts are dehusked, cracked, and the white coconut meat is separated from the shell. The meat is then cleaned and prepared for extraction.

3. Cold-press extraction

   The coconut meat is mechanically pressed at low temperatures without chemical solvents, bleaching, or deodorizing. This method helps preserve the oil’s natural aroma, texture, and nutritional profile.

4. Filtering and bottling

   After extraction, the oil is lightly filtered to remove impurities while retaining its natural character. It is then bottled in food-grade containers for distribution.

The entire process emphasizes minimal processing and purity.

## Season & timing

Coconut palms in Bangladesh produce fruit throughout the year, so this product is not seasonal. However, harvest volumes can vary slightly with regional weather patterns and coconut growth cycles. Because coconuts are available year-round, production and supply remain relatively consistent across seasons.

## Supply chain

1. Local farmers and growers

   Coconuts are sourced from Bangladeshi coconut-growing communities and small agricultural producers.

2. Collection and transport

   Fresh coconuts are transported promptly to processing facilities to reduce storage time and maintain quality.

3. Cold-press processing

   Local processing units extract, filter, and bottle the oil using mechanical cold-press methods.

4. Distribution

   Finished bottles are distributed through retail, wholesale, and online channels across Bangladesh.

This relatively short supply chain helps preserve freshness and supports local agricultural economies.

## Sustainability & sourcing notes

* Minimal processing: Cold pressing reduces the need for chemical refining and high-energy processing.

* Local sourcing: Purchasing coconuts from Bangladeshi growers supports domestic agriculture and rural livelihoods.

* Whole-fruit utilization: Coconut by-products such as husks and shells can be repurposed for fuel, fiber, or agricultural use, helping reduce waste.

While specific farm certifications may vary by batch or supplier, the sourcing approach prioritizes freshness, purity, and local production.
