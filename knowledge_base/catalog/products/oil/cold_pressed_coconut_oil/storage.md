# Storage
## Ideal Conditions

* **Temperature:** Store in a cool, dry place between 15°C and 25°C.
* *Important Physical Characteristic:* Pure coconut oil naturally solidifies and turns opaque white at temperatures below 24°C (75°F). In the warmer climate of Bangladesh, it typically remains a clear liquid. Frequent transitioning between solid and liquid states due to temperature shifts is completely normal and does not alter the oil's nutritional profile or safety.


* **Light:** Keep away from direct sunlight. Ultraviolet rays can accelerate the degradation of the natural polyphenols.
* **Humidity:** Keep the container tightly capped to prevent moisture entry. High humidity or water entry promotes microbial growth and hydrolytic rancidity.

---

## Container Recommendations

* **Ideal Materials:** High-quality dark glass bottles or food-grade polyethylene terephthalate (PET) plastic. Glass is highly recommended if storing the oil for extended periods, as it provides an completely inert barrier.
* **Handling Precautions:** Always ensure that spoons, clean hands, or measuring utensils inserted into the bottle are entirely dry. Introducing water or food particles into the bottle is the primary cause of mold formation.

---

## Shelf Life

| Package Condition | Recommended Shelf Life |
| --- | --- |
| **Unopened Bottle** | 24 months from the date of manufacture. |
| **Opened Bottle** | 12 months (when stored tightly sealed under ideal conditions). |

> **Note:** Refrigeration is not required, but it can extend shelf life further. If refrigerated, the oil will become completely solid; place the bottle in a warm water bath to liquefy it before use. Do not microwave.

---

## Signs of Spoilage

* **Sour or Pungent Odor:** Pure coconut oil has a mild, sweet, nutty aroma. A sharp, sour, cheesy, or distinctly paint-like smell indicates that the fatty acids have oxidized and the oil has gone rancid.
* **Visual Discoloration:** The oil should be crystal clear as a liquid and pure white as a solid. Dark yellowing, grey streaks, or visible black/green spots indicate mold contamination (usually caused by introduced water).
* **Taste Alteration:** A bitter, burning, or intensely sour taste means the product is degraded and should be discarded.
