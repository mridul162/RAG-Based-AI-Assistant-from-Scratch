## When to Choose This Product

<section id="when-to-choose-this">

This cold pressed coconut oil from Bangladesh is likely the best choice when:

**1. Nutrient retention is the priority**  
For customers buying coconut oil specifically for its health properties — MCTs, lauric acid, polyphenols — cold pressing is essential. Refined/RBD oil is not a meaningful substitute in this context.

**2. Topical, hair care, or oil pulling use**  
The full aromatic and bioactive profile matters significantly for skin absorption, hair nourishment, and oil pulling. Refined oil is markedly inferior for these applications. This product delivers the genuine sensory and functional experience.

**3. Preference for local sourcing**  
Customers who prefer to support Bangladeshi producers, reduce import dependency, or value shorter supply chains will find this product preferable to imported virgin coconut oil.

**4. Price-to-quality balance over imported alternatives**  
At ৳770/500mL, this product sits meaningfully below most genuine imported virgin coconut oil brands (৳900–1,400), while offering a comparable cold pressed profile. For buyers who want authentic cold pressed quality without the import premium, this is the practical choice.

**5. Mild flavour profile needed in cooking**  
For baking, light sautéing, or dishes where a subtle coconut note is desirable but not dominant, cold pressed coconut oil performs well. It is better suited to this than the stronger-flavoured mustard oil.

**6. Gift or specialty purchase**  
Customers purchasing for gifting, health-conscious recipients, or specialised dietary needs (e.g., ketogenic diet, MCT supplementation) will find this product a more credible choice than refined oil.

## When a Competitor May Be Better

<section id="when-competitor-better">

This product is not the right choice in every situation. Be honest with customers in the following contexts:

**1. High-heat deep frying → Refined Coconut Oil or Mustard Oil**  
Refined/RBD coconut oil has a higher smoke point (~200–230°C vs ~175–200°C for cold pressed) and is more economical for large-volume frying. For traditional Bangladeshi cooking involving high-heat deep frying (e.g., pakora, fish fry), cold pressed coconut oil is not the optimal choice — and mustard oil remains the culturally dominant and functionally superior option for this use case.

**2. Budget-constrained everyday cooking → Refined Coconut Oil or Mustard Oil**  
At ~৳1.54/mL, this product is roughly 1.5–2.5x the price of refined coconut oil and mustard oil per mL. For households using oil primarily as a cooking medium — where the premium properties of cold pressing are largely destroyed by heat anyway — the value proposition of this product is weaker. Advise customers to reserve cold pressed oil for low-heat cooking, topical use, or raw applications.

**3. Requiring internationally recognised certification → Imported Virgin Coconut Oil**  
Customers who need verified organic certification (USDA Organic, EU Organic), internationally recognised quality marks, or specific import documentation for export-oriented food production may find established imported brands easier to work with. Local COA availability for domestic cold pressed oil is inconsistent across suppliers.

**4. Traditional Bangladeshi cooking flavour profile → Mustard Oil**  
For dishes where the characteristic pungency of mustard oil is integral to the flavour — traditional bharta, fish curry, hilsa preparations — coconut oil (cold pressed or otherwise) is not a substitute. Customers looking for an everyday cooking oil for Bangladeshi cuisine should be guided toward mustard oil rather than coconut oil.

**5. Very long shelf life required → Refined Coconut Oil**  
For bulk purchasing, institutional use, or storage in variable conditions, refined coconut oil's longer shelf life (24–36 months unopened) provides a practical advantage over cold pressed (18–24 months).
