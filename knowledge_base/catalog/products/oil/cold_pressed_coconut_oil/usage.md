# Usage

## Best uses

<section>

### Cooking and everyday food preparation

Cold Pressed Coconut Oil can be used as a general-purpose cooking oil for:

* Sautéing vegetables
* Light to medium frying
* Stir-frying
* Preparing curries
* Cooking rice-based dishes
* Making traditional Bangladeshi snacks

Its mild coconut flavor can add subtle aroma and richness to recipes.

### Baking

Suitable as a replacement for butter, margarine, or vegetable oil in many baked goods:

* Cakes
* Muffins
* Cookies
* Brownies
* Banana bread
* Homemade granola

When substituting, use approximately the same amount of coconut oil as the recipe's required fat unless otherwise specified.

### Hair care

Common uses include:

* Pre-shampoo hair treatment
* Scalp massage
* Hair conditioning
* Managing dryness and frizz

Apply a small amount to the scalp and hair lengths, leave for 30 minutes to several hours, then wash as usual.

### Skin care

Can be used as:

* Body moisturizer
* Dry skin treatment
* Massage oil
* Cuticle moisturizer

Apply a thin layer to clean skin as needed.

### Oil pulling

Traditionally used for oil pulling practices.

* Take approximately 1 tablespoon (15 mL)
* Swish gently in the mouth for 10–20 minutes
* Spit into a waste bin (not the sink)
* Rinse thoroughly afterward

</section>

### Hair care usage

* Short hair: 1–2 teaspoons
* Medium hair: 1 tablespoon
* Long hair: 1–2 tablespoons

Adjust according to hair thickness and dryness.

### Skin care usage

* Use a few drops for facial application if tolerated.
* Use a small amount for localized dry areas.
* Apply larger quantities as needed for body massage.

</section>

## Recipe ideas

<section>

### Coconut vegetable stir-fry

**Ingredients**

* 1 tablespoon Cold Pressed Coconut Oil
* Mixed vegetables
* Garlic
* Salt and spices

**Method**

Heat the oil, sauté garlic, add vegetables, and cook until tender. Serve with rice or bread.

### Coconut rice

**Ingredients**

* 2 cups cooked rice
* 1 tablespoon Cold Pressed Coconut Oil
* Salt to taste

**Method**

Mix warm rice with coconut oil and season lightly for a subtle coconut aroma.

### Homemade granola

**Ingredients**

* Oats
* Nuts and seeds
* Honey or date syrup
* 2–3 tablespoons Cold Pressed Coconut Oil

**Method**

Mix ingredients and bake until golden.

### Banana coconut smoothie

**Ingredients**

* 1 banana
* Milk or plant-based milk
* 1 teaspoon Cold Pressed Coconut Oil
* Ice cubes

**Method**

Blend until smooth and serve immediately.

### Simple coconut cake

Use Cold Pressed Coconut Oil in place of butter or vegetable oil to add mild coconut flavor and moisture.

</section>

## What to avoid

<section>

### Avoid overheating

Although coconut oil is relatively stable, prolonged overheating can degrade flavor and quality. Avoid repeatedly reusing the same oil for deep frying.

### Do not use as a medical treatment

Cold Pressed Coconut Oil is a food and personal care product. It should not replace professional medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment.

### Patch test for sensitive skin

Individuals with sensitive skin should test a small amount on a limited area before extensive topical use.

### Proper storage

* Keep the container tightly closed.
* Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight.
* Avoid introducing water or moisture into the container.

### Natural solidification is normal

Coconut oil may become cloudy, semi-solid, or fully solid in cooler temperatures. This is a natural characteristic of pure coconut oil and does not indicate spoilage or reduced quality.

### External use precautions

Avoid contact with eyes. If irritation occurs, discontinue use and rinse thoroughly.

</section>
