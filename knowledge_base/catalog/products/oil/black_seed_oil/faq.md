### 1. What is Black Seed Oil?

Black Seed Oil is a natural oil extracted from the seeds of *Nigella sativa*, commonly known as black seed, black cumin, or kalojira. It has been traditionally used in Bangladesh, South Asia, the Middle East, and other regions for culinary, wellness, skincare, and hair care purposes.

---

### 2. Is this the same as Kalojira Tel (কালোজিরার তেল)?

Yes. Black Seed Oil, Black Cumin Oil, Nigella Oil, and Kalojira Tel (কালোজিরার তেল) all refer to oil derived from *Nigella sativa* seeds. Different regions may use different names for the same product.

---

### 3. Is the oil cold-pressed?

Yes. This product is produced using a cold-pressing process, which minimizes heat exposure during extraction. Cold pressing helps preserve the oil's natural aroma, flavor, and naturally occurring compounds compared to heavily refined oils.

---

### 4. How can I tell if the oil is genuine?

Authentic Black Seed Oil typically has:

* A dark amber to deep golden-brown color.
* A strong, earthy, slightly spicy aroma.
* A pungent, mildly bitter taste.
* A natural appearance that may include slight sediment.

Extremely pale color, little aroma, or a completely neutral taste may indicate heavy refining or dilution.

---

### 5. Why does the oil have a strong smell and taste?

The characteristic aroma and flavor come naturally from the volatile compounds present in black seeds. A strong smell is generally expected in genuine cold-pressed Black Seed Oil and is one reason the oil is often used in small amounts.

---

### 6. Can I consume Black Seed Oil directly?

Many people traditionally consume Black Seed Oil in small quantities as part of their wellness routine. Because the flavor is strong, some users take it directly while others mix it with food or beverages.

If you are new to Black Seed Oil, start with a small amount to assess tolerance.

---

### 7. Can I use it on my hair?

Yes. Black Seed Oil is commonly used as a hair and scalp oil. It may help condition hair, support scalp moisture, and improve the appearance of dry or dull hair.

For first-time topical use, perform a patch test before applying more broadly.

---

### 8. Can I use it on my skin?

Yes. Black Seed Oil is frequently used in traditional skincare routines. It can be applied in small amounts to help support skin hydration and softness.

Individuals with sensitive skin should patch test first and discontinue use if irritation develops.

---

### 9. Does Black Seed Oil cure diseases?

No. Black Seed Oil should not be considered a cure, treatment, or prevention for any disease.

While it has a long history of traditional use and contains naturally occurring bioactive compounds, health outcomes vary between individuals. Medical conditions should be managed under the guidance of qualified healthcare professionals.

---

### 10. How much Black Seed Oil should I take daily?

Traditional use commonly ranges from:

* ½ teaspoon (approximately 2.5 mL) daily for beginners.
* Up to 1 teaspoon (approximately 5 mL) once or twice daily for adults.

Individual tolerance and needs vary. Excessive consumption is not recommended.

---

### 11. Are there any side effects?

Some individuals may experience:

* Stomach discomfort.
* Nausea.
* Heartburn.
* Diarrhea.
* Skin irritation when applied topically.

If adverse effects occur, discontinue use and seek professional advice if needed.

---

### 12. Who should consult a healthcare professional before use?

Consult a qualified healthcare professional before regular use if you:

* Are pregnant or breastfeeding.
* Take blood-thinning medications.
* Use blood sugar-lowering medications.
* Take blood pressure medications.
* Have significant liver or kidney disease.
* Are preparing for surgery.

---

### 13. How should I store Black Seed Oil?

For best quality:

* Store in a cool, dry place.
* Keep away from direct sunlight and excessive heat.
* Close the cap tightly after each use.
* Use clean, dry utensils if dispensing from larger containers.

Proper storage helps maintain freshness and flavor.

---

### 14. What is the shelf life of Black Seed Oil?

Shelf life depends on storage conditions and packaging. When stored properly and kept sealed, cold-pressed Black Seed Oil typically remains usable for many months.

Always check the product label for the specific best-before date and discard the oil if it develops an unusual smell, taste, or appearance.

---

### 15. Why might natural sediment appear in the bottle?

A small amount of natural sediment can occur in minimally processed cold-pressed oils. This is generally not a sign of spoilage and may simply reflect naturally occurring particles from the seed.

Shake gently if desired before use.

---

### 16. Why does the price change over time?

Black Seed Oil prices can fluctuate due to:

* Changes in kalojira seed prices.
* Seasonal agricultural conditions.
* Processing and packaging costs.
* Transportation and supply-chain factors.

Price changes do not necessarily indicate changes in product quality.
