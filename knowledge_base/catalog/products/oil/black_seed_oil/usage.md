# Usage Guide: Black Seed Oil (কালোজিরার তেল)

## Best Uses

<section>

Black Seed Oil is a versatile oil that can be used for dietary, personal care, and traditional wellness purposes. Due to its strong flavor and aroma, it is typically used in small quantities.

### Dietary Use

Common ways to consume Black Seed Oil include:

* Taken directly in small amounts.
* Mixed with honey or warm water.
* Added to smoothies or fresh juices.
* Drizzled over salads and prepared foods.
* Mixed into yogurt, milk, or herbal beverages.

### Hair Care Use

Black Seed Oil is traditionally used as a hair and scalp oil.

Common applications:

* Scalp massage oil.
* Hair conditioning treatment.
* Mixed with coconut oil or other carrier oils.
* Applied before shampooing as a pre-wash treatment.

### Skin Care Use

The oil can be used as part of a skincare routine:

* Applied to dry skin areas.
* Used as a massage oil.
* Mixed with carrier oils for facial or body use.
* Included in homemade skincare preparations.

### Traditional Household Use

Many households keep Black Seed Oil as a multipurpose wellness oil for regular dietary and personal care use.

</section>

## Dosage / Serving Suggestions

<section>

### Suggested Dietary Intake

| User Type         | Suggested Amount                                        |
| ----------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| First-Time Users  | ¼–½ teaspoon (1–2.5 mL) daily                           |
| Adults            | ½–1 teaspoon (2.5–5 mL) daily                           |
| Experienced Users | Up to 1 teaspoon once or twice daily, if well tolerated |
| Children          | Consult a healthcare professional before regular use    |

### How to Start

For new users:

1. Begin with a small amount.
2. Observe tolerance for several days.
3. Increase gradually if desired and well tolerated.

### Topical Application

#### For Hair

* Apply a small amount directly to the scalp and hair.
* Massage gently for several minutes.
* Leave for 30 minutes to several hours before washing.
* Repeat 1–3 times weekly as desired.

#### For Skin

* Apply a few drops to clean skin.
* Massage gently until absorbed.
* Use once or twice daily if suitable for your skin type.

Always perform a patch test before first-time topical use.

</section>

## Recipe Ideas

<section>

### 1. Honey & Black Seed Oil Blend

A traditional way to consume Black Seed Oil.

**Ingredients**

* 1 teaspoon honey
* ½ teaspoon Black Seed Oil

**Method**

Mix thoroughly and consume immediately.

---

### 2. Warm Water Wellness Drink

**Ingredients**

* 1 glass lukewarm water
* ½ teaspoon Black Seed Oil

**Method**

Stir well and drink slowly.

> Avoid using boiling water, which may affect flavor and aroma.

---

### 3. Yogurt Mix

**Ingredients**

* 1 bowl plain yogurt
* ½ teaspoon Black Seed Oil

**Method**

Mix and consume as a simple snack or breakfast addition.

---

### 4. Salad Dressing

**Ingredients**

* 1 tablespoon olive oil
* ½ teaspoon Black Seed Oil
* Lemon juice
* Salt to taste

**Method**

Whisk together and drizzle over salads.

---

### 5. Hair Nourishing Blend

**Ingredients**

* 1 tablespoon Black Seed Oil
* 1 tablespoon Coconut Oil

**Method**

Mix thoroughly and massage into scalp and hair before washing.

---

### 6. Massage Oil Blend

**Ingredients**

* 1 part Black Seed Oil
* 2–3 parts carrier oil (such as coconut or sesame oil)

**Method**

Blend and apply as desired for massage use.

</section>

## What to Avoid

<section>

### Avoid Excessive Consumption

More oil does not necessarily provide greater benefits. Excessive intake may increase the likelihood of digestive discomfort.

### Avoid High-Heat Cooking

Black Seed Oil is generally best used:

* As a finishing oil.
* In cold preparations.
* In low-heat applications.

Prolonged high heat may alter flavor, aroma, and some naturally occurring compounds.

### Avoid Use if Allergic

Do not use if you have a known allergy to:

* *Nigella sativa* (black seed).
* Black Seed Oil products.
* Any ingredient present in the product.

### Avoid Applying to Broken or Irritated Skin

Discontinue use if redness, itching, burning, or irritation occurs.

### Avoid Using Spoiled Oil

Do not consume or apply oil that has:

* A rancid smell.
* Unusual taste.
* Significant quality deterioration.

### Use Caution with Certain Medical Conditions

Consult a healthcare professional before regular use if you:

* Are pregnant or breastfeeding.
* Take blood-thinning medications.
* Use diabetes medications.
* Take blood pressure medications.
* Have a significant medical condition.

</section>

## Practical Tips

<section>

* Shake gently before use if natural sediment is present.
* Use clean, dry utensils when dispensing.
* Store away from direct sunlight and heat.
* Close the bottle tightly after each use.
* For best quality, use regularly after opening and follow the product's best-before guidance.

</section>
