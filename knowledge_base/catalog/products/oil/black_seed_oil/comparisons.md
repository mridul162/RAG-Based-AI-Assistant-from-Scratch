### Comparison Scope

This document compares our **pure cold-pressed Black Seed Oil (Bangladesh origin)** against:

1. **Imported Black Seed Oil** — typically from Egypt, Turkey, or Ethiopia; widely available online and in pharmacies.
2. **Commercially Refined Black Seed Oil** — locally or regionally produced but solvent-extracted and refined; common in grocery chains and large pharmacy retailers.
3. **Black Seed Oil Capsules** — encapsulated oil (imported brands); a common alternative format sold in pharmacies and health stores.

## When to Choose This Product

<section id="choose-this">

Choose our **Bangladesh cold-pressed Black Seed Oil** when:

- **You prioritise therapeutic quality at a fair local price.** Cold-pressed extraction preserves thymoquinone and other bioactive compounds that refined oils lose. You get genuine potency without paying an import premium.

- **You want to verify what you are buying.** As a direct-supply local product, you can request batch-specific COA, know the pressing date, and assess the oil sensorially before committing to larger quantities.

- **You use it regularly for health purposes.** For daily intake — immune support, respiratory health, joint care, or skin and hair application — consistent quality at an accessible price matters more than the marginal TQ advantage of premium imports.

- **Freshness matters to you.** Locally produced and distributed oil typically has a shorter chain from pressing to delivery, meaning less time in storage compared to imported stock.

- **You need flexible quantities.** Available in 100mL, 250mL, and 500mL, making it practical for both first-time buyers and regular users managing household stock.

## When a Competitor May Be Better

<section id="choose-competitor">

Be honest with yourself about use case before deciding:

**Choose Imported Cold-Pressed BSO (Egyptian / Turkish) if:**
- You require the highest documented thymoquinone content available, e.g. for a specific clinical or intensive therapeutic protocol. Egyptian *Nigella sativa* is well-studied and some varieties consistently yield higher TQ levels.
- You need internationally recognised third-party certifications (USDA Organic, ISO 22000) for professional, export, or institutional use.
- You are purchasing as a premium gift where brand recognition and certified labelling carry weight.

> Trade-off: You will pay 40–150% more per mL and accept longer supply chains with less ability to verify freshness locally.

---

**Choose Commercially Refined BSO if:**
- Your primary purpose is culinary flavouring rather than therapeutic benefit and cost is the dominant concern.
- You need a longer, predictable shelf life for pantry storage.

> Trade-off: Expect significantly reduced bioactive content. Not suitable if health benefits are the reason for purchase.

---

**Choose BSO Capsules if:**
- You travel frequently and need a portable, mess-free format.
- You find the strong taste and aroma of raw oil difficult to tolerate.
- Precise dosing in a capsule format is important for your routine.

> Trade-off: You cannot verify oil quality, freshness, or TQ content from the outside. Cost per equivalent mL of oil is significantly higher. Not suitable for topical (skin/hair) use.

</section>
