# Pricing
## Price drivers

The pricing structure of pure black seed oil (*Nigella sativa*) is determined by several specific supply chain and production factors:

* **Raw Material Market Costs:** The primary driver is the raw market price of high-quality Kalojira seeds in Bangladesh. Because the oil is 100% pure, fluctuations in local agricultural yields directly affect production costs.
* **Cold-Press Extraction Yields:** Cold-pressing is a mechanical process that extracts oil without utilizing external heat or chemical solvents. While this preserves the therapeutic integrity and volatile compounds (like thymoquinone), it yields significantly less oil per kilogram of seeds compared to industrial hot-pressing or chemical extraction methods, increasing the cost per milliliter.
* **Premium Sourcing:** Sourcing clean, mature, and well-dried seeds from reliable agricultural hubs in Bangladesh (such as Natore, Pabna, or Satkhira) carries a premium over lower-grade, dust-laden, or poorly stored seed varieties.

## Value justification

The product's price point reflects its strict quality benchmarks, which differentiate it from generic or highly processed retail oils:

* **Zero Dilution:** Many commercial options reduce costs by blending true black seed oil with cheap carrier oils (such as soybean or palm oil). This product is strictly unadulterated, providing the maximum concentration of active nutrients per dose.
* **Preservation of Bioactives:** The cold-pressing setup protects temperature-sensitive antioxidants and essential fatty acids from thermal degradation. This ensures the distinct, pungent aroma and characteristic throat-tingle remain intact, verifying its high medicinal efficacy.
* **Volume-Based Savings:** The pricing tiers are structurally optimized to reward high-volume users. Moving from the 100mL entry variant to the 500mL family variant reduces the cost per milliliter from 2.40 BDT to 2.20 BDT, offering an inherent cost incentive for long-term therapeutic regimens.

## Cost vs alternatives

When compared to alternative options in the market, the economic profile aligns with premium health and wellness categories:

* **Mass-Market Commercial Blends:** These are priced significantly lower (often 30% to 50% less) but contain high percentages of refined carrier oils. They lack the concentration of thymoquinone required for targeted health benefits, requiring much higher dosage volumes to achieve any therapeutic effect.
* **Chemically Extracted/Hexane-Processed Oils:** These options are cheaper due to high-efficiency industrial solvent extraction. However, they carry risks of chemical residue and lack the premium sensory profile (color, scent, and natural sediment) of unrefined, cold-pressed oil.
* **Imported Black Seed Oil Brands:** Imported counterparts frequently retail at double or triple the local BDT price due to international shipping costs, import duties, and global brand premiums, despite sharing the same core botanical source (*Nigella sativa*). Sourcing locally processed Bangladeshi seeds delivers identical therapeutic value at a far more competitive price per milliliter.
