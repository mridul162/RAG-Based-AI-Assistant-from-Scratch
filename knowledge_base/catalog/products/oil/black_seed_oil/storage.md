# Storage
## Ideal conditions

* **Temperature:** Store in a cool, dry place between 15°C and 25°C. Keep far away from direct heat sources, such as stoves, heaters, or continuous engine heat during transit.
* **Light:** Keep the product entirely away from direct sunlight. Ultraviolet (UV) light accelerates the oxidation process, rapidly degrading the active compound thymoquinone.
* **Humidity:** Protect from high humidity areas. Ensure the storage space is dry to minimize the risk of moisture accumulating around the bottle cap, which can compromise the seal.

## Container recommendations

* **Material:** Heavy amber or dark-tinted glass bottles are strongly recommended to filter out damaging light wavelengths.
* **Seal Integrity:** Always keep the cap tightly closed when not in use. Constant exposure to ambient air introduces oxygen, inducing rancidity over time.
* **Decanting Avoidance:** Keep the oil in its original retail container. If transferring to a different dispenser, ensure the new vessel is clean, thoroughly dried, dark-colored, and airtight. Do not use reactive metal containers (such as iron or copper).

## Shelf life

* **Unopened:** Up to **24 months** from the date of cold-pressing, provided it is kept under ideal temperature and light conditions.
* **Opened:** Best consumed within **6 to 9 months** after opening. Frequent opening exposes the oil to oxygen, which gradually reduces the potency of its therapeutic components.
* **Refrigeration:** Not strictly required, but storing the oil in a refrigerator after opening can extend its chemical stability. Note that cold storage may cause the oil to cloud slightly or thicken; this is normal and will reverse once the oil returns to room temperature.

## Signs of spoilage

Regularly check the oil before use. Discard the product immediately if any of the following changes occur:

* **Altered Aroma:** A transition from a sharp, pungent, peppery medicinal smell to a distinct stale, flat, or sour odor (characteristic of rancid cooking oil).
* **Taste Degradation:** A change from the expected intensely bitter, clean, scratchy tingle to a harsh, deeply unpleasant, or chemical sourness.
* **Texture Changes:** Excessive stickiness or an uncharacteristically thick, gummy buildup around the bottleneck that does not thin out at room temperature.
* **Visible Mold:** Any unusual mold growth or persistent white film around the interior cap or neck due to external moisture contamination. *(Note: Fine, dark seed sediment settling at the very bottom is completely natural and should not be confused with spoilage).*
