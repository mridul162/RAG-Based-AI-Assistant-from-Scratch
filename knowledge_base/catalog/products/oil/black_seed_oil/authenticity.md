# Authenticity Guide: Black Seed Oil (কালোজিরার তেল)

**Product ID:** black_seed_oil  
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-11  
**Purpose:** Help buyers verify they are receiving genuine, pure, cold-pressed Black Seed Oil (*Nigella sativa*).

---

## Visual Indicators

<section id="visual">

Genuine cold-pressed Black Seed Oil has a distinct appearance that is difficult to replicate cheaply.

| Attribute | Authentic | Suspect / Reject |
|---|---|---|
| **Color** | Deep amber to dark brownish-gold; slightly opaque | Pale yellow, very clear, or water-white |
| **Clarity** | Slight natural haziness or fine sediment is normal, especially when cold | Overly clear and brilliant (may indicate heavy filtering or solvent extraction) |
| **Sediment** | Fine dark particles may settle at the bottom — this is a positive sign | Completely sediment-free after weeks of storage |
| **Viscosity** | Medium-bodied; flows like a light olive oil | Watery and thin (diluted) or unusually thick and sticky (mixed with castor oil) |
| **Bottle** | Amber or dark glass preferred; UV-protective packaging | Clear plastic bottle with no mention of cold-press process |
| **Label** | States *Nigella sativa*, extraction method (cold-pressed), and origin | Vague terms like "black oil blend" or no botanical name |

> **Tip:** Hold the bottle up to light. Authentic cold-pressed oil should show a rich, dark amber tone — not a bright or translucent yellow.

</section>

## Taste & Aroma Indicators

<section id="sensory">

Black Seed Oil has one of the most recognisable sensory profiles of any edible oil. If the oil does not match this profile, treat it with suspicion.

### Aroma
- **Authentic:** Intensely pungent, earthy, and slightly smoky. A sharp, almost medicinal smell — similar to a blend of oregano, onion, and black pepper. The aroma should hit you immediately when the bottle is opened.
- **Suspect:** Faint or generic "oily" smell. A rancid, fishy, or excessively bitter odour (indicates oxidation from poor storage or old stock). A neutral or bland smell (indicates heavy dilution).

### Taste
- **Authentic:** Bitter, peppery, and warming on the tongue. Leaves a mild burning sensation in the throat — this is caused by **thymoquinone**, the primary active compound. A slight nuttiness may follow.
- **Suspect:** Tasteless or very mild. Purely bitter without the peppery warmth. Tastes like generic vegetable oil with slight colouring.

### Skin Feel (when applied)
- **Authentic:** Absorbs at a moderate pace; leaves a slightly warming sensation on skin. Not greasy or heavy.
- **Suspect:** Feels identical to a common carrier oil (like soybean or sunflower) with no warming sensation.

> **Field test:** Place 2–3 drops on the back of your hand and rub gently. Genuine Black Seed Oil will produce a mild, noticeable warmth within 30–60 seconds.

## Lab / Certification Checks

<section id="lab">

For buyers purchasing in bulk or for therapeutic use, the following checks provide the highest level of assurance.

### What to Look For on the Label or COA (Certificate of Analysis)

| Parameter | What Genuine Oil Should Show |
|---|---|
| **Botanical name** | *Nigella sativa* L. |
| **Extraction method** | Cold-pressed (expeller-pressed); no heat or chemical solvents |
| **Thymoquinone (TQ) content** | Typically **0.5% – 1.5%** for quality cold-pressed oil |
| **Free Fatty Acid (FFA)** | Low FFA indicates fresh, well-stored oil (below 2% is good) |
| **Peroxide Value** | Low peroxide value confirms the oil is not rancid |
| **Heavy metals** | Should be tested and within safe limits |
| **Adulteration screen** | Gas Chromatography–Mass Spectrometry (GC-MS) profile should match *Nigella sativa* fatty acid composition |

### Key Fatty Acids in Authentic *Nigella sativa* Oil (GC-MS Reference)

| Fatty Acid | Expected Range |
|---|---|
| Linoleic acid (Omega-6) | 50–60% |
| Oleic acid (Omega-9) | 20–25% |
| Palmitic acid | 10–14% |
| Thymoquinone (active) | 0.5–1.5% |

> Any significant deviation from the linoleic acid dominance is a red flag for adulteration with cheaper oils.

### Bangladesh-Specific Notes
- There is currently no mandatory national certification specific to cold-pressed Black Seed Oil in Bangladesh.
- Reputable suppliers should be able to provide a **batch-level COA** from a third-party lab on request.
- BSTI (Bangladesh Standards and Testing Institution) registration on edible oils is a basic compliance marker — its absence on a commercial product is a concern.

</section>

## Common Fakes to Watch For

<section id="fakes">

Black Seed Oil commands a premium price, making it a frequent target for adulteration. The following are the most commonly encountered fraudulent practices in the Bangladesh market.

### 1. Dilution with Cheap Carrier Oils
**What happens:** Soybean oil, sunflower oil, or refined palm oil is blended in at 30–70% to stretch volume.  
**How to detect:**
- Aroma will be noticeably weaker and less pungent.
- Colour will be lighter and more yellow.
- The throat-warming sensation after tasting will be absent or very faint.
- GC-MS will show elevated oleic acid or linoleic acid ratios inconsistent with pure *Nigella sativa*.

---

### 2. Solvent-Extracted Oil Sold as Cold-Pressed
**What happens:** Heat or chemical solvents (like hexane) are used to extract oil cheaply, but the label claims "cold-pressed."  
**How to detect:**
- Solvent-extracted oil tends to be paler and cleaner in appearance.
- It lacks the characteristic pungency — thymoquinone degrades significantly under heat.
- Thymoquinone content on lab test will be very low (often below 0.3%).
- No natural sediment present.

---

### 3. Old or Oxidised Stock Repackaged
**What happens:** Expired or improperly stored oil is repackaged in fresh bottles.  
**How to detect:**
- Smell: rancid, stale, or "off" — similar to old cooking oil.
- Taste: excessively bitter and acrid without the characteristic warmth.
- Check manufacture/pressing date on label; if absent, ask the supplier.
- High peroxide value on lab test confirms oxidation.

---

### 4. Mixing with Castor Oil or Mineral Oil
**What happens:** Castor oil (thick and viscous) or low-grade mineral oil is added to increase volume and alter texture to mimic a thicker, richer product.  
**How to detect:**
- Castor blend: Oil feels unusually sticky and does not spread easily on skin.
- Mineral oil blend: Does not absorb into skin at all — sits on the surface indefinitely.
- Neither produces the warming sensation on skin or tongue.

---

### 5. Synthetic Thymoquinone Spiking
**What happens:** A small amount of synthetic thymoquinone is added to cheap oil to pass basic TQ content tests.  
**How to detect:** This is difficult to detect without a full **GC-MS fatty acid profile**. If TQ content is in range but the fatty acid composition does not match *Nigella sativa*, suspect spiking. This is rare in the consumer market but possible at industrial scale.

---

### Quick Authenticity Checklist for Buyers

- [ ] Dark amber colour with slight natural haze
- [ ] Intense, pungent aroma upon opening
- [ ] Peppery, bitter taste with throat warmth
- [ ] Mild warming sensation when rubbed on skin
- [ ] Label states *Nigella sativa*, cold-pressed, with a pressing/manufacture date
- [ ] Supplier can provide COA or lab report on request
- [ ] No unusually thin consistency or neutral smell
