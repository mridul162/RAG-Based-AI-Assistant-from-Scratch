# Sourcing
# Sourcing Information: Black Seed Oil (কালোজিরার তেল)

## Geographic Origin

<section>

Black Seed Oil is produced from the seeds of *Nigella sativa*, commonly known as kalojira (কালোজিরা), black seed, or black cumin. The seeds used for this product are sourced from agricultural regions within Bangladesh where kalojira is cultivated as a spice and oilseed crop.

### Origin Summary

| Attribute       | Details                           |
| --------------- | --------------------------------- |
| Botanical Name  | *Nigella sativa*                  |
| Common Names    | Kalojira, Black Seed, Black Cumin |
| Product Origin  | Bangladesh                        |
| Raw Material    | Mature dried kalojira seeds       |
| Processing Type | Cold-pressed oil extraction       |

### Growing Conditions

Kalojira grows well in:

* Well-drained soils.
* Moderate winter temperatures.
* Dry conditions during seed maturation.
* Small-scale and commercial farming systems.

Bangladesh's climate supports seasonal cultivation of kalojira, making it a familiar crop in many agricultural communities.

### Local Sourcing Benefits

Locally sourced seeds may offer:

* Reduced transportation distances.
* Faster movement from harvest to processing.
* Support for domestic agricultural producers.
* Improved supply chain visibility compared to some imported alternatives.

</section>

## Harvest Method

<section>

### Cultivation

Kalojira is grown primarily for its aromatic seeds. Farmers cultivate the crop during the cooler growing season and manage it using conventional agricultural practices suitable for local conditions.

### Harvesting Process

The harvesting process typically includes:

1. Allowing seed pods to mature fully on the plant.
2. Harvesting plants when seed capsules dry and ripen.
3. Drying harvested material to reduce moisture.
4. Separating seeds from stems and plant matter.
5. Cleaning and sorting seeds before processing.

### Seed Selection

Before oil extraction, seeds are generally:

* Cleaned to remove dust and foreign materials.
* Inspected for quality.
* Dried to an appropriate moisture level.
* Sorted to improve consistency.

### Oil Extraction

The oil is obtained through cold pressing.

Cold pressing typically involves:

* Mechanical extraction without solvent use.
* Minimal heat generation during processing.
* Retention of the oil's characteristic aroma, flavor, and natural composition.

The resulting oil is filtered before packaging while remaining less processed than heavily refined oils.

</section>

## Season & Timing

<section>

### Crop Season

In Bangladesh, kalojira is generally cultivated as a winter-season crop.

Typical agricultural stages include:

| Stage         | Approximate Period          |
| ------------- | --------------------------- |
| Sowing        | Late autumn to early winter |
| Growth Period | Winter months               |
| Harvest       | Late winter to early spring |

Actual timing may vary depending on region, weather conditions, and farming practices.

### Availability

Although harvesting is seasonal, Black Seed Oil is available throughout the year because:

* Seeds can be stored after harvest.
* Oil can be pressed in batches as needed.
* Proper storage helps preserve seed quality for future processing.

As a result, consumers can generally purchase Black Seed Oil year-round.

### Freshness Considerations

Freshly harvested and properly stored seeds are preferred for oil production because seed quality influences:

* Aroma.
* Flavor.
* Appearance.
* Overall oil characteristics.

</section>

## Supply Chain

<section>

### From Farm to Consumer

The typical supply chain follows these stages:

```text
Cultivation
      ↓
Harvesting
      ↓
Drying & Cleaning
      ↓
Seed Storage
      ↓
Cold Pressing
      ↓
Filtration
      ↓
Packaging
      ↓
Distribution
      ↓
Consumer
```

### Processing Workflow

#### Step 1: Seed Procurement

Quality kalojira seeds are sourced from agricultural producers and suppliers.

#### Step 2: Cleaning & Preparation

Seeds are cleaned and prepared for pressing to remove impurities and improve processing consistency.

#### Step 3: Cold Press Extraction

Oil is mechanically extracted using cold-press methods that avoid chemical solvents.

#### Step 4: Filtration

The oil is filtered to remove larger suspended particles while maintaining its natural character.

#### Step 5: Packaging

The finished oil is packaged into retail containers of various sizes, including:

* 100 mL
* 250 mL
* 500 mL

#### Step 6: Distribution

Products are distributed through retail, direct-sales, online, and delivery channels throughout Bangladesh.

<section>

Local sourcing may contribute to:

* Income opportunities for Bangladeshi farmers.

* Avoids chemical solvent extraction.
