# Usage

## Best Uses

First Cold Pressed Deshi Mustard Oil is primarily used as a cooking oil and flavoring ingredient in traditional Bengali cuisine. Its strong aroma and pungent taste make it particularly suitable for dishes where mustard flavor is a key component.

### Recommended Culinary Uses

* Fish curries and mustard-based fish recipes
* Bhorta (mashed vegetables, fish, or lentils)
* Vegetable curries and stir-fries
* Dal tempering (tadka)
* Pickles (achar)
* Marinades for fish and meat
* Traditional Bengali shorshe dishes
* Salad-style raw finishing in selected recipes
* Deep frying and shallow frying
* Homemade spice pastes and condiments

### Traditional Non-Culinary Uses

In many South Asian households, mustard oil is traditionally used for:

* Hair oiling and scalp massage
* Body massage
* Seasonal skin care routines

These uses are traditional cultural practices and are not intended as medical treatments.

### General Guidance

* Start with smaller amounts if unfamiliar with mustard oil's pungent flavor.
* Adjust quantity according to personal taste and recipe requirements.
* Use as part of a balanced diet, as with any edible oil.

## Recipe Ideas

### Traditional Aloo Bhorta

**Ingredients**

* Boiled potatoes
* Mustard oil
* Green chilies
* Onion
* Salt

**Method**

1. Mash boiled potatoes.
2. Add chopped onion, green chilies, and salt.
3. Mix in 1–2 teaspoons of mustard oil.
4. Serve immediately.

### Shorshe Fish Marinade

**Ingredients**

* Fish pieces
* Mustard paste
* Mustard oil
* Salt
* Turmeric

**Method**

1. Combine mustard paste, oil, salt, and turmeric.
2. Coat fish thoroughly.
3. Cook according to recipe.

### Dal Tadka

**Ingredients**

* Cooked lentils
* Mustard oil
* Garlic
* Dried chili
* Mustard or cumin seeds

**Method**

1. Heat mustard oil.
2. Add spices and aromatics.
3. Pour over cooked lentils before serving.

### Mixed Vegetable Stir-Fry

**Ingredients**

* Seasonal vegetables
* Mustard oil
* Garlic
* Salt
* Spices

**Method**

1. Heat mustard oil.
2. Add aromatics and vegetables.
3. Stir-fry until cooked.

### Homemade Pickle Base

Mustard oil is commonly used as the oil base in traditional pickles due to its strong flavor and compatibility with spices.

## What to Avoid

### 1. Avoid Moisture Contamination

Always use a clean, dry spoon and ensure water does not enter the container, as moisture can reduce product quality and shelf life.

### 2. Avoid Direct Sunlight

Store in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and excessive heat to help preserve aroma and freshness.

### 3. Avoid Repeated Reheating

Do not repeatedly reuse or overheat the same batch of oil multiple times, as this can negatively affect flavor and overall quality.

### 4. Avoid Mixing with Strongly Flavored Oils

Mixing with other oils may dilute the distinctive aroma and taste that characterize traditional cold pressed mustard oil.

### 5. Use Moderately

Like all edible oils, mustard oil is calorie-dense and should be consumed in moderation as part of a balanced diet.

### 6. External Use Considerations

For traditional hair or skin applications:

* Perform a patch test before first use.
* Discontinue use if irritation occurs.
* Avoid contact with eyes and sensitive areas.

### 7. Quality Preservation

* Keep the container tightly sealed after use.
* Avoid storing near strong odors, as oils can absorb surrounding smells over time.
* Follow the product's recommended storage and best-before guidelines.
