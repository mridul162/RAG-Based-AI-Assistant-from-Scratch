## When to Choose This Product

<section id="choose-this">

Choose **First Cold Pressed Deshi Mustard Oil** when:

- You want **authentic Bengali flavour** — the sharp, pungent taste that defines dishes like shorshe ilish (hilsa in mustard sauce), bhorta, or mustard-based achar.
- You prioritize **maximum nutritional value** — cold pressing preserves natural omega-3 fatty acids, antioxidants, and tocopherols that are largely destroyed in refined oil.
- You are looking for an oil with **no chemical processing** — no hexane, no deodorizers, no bleaching agents.
- You use mustard oil for **topical purposes** — hair oiling, joint massage, or skin care — where the full natural compound profile matters.
- You want **traceability and purity** — small-batch cold pressed production makes it easier to verify origin and process than large industrial refining.
- You cook traditional dishes where the **raw or lightly heated mustard oil** flavour is part of the recipe (e.g. mustard-marinated fish, raw bhorta dressing).

## When a Competitor May Be Better

<section id="choose-competitor">

Consider an alternative when:

**Commercially Refined Mustard Oil (Teer, Rupchanda, etc.) may be better if:**
- You need a **neutral-tasting oil** that won't overpower delicate dishes or non-Bengali recipes.
- You are cooking for guests or children who are **not accustomed to strong mustard pungency**.
- You need **long shelf life** and buy oil in large quantities infrequently.
- **Budget is the primary concern** — refined mustard oil is significantly cheaper per litre at scale.
- You need **consistent, regulated quality** with widely available BSTI certification and branded accountability.

**Machine-Ghani / Hot-Pressed Deshi Mustard Oil may be better if:**
- You want **some mustard character** but at a lower price point than cold pressed.
- You have access to a trusted local ghani mill where you can verify the seed source.
- You are using it primarily for **high-heat frying** where the nutritional difference between cold and hot pressed is less relevant.

**Imported / Hybrid Mustard Oil (Sarson) may be better if:**
- You are cooking **North Indian or Pakistani dishes** (e.g. sarson da saag) that are traditionally made with yellow mustard oil.
- You or your household prefer **milder mustard flavour** with less pungency.
- You are introducing mustard oil to someone **new to the ingredient** and want a gentler entry point.
