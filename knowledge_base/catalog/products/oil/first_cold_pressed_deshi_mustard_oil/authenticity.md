# Authenticity Guide — First Cold Pressed Deshi Mustard Oil

**Product:** First Cold Pressed Deshi Mustard Oil (প্রথম চাপের দেশি সরিষার তেল)
**Purpose:** Help buyers verify they are receiving genuine, unadulterated first cold pressed deshi mustard oil.

---

## Visual Indicators

<section id="visual">

| Indicator | Authentic | Suspect / Adulterated |
|---|---|---|
| **Color** | Deep golden-amber to slightly greenish-gold; naturally opaque or semi-opaque | Pale yellow, water-clear, or unnaturally bright yellow |
| **Clarity** | Slightly cloudy or hazy at room temperature — natural fine sediment is acceptable | Perfectly clear and water-thin (suggests refined oil blending) |
| **Cold behavior** | Thickens noticeably in cool temperatures (below ~15°C); may show slight solidification at edges | Stays fully liquid and thin in cold — suggests dilution with refined oil |
| **Sediment** | Fine natural seed sediment may settle at the bottom of unfiltered variants | No sediment at all in an unfiltered product can indicate heavy processing |
| **Surface sheen** | Rich, slightly viscous surface when poured | Watery, fast-spreading surface |

**Practical test:** Pour a small amount onto a white plate. Genuine cold pressed deshi mustard oil will spread slowly and leave a deep golden film.

</section>

## Taste & Aroma Indicators

<section id="sensory">

Genuine first cold pressed deshi mustard oil has a distinct sensory profile that is difficult to fake convincingly.

**Aroma**
- Strong, sharp, pungent smell — this comes from natural **allyl isothiocyanate** compounds in deshi (local variety) mustard seeds
- The pungency should be immediate and strong when the bottle is opened — it may cause mild eye-watering
- Aroma should be raw and "alive" — not flat, stale, or chemically neutral
- Refined or blended oils have a weak, flat, or generic vegetable oil smell

**Taste**
- Sharp, slightly bitter, with a characteristic biting heat on the tongue and back of the throat
- The heat sensation should linger briefly — similar to mild horseradish
- Should leave a clean finish without a greasy or rancid aftertaste
- Artificially colored or blended oils taste bland, flat, or generically "oily"

**Cooking behavior**
- When heated in a pan, genuine mustard oil produces a pungent, nose-clearing steam before settling into a nutty aroma — this is normal and expected
- The smoke point is moderate; it will smoke before other refined oils would

**Quick sensory check:** Rub a few drops between your palms. Genuine cold pressed deshi mustard oil will produce a warm, pungent, sharp smell when friction is applied. Adulterated oil smells faint or neutral.

</section>

## Lab / Certification Checks

<section id="certification">

For high-confidence verification, the following laboratory and documentary checks apply:

**What to look for on the label / documentation**
- Confirmation that extraction used **cold-press (expeller) method only** — no solvent extraction, no hexane
- Statement of **"first press"** or **"first cold pressed"** — this means oil from the first mechanical pressing of seeds, without reprocessing
- Country and ideally region of seed origin (Bangladesh; ideally North Bengal / Rajshahi / Dinajpur region for deshi varieties)
- Net weight and batch/lot number for traceability

**Recommended lab parameters (for bulk or B2B buyers)**

| Parameter | Expected value for genuine product |
|---|---|
| **Erucic acid content** | High (typically 30–50%) — natural marker of true mustard oil; low erucic acid suggests blending with other oils |
| **Allyl isothiocyanate** | Detectable — confirms deshi seed variety |
| **Peroxide value** | Low (fresh oil); elevated peroxide = rancid or old stock |
| **Free Fatty Acid (FFA)** | Low in fresh, well-stored oil |
| **Refractive index** | Consistent with mustard oil standard |
| **Absence of mineral oil / paraffin** | Confirm via standard adulteration panel |

**Regulatory note:** In Bangladesh, edible oils fall under BSTI (Bangladesh Standards and Testing Institution) standards. Buyers may request a BSTI compliance declaration from the seller.

</section>

## Common Fakes to Watch For

<section id="fakes">

### 1. Refined oil sold as cold pressed
**What it is:** Commercially refined mustard oil (heat-extracted, solvent-processed, deodorized) relabeled as "cold pressed."
**How to spot it:** Weak or absent pungency; pale color; stays fully liquid and clear in cold; no lingering heat on the tongue.

---

### 2. Blending with cheaper vegetable oils
**What it is:** Genuine mustard oil extended with refined soybean, palm, or sunflower oil.
**How to spot it:** Reduced pungency, thinner consistency, lighter color, little to no eye-watering when bottle is opened. Lab test will show low erucic acid and altered fatty acid profile.

---

### 3. Artificial coloring to mimic mustard oil
**What it is:** Pale refined oil colored with synthetic dyes (e.g. metanil yellow) to achieve a golden-yellow appearance.
**How to spot it:** Color looks unnaturally uniform and bright. No real pungency. Aroma is flat. A turmeric paper test or basic lab dye screening can detect synthetic colorants.

> ⚠️ **Metanil yellow** (also called "butter yellow") is a banned colorant in Bangladesh and is toxic — its presence in edible oil is a serious food safety issue.

---

### 4. Second or third press sold as first press
**What it is:** Oil extracted from seed cake left after the first press — lower quality, weaker flavor — but labeled as first cold pressed.
**How to spot it:** Weaker aroma and color compared to true first press. Requires lab testing (fatty acid profile, tocopherol levels) for definitive identification.

---

### 5. Non-deshi seed variety sold as "deshi"
**What it is:** Oil pressed from imported hybrid or yellow mustard (sarson) seeds rather than local Bangladeshi deshi (black/brown) mustard.
**How to spot it:** Deshi variety has a sharper, more aggressive pungency and darker color. Hybrid/yellow mustard oil is milder and lighter. Seed origin documentation from the seller is the best check.

---

### Quick Field Test Summary

| Test | Method | Genuine result |
|---|---|---|
| Cold test | Refrigerate for 30 min | Thickens or hazes |
| Smell test | Open bottle, sniff | Sharp, pungent, eye-watering |
| Palm rub test | Rub drops between palms | Strong pungent warmth |
| Plate spread test | Drop on white plate | Slow spread, deep golden film |
| Heat test | Heat small amount in pan | Pungent smoke, then nutty aroma |

</section>
