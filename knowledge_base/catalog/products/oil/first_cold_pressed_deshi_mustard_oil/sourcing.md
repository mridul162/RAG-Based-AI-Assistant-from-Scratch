## Geographic Origin

First Cold Pressed Deshi Mustard Oil is produced from locally cultivated mustard seeds grown in agricultural regions of Bangladesh. Mustard is one of the country's most important oilseed crops and is widely cultivated across fertile farming areas where winter-season conditions support healthy seed development.

While specific sourcing regions may vary by harvest and supplier, mustard seeds are commonly sourced from traditional farming areas known for oilseed production. The quality of the final oil depends largely on seed freshness, variety, moisture content, and post-harvest handling.

## Harvest Method

Mustard oil production begins with the cultivation and harvesting of mature mustard plants.

### Seed Harvesting Process

1. Mustard crops are grown during the cool winter growing season.
2. Plants are allowed to mature until seed pods fully develop.
3. Harvesting takes place when pods dry naturally and seeds reach optimal maturity.
4. Seeds are cleaned to remove dust, plant matter, and impurities.
5. The cleaned seeds are dried to appropriate moisture levels before oil extraction.

### First Cold Pressing Process

After cleaning and preparation:

1. Mustard seeds are fed into a mechanical (wooden) oil press.
2. Oil is extracted through physical pressure rather than chemical solvents.
3. Excessive external heat is avoided during extraction.
4. The freshly extracted oil is allowed to settle naturally.
5. Fine seed particles may be removed through filtration.
6. The oil is packaged after quality inspection.

The first pressing is generally valued because it captures the strongest natural aroma, flavor, and color from the mustard seeds.

## Season & Timing

### Mustard Growing Season

| Stage          | Typical Timing in Bangladesh          |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Sowing         | October – November                    |
| Crop Growth    | November – January                    |
| Harvest        | January – March                       |
| Oil Production | Year-round from harvested seed stocks |

Although mustard is a seasonal crop, properly stored seeds allow cold pressed mustard oil to be produced and supplied throughout the year.

### Natural Seasonal Variation

Minor differences may occur between production batches due to:

* Seed variety
* Weather conditions
* Soil characteristics
* Harvest timing
* Storage conditions

Such variations are normal in traditionally sourced agricultural products.

## Supply Chain

<section>

### Farm-to-Consumer Journey

**Step 1: Seed Cultivation**

* Farmers cultivate mustard crops using seasonal agricultural practices.

**Step 2: Harvesting**

* Mature mustard seeds are harvested and collected.

**Step 3: Cleaning & Sorting**

* Seeds are cleaned and sorted to improve extraction quality.

**Step 4: Cold Press Extraction**

* Oil is mechanically extracted through first cold pressing without solvent-based processing.

**Step 5: Settling & Filtration**

* Freshly pressed oil is allowed to settle and is filtered when necessary.

**Step 6: Packaging**

* Oil is packed into sealed food-grade containers to maintain freshness and prevent contamination.

**Step 7: Distribution**

* Packaged oil is supplied to wholesalers, retailers, and consumers.

## Sustainability & Ethical Sourcing Notes

### Agricultural Sustainability

* Mustard is a widely cultivated local oilseed crop that supports domestic agriculture.
* Local sourcing can reduce transportation distances compared with imported edible oils.
* Mustard cultivation often forms part of seasonal crop rotation systems used by Bangladeshi farmers.

### Support for Rural Communities

* Purchasing locally produced mustard oil helps support farmers, seed suppliers, oil processors, and rural agricultural economies.
* Traditional cold-press operations often create value within local supply chains rather than relying heavily on imported processing inputs.

### Processing Considerations

* Cold pressing relies on mechanical (wooden) extraction rather than chemical solvent extraction.
* Minimal processing helps preserve the oil's natural characteristics while reducing processing complexity.

### Quality & Traceability

The final quality of cold pressed mustard oil depends on:

* Seed quality
* Freshness of harvested mustard
* Proper storage practices
* Hygienic processing conditions
* Appropriate packaging and transportation

Specific sustainability certifications, organic claims, or ethical sourcing certifications should only be stated when independently verified by the producer or certifying authority.
