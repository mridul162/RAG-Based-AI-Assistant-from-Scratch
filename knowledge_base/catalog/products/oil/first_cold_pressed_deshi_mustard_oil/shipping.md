# Shipping and Handling Profile: First Cold Pressed Deshi Mustard Oil

This document provides technical guidelines and operational specifications for the safe packaging, fulfillment, and transit of **First Cold Pressed Deshi Mustard Oil (প্রথম চাপের দেশি সরিষার তেল)**.

---

## Temperature Requirements

Because this product is unrefined and 100% cold-pressed, it retains volatile flavor compounds (specifically Allyl Isothiocyanate) and natural antioxidants that are sensitive to extreme environments.

* **Optimal Transit Temperature:** 15°C to 25°C (Room temperature).
* **Heat Sensitivity:** Avoid prolonged exposure to direct sunlight, engine manifold heat in delivery vehicles, or staging environments exceeding 40°C. High heat accelerates oxidation and diminishes the signature pungent aroma (*ঝাঁজ*).
* **Cold Sensitivity:** In cold weather or air-conditioned storage below 10°C, the oil may exhibit slight cloudiness or natural sedimentation. This is non-hazardous and reverses when returned to room temperature; it does not require heated transport.

---

## Packaging Notes

As a liquid fat product prone to leakage under pressure changes and mechanical shocks, fulfillment must adhere to the following packaging protocols:

### Container Specifications

* **1L and 2L Variants:** Housed in food-grade, high-density polyethylene (HDPE) or thick PET bottles featuring an inner induction-sealed wad and a tamper-evident screw cap.
* **5L Variant:** Housed in heavy-duty HDPE jerrycans with reinforced handles and secure industrial caps.

### Fulfillment & Transit Security

* **Orientation Constraints:** Packages must be marked with **"This Side Up"** directional arrows to keep the bottle caps upright and prevent slow capillary leaks.
* **Leak Proofing:** Every individual bottle must be placed into a sealed poly-bag before outer boxing to contain accidental leakage.
* **Cushioning:** Use sufficient corrugated dividers, air pillows, or bubble wrap to prevent shifting inside the master carton, particularly for the heavier 2L and 5L variants.

---

## Regulatory Considerations

### Domestic Transit (Within Bangladesh)

* **Spill Classification:** Non-hazardous consumer good. However, in the event of major en-route transit breakage, the spill must be treated with absorbent material (sawdust or sand) due to slip hazards; it must not be flushed directly into municipal drainage systems.
* **Weight Restrictions:** Standard delivery couriers within Bangladesh handle up to 5kg or 10kg per parcel without special freight classification. Consolidated orders exceeding 20kg (multiple 5L containers) must shift to heavy-goods logistics handlers.

---

## International Shipping

Exporting mustard oil involves strict customs classifications and regulatory barriers depending on the destination country.

* **HS Code Category:** `1514.91.10` (Crude/Unrefined Mustard Oil) or `1514.99.00` (Other Mustard Oil and fractions).
* **United States (FDA Restrictions):** The US FDA restricts the import of express mustard oil for cooking purposes due to high natural **Erucic acid** levels (which typically exceed 40% in authentic *Deshi* seeds). International shipments bound for the US must be explicitly labeled and documented as **"For External Use Only"** or designated for cosmetic/massage application to pass US Customs.
* **EU and UK Regulations:** Allowed in limited quantities as a traditional food product or specialty item, but requires a complete Certificate of Analysis (CoA), phytosanitary clearance, and commercial invoices detailing the exact botanical name (*Brassica* species).
* **Required Export Documentation:**
1. Material Safety Data Sheet (MSDS) / Product Specification Sheet.
2. Certificate of Analysis (Purity and Composition report).
3. BSTI (Bangladesh Standards and Testing Institution) certification.
4. Commercial Invoice and Packing List specifying liquid volume.
