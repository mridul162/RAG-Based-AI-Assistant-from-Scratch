# Best Uses

<section>

Black Seed Flower Honey is a versatile natural sweetener that can be consumed directly or incorporated into a variety of foods and beverages. Its floral aroma and mild herbal notes make it particularly suitable for both traditional and modern dietary uses.

### Direct consumption

* Consume directly by the spoonful as a natural sweetener.
* Enjoy on its own to appreciate its distinctive flavor profile.

### Beverages

* Stir into warm water for a simple honey drink.
* Add to tea, herbal infusions, or milk.
* Blend into smoothies and fruit-based beverages.
* Use as a sweetener for homemade lemon drinks.

### Breakfast foods

* Drizzle over toast, bread, or flatbread.
* Mix into oatmeal, porridge, or cereal.
* Add to yogurt or fruit bowls.
* Use as a topping for pancakes, waffles, or crepes.

### Cooking and baking

* Use as a natural sweetener in desserts.
* Incorporate into salad dressings and marinades.
* Add to homemade energy bars or granola.
* Use in baking recipes that call for honey.

</section>

# Dosage / Serving Suggestions

<section>

There is no universally recommended daily serving size for honey. Consumption should be based on individual dietary needs and overall sugar intake.

<table>

| Usage Method           | Suggested Serving                       |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| Direct consumption     | 1–2 teaspoons (5–10 g) per serving      |
| Warm water or tea      | 1–2 teaspoons per cup                   |
| Yogurt or oatmeal      | 1–2 teaspoons per bowl                  |
| Toast or bread topping | 1–2 teaspoons                           |
| Smoothies              | 1 tablespoon (approximately 15 g)       |
| Baking and recipes     | Adjust according to recipe requirements |

</table>

### General guidance

* Start with smaller amounts if trying the product for the first time.
* Consume in moderation as honey is a concentrated source of natural sugars.
* Adjust serving size according to taste preference and dietary requirements.

</section>

# Recipe Ideas

<section>

### Honey Lemon Drink

**Ingredients**

* 1 glass warm water
* 1–2 teaspoons Black Seed Flower Honey
* Fresh lemon juice to taste

**Method**

1. Add honey to warm (not boiling) water.
2. Stir until dissolved.
3. Add lemon juice and serve.

### Honey Yogurt Bowl

**Ingredients**

* Plain yogurt
* 1 tablespoon Black Seed Flower Honey
* Fresh fruits
* Nuts or seeds (optional)

**Method**

1. Place yogurt in a bowl.
2. Add fruit and toppings.
3. Drizzle honey over the mixture before serving.

### Honey Oatmeal

**Ingredients**

* Cooked oats
* 1–2 teaspoons Black Seed Flower Honey
* Sliced banana or seasonal fruits

**Method**

1. Prepare oatmeal as desired.
2. Allow it to cool slightly.
3. Stir in honey and add fruit toppings.

### Simple Honey Salad Dressing

**Ingredients**

* 1 tablespoon Black Seed Flower Honey
* 2 tablespoons olive oil
* 1 tablespoon lemon juice
* Pinch of salt

**Method**

1. Whisk ingredients together.
2. Drizzle over fresh salads before serving.

### Honey Toast

**Ingredients**

* Bread or toast
* Black Seed Flower Honey

**Method**

1. Toast bread to preference.
2. Spread or drizzle honey on top.
3. Serve immediately.

</section>

# What to Avoid

<section>

### Avoid excessive heat

* Prolonged exposure to high temperatures can affect honey's flavor, aroma, and naturally occurring compounds.
* For beverages, add honey after the liquid has cooled to a comfortably warm temperature rather than boiling hot.

### Do not feed to infants under 12 months

* Honey should not be given to children younger than one year of age.

### Avoid contamination

* Always use a clean, dry spoon when handling honey.
* Avoid introducing water or food particles into the container.

### Do not refrigerate unnecessarily

* Refrigeration may accelerate crystallization.
* Store at normal room temperature in a tightly sealed container.

### Do not mistake crystallization for spoilage

* Crystallization is a natural characteristic of genuine honey.
* If preferred, place the container in warm water and stir gently to restore a more liquid consistency.

### Use moderation

* Although natural, honey is still a source of sugars and calories.
* Individuals managing blood sugar intake should consider portion sizes and seek professional dietary advice when appropriate.

</section>
