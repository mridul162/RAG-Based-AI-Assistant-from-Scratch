# Pricing
## Price drivers

The pricing of Black Seed Flower Honey is primarily determined by three critical supply-chain factors:

* **Monofloral Sourcing Constraints:** Bees must harvest nectar almost exclusively during the short blooming window of the *Nigella sativa* (black seed/kalojira) crop in Bangladesh. This limited geographical and seasonal window increases production risk and setup costs compared to polyfloral (wildflower) honeys.
* **Purity Maintenance:** Preventing cross-contamination from adjacent agricultural blooms requires strict hive placement and timing. The cost of labor to manage, harvest, and test the honey for monofloral purity contributes significantly to the base cost.
* **Supply Volatility:** While the product is kept available year-round, the yield depends entirely on the annual black seed crop success. Bad weather during the short blooming cycle dramatically spikes input costs.

## Value justification

While cheaper alternatives exist on the market, the 1,200 BDT/kg price point reflects specific quality guarantees:

* **Zero Adulteration:** The price accounts for rigorous filtration processes that remove debris without heating or introducing high-fructose corn syrup (HFCS), which is commonly used to dilute cheaper commercial honeys.
* **Traceable Origin:** 100% of the harvest is sourced from dedicated black seed farming regions within Bangladesh, ensuring direct support for local ecosystems and transparent agricultural practices.
* **Bioactive Retention:** Because the honey is kept unpasteurized, all naturally occurring enzymes, trace antioxidants, and specific pollen signatures of the black seed flower remain intact.

## Cost vs alternatives

When evaluating this product against alternative sweeteners or different honey grades, consider the following cost-to-benefit breakdown:

* **vs. Commercial Polyfloral Honey:** Commercial honey is roughly 35% cheaper but lacks the distinct medicinal profile, higher antioxidant levels, and unique herbal flavor notes derived from *Nigella sativa* nectar.
* **vs. Imported Manuka Honey:** Imported premium honeys can cost upward of 4,000 BDT to 6,000 BDT per kg due to import duties and global branding. Black Seed Flower Honey provides a comparable raw, premium bioactive profile at a fraction of the cost by sourcing locally within Bangladesh.
* **vs. Refined Sweeteners:** Refined sugar or commercial syrups are significantly cheaper but carry a far higher glycemic index and offer zero nutritional or enzymatic value.
