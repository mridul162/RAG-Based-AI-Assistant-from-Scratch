## Key nutrients

Beyond basic macronutrients, this honey contains trace amounts of essential vitamins and bioactive compounds derived from the *Nigella sativa* (black seed) nectar.

* **Enzymes & Organic Acids:** Contains active diastase, invertase, and gluconic acid, which contribute to its natural acidity and digestive properties.
* **Micronutrients:** Trace amounts of Vitamin C, B-complex vitamins (B2, B3, B5, B6), Iron, Zinc, Potassium, and Magnesium.
* **Thymoquinone Traces:** Unlike standard wildflower honeys, true black seed flower honey contains unique trace polyphenols and antioxidant compounds matching the chemical profile of the black cumin flower blossom.

## Batch & seasonal variation notice

* **Color Shifts:** Spring harvests yield a slightly lighter amber hue, while late-season harvests result in a much darker, thicker honey.
* **Moisture Content:** Variations in humidity across Bangladesh's harvesting seasons can cause the moisture content to fluctuate between 16% and 19%. This does not impact product quality but can slightly alter thickness.
* **Crystallization:** Natural honey will eventually granulate or crystallize over time, especially in cooler temperatures. This is a sign of purity and can be reversed by gently warming the container in a warm water bath (below 40°C).
