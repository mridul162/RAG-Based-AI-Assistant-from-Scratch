# Aliases
## Common names

These are the primary, widely accepted English names used for this product in standard retail and consumer environments:

* **Black Seed Flower Honey** — Standard marketing and product label name.
* **Black Seed Honey** — Common shortened consumer version.
* **Black Cumin Honey** — Often used interchangeably by consumers who refer to *Nigella sativa* as black cumin.

---

## Regional names

Because this product originates in Bangladesh and serves a multilingual customer base, users frequently query it using Bengali script or phonetic romanizations (Banglish).

### Bengali Script (বাংলা)

* **কালোজিরা ফুলের মধু** — Official regional name specifying the floral source.
* **কালোজিরা মধু** — Most common conversational name.
* **কালো জিরা মধু** — Variant spelling with a space separator.
* **কালো মধু** — Colloquial shorthand emphasizing the naturally dark color of the honey.
* **মধু** — Generic term for honey, often mapped to this specific high-priority product.

### Banglish / Romanized Bengali

* **kalojira fuler modhu** — Phonetic spelling of the full official Bengali name.
* **kalojira modhu** / **kalo jira modhu** — Common phonetic variants.
* **kalo fuler modhu** — Translates to "black flower honey".
* **kalo modhu** — Direct translation of "black honey".
* **fuler modhu** — Generic phonetic term for flower honey.

## Scientific / trade names

These terms identify the strict biological and commercial classification of the product to ensure precision in technical or regulatory customer service queries:

* **Nigella Sativa Flower Honey** — Scientific name mapping the precise monofloral source (*Nigella sativa*).
* **Nigella Honey** — Shortened botanical trade name.
* **Monofloral Black Seed Honey** — Commercial trade class denoting that the honey is harvested primarily from a single plant species rather than mixed wildflowers.
