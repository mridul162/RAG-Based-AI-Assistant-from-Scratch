## When to Choose This Product

<section id="choose-this">

Choose Black Seed Flower Honey when one or more of the following apply:

**1. You want the specific properties associated with nigella (*kalonjira*)**
This is the only honey on the comparison list that is derived from *Nigella sativa* flowers. If you are specifically seeking honey associated with black cumin for functional or traditional use, this is the correct product. No other honey listed shares this floral origin.

**2. You prefer a bold, complex flavour with a savoury edge**
The bitter-warm aftertaste of black seed flower honey makes it suitable for pairing with strong foods — whole-grain bread, aged cheese, plain yoghurt, or warm water as a morning tonic. Buyers who find standard honey too one-dimensionally sweet often prefer this profile.

**3. Year-round availability matters to you**
Unlike mustard honey (winter-only) and litchi honey (spring-only), this product is listed as available year-round. This makes it a more reliable option for regular, repeat purchase.

**4. You are buying at mid-range budget for single-origin honey**
At ৳600/kg, it is priced above everyday mustard honey but below premium Sundarbans honey. It offers a distinctive, traceable floral origin at a reasonable price point.

**5. You want a honey that is visually distinctive**
The dark near-black colour makes it easy to identify and is often preferred for gifting or when buyers want a product that looks clearly different from standard honey.

## When a Competitor May Be Better

<section id="choose-competitor">

Be straightforward with buyers about cases where an alternative is more suitable:

**Choose Mustard Flower Honey (সরিষা ফুলের মধু) if:**
- Budget is the primary concern. Mustard honey is widely available at ৳400–৳550/kg and is entirely adequate for everyday use, cooking, and sweetening.
- You prefer a lighter, milder flavour without any bitterness or spice note.
- You use large quantities regularly and cost per gram matters.
- You want honey that crystallises predictably — mustard honey's firm, white crystallisation is itself an authenticity indicator, making it somewhat harder to adulterate convincingly.

**Choose Litchi Flower Honey (লিচু ফুলের মধু) if:**
- You want a delicate, fragrant honey primarily for tea, light desserts, or gifting.
- The recipient or user dislikes any bitterness or strong aftertaste.
- You are buying for children (over 12 months) or people who are new to honey and may be put off by strong flavour profiles.

**Choose Sundarbans Forest Honey (সুন্দরবনের মধু) if:**
- You specifically want wild-foraged, multi-flora honey from a geographically protected forest ecosystem.
- Premium provenance and uniqueness are more important than price.
- You prefer a very thick, dense texture and an earthy, complex flavour profile.
- *Caveat:* Verify origin documentation carefully — the "Sundarbans" label is one of the most commonly misappropriated names in the Bangladeshi honey market. Only buy from suppliers who can demonstrate sourcing credentials.

---

> **General note on all honey comparisons:** Floral origin is the primary quality differentiator in single-origin honey, not brand. A well-harvested, correctly stored mustard honey is better than a poorly handled or adulterated black seed honey. Authenticity (see `authenticity.md`) matters more than the label.
