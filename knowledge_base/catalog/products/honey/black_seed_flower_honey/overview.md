# Overview

Black Seed Flower Honey (Kalojira Flower Honey) is a variety of honey produced primarily from the nectar of flowers of the black seed plant (*Nigella sativa*). It is valued for its distinctive flavor profile, characteristic aroma, and association with one of the most widely cultivated spice crops in Bangladesh. During the flowering season, beekeepers place honey bee colonies near black seed fields, allowing bees to collect nectar predominantly from these blossoms.

This honey typically ranges in color from light amber to dark amber, depending on factors such as nectar composition, regional growing conditions, and harvest period. Some batches may appear darker than common floral honeys, which has led to colloquial names such as "black honey" among consumers. Like all natural honey, it may crystallize over time without affecting its quality or authenticity.

Black Seed Flower Honey is known for its rich floral aroma accompanied by mild herbal and slightly spicy notes. The taste is moderately sweet, often with a deeper and more complex flavor than many multifloral honeys. Its unique sensory profile makes it easily distinguishable from common wildflower or mixed-flower honey varieties.

## Origin & Background

Black seed (*Nigella sativa*) has been cultivated for centuries across South Asia, the Middle East, and North Africa as a culinary and agricultural crop. In Bangladesh, black seed is grown in various regions, providing a valuable nectar source for honey bees during its flowering period.

The production of Black Seed Flower Honey depends on the flowering cycle of the crop. To obtain honey with a strong black seed floral character, beekeepers strategically position apiaries near flowering fields. Environmental conditions such as soil quality, climate, rainfall, and nectar flow can influence the final color, aroma, and taste of each harvest. As a result, slight seasonal variations are considered normal in authentic honey.

## Why It Matters

Black Seed Flower Honey stands out because of its identifiable floral source and distinctive sensory characteristics. Unlike generic multifloral honey, it offers a more specific origin profile that appeals to consumers interested in monofloral honey varieties.

Its balanced sweetness, floral aroma, and subtle herbal complexity make it suitable for direct consumption, mixing into beverages, or use as a natural sweetener in everyday diets. For consumers seeking honey with a recognizable botanical origin and unique flavor profile, Black Seed Flower Honey represents a distinctive and authentic choice among Bangladeshi honey varieties.
