# Storage
## Ideal conditions

To preserve the nutritional profile, enzymatic activity, and flavor of raw Black Seed Flower Honey, store it according to these environmental parameters:

* **Temperature:** Store at room temperature between **15°C and 25°C**. Avoid exposure to temperatures above **40°C**, as high heat destroys beneficial enzymes and antioxidants. Do not refrigerate; cold temperatures accelerate crystallization (granulation).
* **Light:** Keep the container in a dark place, such as a pantry or cupboard. Direct sunlight and UV rays degrade the natural bioactive compounds over time.
* **Humidity:** Store in a dry environment. Because honey is highly hygroscopic (it absorbs moisture from the air), it must be kept away from high-humidity areas when the container is open.

---

## Container recommendations

The packaging material affects the chemical stability and shelf life of the honey:

* **Best Material:** Glass or high-density polyethylene (HDPE) plastic containers. Glass is chemically inert and prevents any gas exchange or chemical leaching.
* **Seal Integrity:** Always use an airtight, screw-top lid. Exposure to open air allows the honey to absorb moisture, raising its water content and increasing the risk of fermentation.
* **Avoid Metals:** Never store honey long-term in bare metal containers (especially iron, copper, or aluminum). The natural acidity of honey (typically pH 3.5 to 4.5) can react with metal surfaces, causing corrosion and altering the taste.

---

## Shelf life

Pure, low-moisture honey technically does not inspire a hard expiration date, but commercial and nutritional guidance limits are as follows:

| Storage State | Recommended Shelf Life | Guidance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Unopened** | 2 Years (24 Months) | Maintains peak flavor and enzyme activity when stored under ideal conditions. |
| **Opened** | 1 Year (12 Months) | Best consumed within 12 months due to frequent exposure to ambient air moisture and potential cross-contamination from utensils. |

---

## Signs of spoilage

While raw honey is a natural preservative, environmental contamination or high moisture content can cause degradation. Watch for these distinct signs:

* **Fermentation (Spoilage):** If the moisture content exceeds 19% due to improper sealing, airborne yeasts will ferment the sugars. Signs include a sharp, sour, beer-like odor, a bubbling or foaming surface, and a bulging lid. **Do not consume fermented honey.**
* **Severe Discoloration & Taste Change:** Natural honey darkens and loses its distinct herbal aroma over several years. If the honey turns completely black, emits a burnt or metallic smell, or tastes distinctly bitter, it has degraded due to heat damage or age.
* **Note on Crystallization (Not Spoilage):** The appearance of white, cloudy, solid crystals or a completely solidified texture is a natural physical process, not a sign of spoilage. To liquefy crystallized honey, place the tightly sealed jar in a warm water bath (maximum 40°C) until the crystals dissolve. Do not microwave.
