# Shipping
## Temperature requirements

* **Heat Sensitivity:** Honey must not be exposed to temperatures exceeding **40°C** during transit. Prolonged exposure to high heat degrades the natural enzymes, antioxidants, and darkens the color of Black Seed Flower Honey. Courier dispatch should avoid extended holding periods in non-climate-controlled sorting hubs during summer months in Bangladesh.
* **Cold Exposure:** While low temperatures do not spoil the honey, shipping in extremely cold conditions or via refrigerated freight will accelerate natural crystallization (solidification).
* **Seasonal Restrictions:** There are no seasonal shipping freezes since the product is stable year-round, but thermal protective wrap is recommended during peak summer transits.

---

## Packaging notes

To prevent transit failure, leakage, and breakage of heavy glass or rigid plastic honey containers, shipments must adhere to the following configurations:

* **Leak Prevention:** Every jar requires an induction-sealed inner foil liner beneath the screw cap. Lids must be secured with pressure-sensitive tamper-evident tape to prevent loosening from vibration.
* **Fragility Cushioning:** If packaged in glass, jars must be individually wrapped in a minimum of two layers of bubble wrap (or air-column bags) and isolated from each other using double-wall corrugated cardboard dividers.
* **Orientation Labeling:** Shipping cartons must feature prominent "Liquid", "Fragile", and "This Side Up" directional arrows on at least two opposing vertical faces.

## Regulatory considerations

* **Domestic Classification:** Within Bangladesh, this product moves under standard domestic "Liquid/Food Grade" freight guidelines. It is not classified as hazardous material (Hazmat).
* **Courier Restrictions:** Certain local point-to-point courier networks limit single-package liquid weights to 5kg. Orders exceeding this threshold must be split across multiple consignments or shifted to dedicated logistics partners.

---

## International shipping

Shipping Black Seed Flower Honey outside of Bangladesh requires strict compliance with international biosecurity laws, as honey is classified as an animal product/byproduct.

* **Import Restrictions:** Countries such as Australia, New Zealand, and certain regions within the European Union have severe restrictions or outright bans on inbound personal postal shipments of raw honey to protect local bee populations from pathogens.
* **Required Documentation:** Commercial export shipments require:
* Certificate of Origin (confirming Bangladesh origin).
* Phytosanitary Certificate issued by the Ministry of Agriculture.
* Laboratory analysis report confirming moisture content (under 19%) and monofloral pollen verification.


* **US Market (FDA):** Shipments to the United States require a Prior Notice filing with the US Food and Drug Administration (FDA) and must originate from an FDA-registered facility.
