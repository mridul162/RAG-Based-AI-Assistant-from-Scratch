## Key Nutrients

### Glycemic Index (GI)

Lychee Flower Honey has a **Moderate Glycemic Index (typically ranging between 55 to 58)**. Because it features a higher fructose-to-glucose ratio compared to standard commercial multi-flower honeys, it is absorbed slightly slower by the body, preventing rapid blood sugar spikes. However, individuals with diabetes should still manage consumption carefully.

### Antioxidants and Enzymes

* **Flavonoids & Phenolic Acids:** Rich in floral-derived antioxidants that help neutralize free radicals.
* **Active Enzymes:** Contains trace amounts of diastase, invertase, and glucose oxidase, introduced naturally by the bees during honey maturation.

### Micronutrients

Contains trace amounts of essential vitamins and minerals, including **Vitamin C** (influenced by the parent lychee fruit blossoms), **Iron**, **Zinc**, and **Magnesium**.

> ### ⚠️ Important Notice for Customers

> 
> * **Infant Warning:** Do not feed honey to infants under 12 months of age due to the risk of infant botulism, a rare but serious form of food poisoning caused by naturally occurring *Clostridium botulinum* spores.
> * **Crystallization:** Raw honey may slowly crystallize over time or when exposed to cold temperatures. This is a natural attribute of pure honey and does not affect its nutritional value or safety. To liquefy, place the jar in warm water (under 40°C).
