# Usage
## Best uses

<section>

Lychee Flower Honey has a mild sweetness and delicate floral-fruity aroma, making it a versatile honey for both direct consumption and culinary use.

### Direct consumption

* Enjoy 1–2 teaspoons directly from the spoon.
* Suitable as a simple daily sweetener for those who enjoy natural honey.

### In beverages

* Stir into warm water for a naturally sweet drink.
* Add to tea, herbal infusions, or lemon water.
* Mix into milk, smoothies, or fruit-based drinks.

### As a natural sweetener

* Use in place of refined sugar in beverages and recipes.
* Suitable for sweetening yogurt, oats, breakfast cereals, and porridge.

### As a topping

* Drizzle over toast, pancakes, waffles, or flatbreads.
* Pair with fresh fruits such as bananas, apples, oranges, or seasonal berries.

### With dairy products

* Mix into plain yogurt or Greek yogurt.
* Combine with cottage cheese or soft cheese for a mildly sweet snack.

</section>

## Dosage / serving suggestions

<section>

<table>

| Usage               | Suggested Serving                                 |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- |
| Direct consumption  | 1–2 teaspoons (5–10 g)                            |
| Warm water or tea   | 1–2 teaspoons per cup                             |
| Yogurt or oats      | 1 tablespoon (10–15 g)                            |
| Toast or pancakes   | 1–2 tablespoons, as desired                       |
| Smoothies           | 1 tablespoon per serving                          |
| Baking and desserts | Adjust according to recipe sweetness requirements |

</table>

### General guidance

* Consume according to personal dietary needs and preferences.
* Because honey is a natural source of sugars, moderation is recommended as part of a balanced diet.
* Individuals managing sugar intake should consider honey within their overall nutrition plan.

</section>

## Recipe ideas

<section>

### Honey Lemon Drink

**Ingredients**

* 1 cup warm water
* 1–2 teaspoons Lychee Flower Honey
* Fresh lemon juice

**Method**

1. Add honey to warm (not boiling) water.
2. Stir until dissolved.
3. Add lemon juice and serve.

### Honey Yogurt Bowl

**Ingredients**

* Plain yogurt
* 1 tablespoon Lychee Flower Honey
* Fresh fruit slices
* Optional nuts or seeds

**Method**

1. Add fruit to yogurt.
2. Drizzle honey over the top.
3. Add nuts or seeds if desired.

### Fruit and Honey Salad

**Ingredients**

* Mixed seasonal fruits
* 1–2 tablespoons Lychee Flower Honey
* Optional mint leaves

**Method**

1. Combine chopped fruits.
2. Drizzle honey evenly.
3. Garnish with mint and serve.

### Honey Oatmeal

**Ingredients**

* Cooked oats
* 1 tablespoon Lychee Flower Honey
* Fruits or nuts

**Method**

1. Prepare oatmeal as usual.
2. Allow it to cool slightly.
3. Stir in honey and add toppings.

</section>

## What to avoid

<section>

### Do not give to infants under 12 months

* Honey should not be fed to babies under one year of age.

### Avoid excessive heat

* For best flavor and aroma retention, avoid adding honey to boiling liquids.
* Allow beverages to cool slightly before mixing in honey.

### Do not refrigerate unnecessarily

* Store in a cool, dry place at room temperature.
* Refrigeration may accelerate crystallization.

### Do not mistake crystallization for spoilage

* Pure honey may naturally crystallize over time.
* Crystallized honey remains safe to consume and can be gently liquefied by placing the container in warm water.

### Avoid contamination

* Always use a clean, dry spoon.
* Keep the container tightly closed after use to help maintain quality.

</section>
