# Storage
# Lychee Flower Honey (লিচু ফুলের মধু) - Storage and Shelf Life Guide

This document provides definitive instructions for the storage and preservation of Pure Lychee Flower Honey. Following these guidelines ensures the honey retains its distinct floral aroma, light color, and active enzyme profile while preventing premature crystallization or moisture-driven fermentation.

---

## Ideal Conditions

Honey is naturally hygroscopic, meaning it readily absorbs moisture from the surrounding environment. In a high-humidity climate like Bangladesh, maintaining correct storage conditions is critical.

* **Temperature:** Store at room temperature between **18°C and 25°C**. Avoid storing next to heat-generating kitchen appliances (stoves, ovens) or in direct sunlight, as prolonged heat degrades the honey's natural enzymes and darkens its color.
* **Do Not Refrigerate:** Refrigeration speeds up the natural process of crystallization, turning the liquid honey into a thick, semi-solid grainy texture. While it does not spoil crystallized honey, it changes the user experience.
* **Humidity Control:** Keep the environment dry. Always seal the lid tightly immediately after use to prevent the honey from drawing water vapor out of the air.

---

## Container Recommendations

The type of packaging significantly influences the longevity and purity of raw honey.

* **Best Choice:** High-quality food-grade glass jars or airtight premium PET/HDPE plastic containers. Glass is completely non-reactive and serves as an excellent barrier against moisture and outside odors.
* **Avoid Metal:** Never store raw honey in unlined metal containers for extended periods. The natural organic acids present in honey can react with metals, causing corrosion and altering the taste and safety of the product.
* **Utensil Care:** Always use a completely clean, **dry spoon** (wood, plastic, or stainless steel) when scooping honey. Introducing even a few drops of water or food crumbs can introduce wild yeasts, triggering spoilage.

---

## Shelf Life

Pure, properly stored Lychee Flower Honey possesses a remarkably long shelf life due to its low moisture content and natural acidity.

| Packaging State | Recommended Shelf Life | Guidance |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Unopened Jar** | 2 to 3 Years | Stored in a cool, dark pantry away from sunlight. Flavor and aroma remain optimal. |
| **Opened Jar** | 1 to 2 Years | Dependent on strict adherence to clean handling practices and immediate, tight resealing after each use. |

> **Note on Crystallization vs. Expiry:** Crystallization is a natural physical shift, not a chemical expiration. Lychee honey has a high fructose-to-glucose ratio, making it highly stable and resistant to crystallization for many months compared to mustard honey. If it does freeze or crystallize, place the container in a warm water bath (below 40°C) to restore its liquid state.

## Signs of Spoilage

While pure honey can technically last indefinitely under laboratory conditions, improper handling in home environments can lead to degradation or microbial spoilage.

### 1. Fermentation (Moisture Level > 20%)

If the honey absorbs excess ambient moisture from a loose lid or a wet spoon, naturally occurring wild yeasts will wake up and ferment the sugars.

* **Visual Signs:** Rapidly forming bubbles, a layer of foam on the surface, or an expanding volume that pushes against the lid.
* **Odor/Taste:** A sharp, sour, alcoholic, or yeast-like smell replacing the pleasant fruity-floral aroma.

### 2. Flavor and Heat Degradation

* **Visual Signs:** Extreme darkening of the honey from golden-light to deep brown, accompanied by complete separation into distinct watery and thick layers.
* **Odor/Taste:** A bitter, burnt, or metallic taste caused by high Hydroxymethylfurfural (HMF) accumulation from excessive heat exposure.

> ⚠️ **Actionable Rule:** If the honey exhibits signs of fermentation (sour odor, active bubbling, or foaming), it has spoiled and must be discarded. Do not consume fermented honey.
