### 1. What exactly is Lychee Flower Honey? Is it different from regular honey?

Yes. Lychee Flower Honey (লিচু ফুলের মধু) is a **single-floral honey**, meaning bees collect nectar primarily from lychee (*Litchi chinensis*) blossoms during the lychee flowering season. This gives it a distinctly light, mildly fruity, and floral flavour profile that is noticeably different from multi-floral or forest honeys. Regular or "mixed flower" honey is collected from various floral sources throughout the year and tends to have a more generic, sometimes stronger taste.

This honey is a natural product collected from lychee orchards in Bangladesh. Unless explicitly stated otherwise on your order or packaging, it has not been heavily processed or heat-treated. However, "raw" is not a certified standard in Bangladesh — if this matters to you, ask the seller directly about their post-harvest handling process (filtering, heating, packaging temperature).

### 3. How can I tell if the honey I received is genuine lychee flower honey and not adulterated?

Genuine lychee flower honey has a few recognisable characteristics:

- **Colour:** Light golden to pale amber. It should not be deep brown or opaque.
- **Aroma:** Delicate, lightly fruity and floral — not sharp, pungent, or chemically sweet.
- **Taste:** Mild and clean sweetness with a subtle floral finish. It should not taste excessively sweet, caramel-like, or artificial.
- **Texture:** Smooth and moderately viscous. It may crystallise over time — this is normal and actually a sign of natural sugar content.
- **Crystallisation:** Lychee honey crystallises relatively slowly compared to mustard honey. If a honey marketed as lychee flower honey crystallises within days, it may be mixed with mustard honey.

No home test is fully conclusive. Adulteration in the Bangladesh honey market is a known issue. Sourcing from a traceable, reputable seller is the most practical safeguard.

### 4. Why is this honey only available seasonally?

Lychee trees bloom for a short window — typically **March to May** in Bangladesh, depending on the year and region. Bees can only collect lychee nectar during active bloom. Once the flowers fall, that season's harvest is complete. This makes lychee flower honey inherently limited in volume and unavailable outside its harvest window unless stored stock from the previous season remains. Pricing and availability may shift as seasonal stock depletes.

### 6. My honey has turned cloudy or solid. Has it gone bad?

No. **Crystallisation is a natural process** in pure honey and is generally a positive sign — heavily adulterated or sugar-syrup-based products often do not crystallise properly. Lychee flower honey can crystallise over time, especially in cooler temperatures. To reliquefy it, place the sealed jar in a bowl of warm (not boiling) water and stir gently. Do not microwave directly or heat above ~40°C, as this can degrade beneficial enzymes and alter flavour.

### 7. What is the shelf life of this honey?

Properly stored honey has an exceptionally long shelf life. **Under standard conditions (sealed jar, cool and dry location, away from direct sunlight), this honey can last 12–24 months** without significant quality loss. Honey does not truly "expire" in the conventional sense, but flavour and aroma can fade over time. Discard if it smells fermented, shows visible mould, or has an off taste — which can occur if moisture has entered the jar.

---

### 8. How should I store this honey?

- Keep in a **tightly sealed container** at all times.
- Store at **room temperature** in a cool, dry place — a kitchen cupboard away from the stove is ideal.
- **Avoid refrigeration** — cold temperatures accelerate crystallisation without any preservation benefit.
- Keep away from **direct sunlight** and heat sources.
- Always use a **clean, dry spoon** — introducing moisture or food particles can promote fermentation.

### 9. Can I give this honey to my baby or toddler?

**No. Honey of any kind must not be given to children under 12 months of age.** All natural honey — including this product — may contain *Clostridium botulinum* spores, which can cause infant botulism in babies whose digestive systems are not yet mature enough to neutralise them. This is a serious, potentially life-threatening condition. For children aged 1–3 years, consult your paediatrician before introducing honey into the diet. See the `warnings.md` file for full safety details.

### 10. I have diabetes. Can I consume this honey?

Not without medical guidance. Honey is high in natural sugars — primarily fructose and glucose — and **will raise blood glucose levels**. It is not a safe substitute for sugar in diabetic diets without accounting for its glycaemic impact. If you have Type 1, Type 2, or gestational diabetes, speak to your physician or a dietitian before consuming this or any honey product.

### 11. What are the health benefits of lychee flower honey?

Like all natural honeys, lychee flower honey contains natural sugars, trace enzymes, small amounts of antioxidants, and minor quantities of vitamins and minerals. Some general benefits associated with natural honey include use as a natural sweetener, soothing properties for mild sore throats, and antioxidant content.

**However, there are no verified health claims specific to lychee flower honey** that are backed by clinical research. Be cautious of sellers making strong medicinal claims (e.g. curing diseases, guaranteed immune-boosting). Honey is a food product, not a medicine. For therapeutic use, consult a qualified health professional.

### 12. Can I use this honey for cooking or baking?

Yes, with some caveats. Lychee flower honey's **mild, delicate flavour makes it well-suited for uses where you do not want a strong honey taste** — such as in teas, salad dressings, yogurt, smoothies, or light marinades. For high-heat baking or cooking above ~150°C, the delicate floral notes will largely cook off, and a cheaper general-purpose honey would give similar results. Avoid boiling honey directly or adding it to very hot liquids, as high temperatures reduce its nutritional content.
