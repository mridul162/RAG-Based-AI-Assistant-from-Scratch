# Sourcing
## Geographic origin

<section>

Lychee Flower Honey is sourced from lychee-growing regions of Bangladesh, where large numbers of lychee trees bloom during a short seasonal flowering period. Beekeepers place managed bee colonies near flowering orchards so that honey bees can collect nectar primarily from lychee blossoms.

The exact sourcing region may vary from season to season depending on flowering conditions, orchard availability, and beekeeper operations. Common lychee-producing areas in Bangladesh include districts known for commercial lychee cultivation, where extensive orchards provide a concentrated nectar source during bloom.

Because the honey is linked to a specific flowering crop, its characteristics are influenced by local climate, soil conditions, rainfall patterns, and the overall health of the flowering season.

</section>

## Harvest method

<section>

### Nectar collection

1. Honey bees forage on blooming lychee flowers and collect nectar.
2. The nectar is transported back to the hive.
3. Bees naturally process and store the nectar in honeycomb cells, where it gradually becomes honey through enzymatic activity and moisture reduction.

### Honey harvesting

1. Beekeepers monitor hive development throughout the flowering season.
2. Once honey-filled combs are sufficiently capped by the bees, the frames are removed from the hive.
3. Honey is extracted from the combs using mechanical extraction methods that preserve the comb structure for future use.
4. The extracted honey is filtered to remove wax particles and other natural debris.
5. The honey is then stored, packed, and prepared for distribution.

### Processing approach

* Typically minimally processed to retain its natural characteristics.
* No artificial flavors, colors, or fragrances are added.
* Natural variation in color, aroma, and texture may occur between harvests.

</section>

## Season & timing

<section>

| Floral source     | Lychee (Litchi) blossoms                                        |
| Harvest type      | Seasonal                                                        |

| Availability      | Limited to annual harvest cycles                                |

Lychee Flower Honey is considered a seasonal honey because lychee trees bloom for only a short period each year. The quantity and quality of the harvest can vary depending on environmental conditions such as rainfall, temperature, and flowering intensity.

As a result, supply is often limited to the honey collected during that year's bloom, making fresh seasonal batches particularly valued by consumers.

</section>

## Supply chain

<section>

### From orchard to hive

* Bee colonies are positioned near flowering lychee orchards.
* Bees collect nectar directly from the blossoms during the flowering season.

### From hive to extraction

* Mature honey frames are harvested by beekeepers.
* Honey is extracted, filtered, and prepared for storage.

### From processing to packaging

* Honey is transferred into food-grade storage containers.
* It is packaged into retail sizes suitable for consumer purchase.
* Packaging is designed to protect the honey from moisture and contamination.

### From supplier to consumer

* Finished products are distributed through retailers, online stores, and direct sales channels.
* Consumers receive honey that reflects the characteristics of the seasonal lychee blossom harvest.

### Sustainability and ethical considerations

* Responsible beekeeping practices help support pollination of flowering crops and surrounding vegetation.
* Harvesting honey from managed colonies allows bees to continue their natural pollination activities while producing harvestable honey.
* Sustainable sourcing depends on maintaining healthy bee colonies, responsible hive management, and minimizing unnecessary disturbance to the bees during harvesting.

</section>
