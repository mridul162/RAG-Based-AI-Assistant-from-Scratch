## Overview

Sundarbans Kholisha Flower Honey is a wild, single-source honey collected from the Kholisha plant (*Aegiceras corniculatum*) — a mangrove shrub that grows along the tidal creeks of the Sundarbans, the world's largest mangrove forest, right here in Bangladesh. It is not farmed. Bees forage freely across the mangrove ecosystem, and the honey they produce reflects exactly where it comes from.

## Origin & Background

The Sundarbans spans the southern delta of Bangladesh and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a Ramsar Wetland — one of the most ecologically protected areas in the world. The Kholisha plant blooms for a short window each year, roughly February to April, which is when traditional honey collectors called **Mouwalis** enter the forest by boat under seasonal permits from the Bangladesh Forest Department.

Collection is done by hand from wild hives, using smoke to calm the bees. It is skilled, physically demanding, and not without risk — the forest is home to Bengal tigers and saltwater crocodiles. The honey that reaches your family carries the effort and knowledge of communities who have worked this forest for generations.

Because bees in the Sundarbans feed on a relatively consistent and contained set of mangrove plants, the honey has a more uniform and recognisable flavour compared to mixed wildflower honeys from open farmland.
