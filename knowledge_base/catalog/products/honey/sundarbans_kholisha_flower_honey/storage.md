# Storage — Sundarbans Kholisha Flower Honey

*Raw honey is one of the easiest foods to store — but a few simple habits make a real difference in keeping it fresh and safe for your family.*

## Ideal Conditions

<table>
<thead><tr><th>Factor</th><th>Recommendation</th><th>Why It Matters</th></tr></thead>

<tr><td>Temperature</td><td>Room temperature — ideally 20°C to 30°C</td><td>Keeps honey at a stable, pourable consistency. Bangladesh's ambient indoor temperature is generally fine year-round.</td></tr>

<tr><td>Sunlight</td><td>Keep away from direct sunlight</td><td>UV exposure can break down naturally occurring enzymes and alter colour and flavour.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Humidity</td><td>Dry environment only</td><td>Honey is hygroscopic — it absorbs moisture from the air. High humidity raises the moisture content of the honey, which increases the risk of fermentation.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Refrigeration</td><td>Not recommended</td><td>Cold temperatures accelerate crystallisation significantly and do not extend shelf life. Refrigerating raw honey is unnecessary and makes it harder to use.</td></tr>

**One practical tip for Bangladeshi households:** During the hot and humid monsoon months (June–September), make sure the jar lid is tightly sealed after every use. Humidity is at its highest during this period and leaving the lid loosely on invites moisture in.

<section id="container-recommendations">

## Container Recommendations

**Glass is the best container for honey** — it is non-reactive, does not absorb odours, and keeps honey in its natural state indefinitely. If your Hasanah Mart order arrives in a glass jar, we recommend keeping it in that same jar until it is finished.

If you need to transfer honey to another container:

- **Food-grade glass jars** with tight-fitting lids are ideal. Old jam jars work well if washed thoroughly and dried completely before use.
- **Food-grade plastic** (labelled HDPE or marked with recycling codes 2 or 5) is acceptable for short-term storage. Avoid thin or low-quality plastic — honey's mild acidity can interact with poor-quality plastic over time.
- **Stainless steel** is acceptable for short-term use, such as a serving container on the table.
- **Avoid** iron, copper, zinc, or other reactive metals entirely. Honey's natural acidity will cause slow leaching from these materials.

**Always use a clean, dry spoon** when taking honey from the jar. A wet spoon introduces water directly into the honey, which raises the local moisture level and can trigger fermentation in that spot over time.

**On crystallisation:** Kholisha honey will crystallise naturally over time — typically within several months to a year of harvest, depending on storage temperature. This is not spoilage. Crystallised honey is safe to eat and has the same flavour. To return it to liquid form, place the sealed jar in a bowl of warm water (not boiling — keep below 40°C) for 15–20 minutes, stirring occasionally if needed. Do not microwave.

## Signs of Spoilage

Raw honey rarely spoils when stored correctly, but it is important to know what to look for. Here are the signs that something has gone wrong.

**Do not consume the honey if you notice:**

- **Sour or fermented smell** — a sharp, yeasty, or alcoholic odour means fermentation has occurred, almost always caused by excess moisture entering the jar. This is the most important warning sign.
- **Fizzing or active bubbling inside the jar** — a sign of active fermentation. This is different from the thin natural foam that may appear on fresh raw honey when first opened.
- **Sour, sharp, or off taste** — if it tastes wrong, trust that. Pure honey should taste clean and mildly sweet.
- **Visible mould** — extremely rare in properly stored honey but possible if significant water has been introduced. If you see any growth on the surface, discard the entire jar.
- **Very watery texture with no viscosity** — if honey that was previously thick becomes noticeably thin and runny without being warmed, moisture contamination is likely.

**What is NOT a sign of spoilage:**

- Crystallisation — completely normal, as described above
- Colour darkening over time — natural and harmless
- A thin layer of foam or bubbles on the surface when first opened — normal in fresh raw honey
- Slight variation in colour between batches — expected in wild-harvested honey
