# Sundarbans Kholisha Flower Honey — Usage

## Best uses

### Daily sweetener
Use Kholisha flower honey as a natural sweetener in tea, warm water, milk, coffee, or homemade drinks.

### Breakfast pairing
Pairs well with:
- Bread and toast
- Paratha and ruti
- Pancakes and waffles
- Oats and cereal
- Yogurt and fruits

### Traditional food use
Commonly enjoyed with:
- Chitoi pitha
- Ruti
- Semai
- Homemade desserts
- Lemon water

### Dessert topping
Can be drizzled over:
- Fruit bowls
- Ice cream
- Cakes
- Custards
- Smoothie bowls

### Homemade drinks
Useful for preparing:
- Honey lemon water
- Warm honey tea
- Ginger honey drinks
- Cold summer drinks

---

## Dosage / serving suggestions

| Usage type | Suggested amount |
|---|---|
| Tea or warm water | 1 to 2 teaspoons |
| Toast or breakfast foods | 1 to 2 tablespoons |
| Smoothies or yogurt | 1 tablespoon |
| Homemade desserts | Adjust to taste |
| Family recipes | Use as needed |

### Everyday serving guidance
- Usually consumed in small to moderate amounts as part of regular meals.
- Stir well before use if natural settling occurs.
- Use a clean, dry spoon to keep the honey fresh.

---

## Recipe ideas

### Honey lemon drink
Mix:
- 1 glass of warm water
- 1 teaspoon honey
- Fresh lemon juice

### Honey yogurt bowl
Add honey to:
- Plain yogurt
- Banana slices
- Seasonal fruits
- Oats or nuts

### Honey milk drink
Mix:
- Warm milk
- 1 teaspoon honey

### Honey toast
Spread honey over:
- Toasted bread
- Butter toast
- Paratha

### Homemade salad dressing
Mix honey with:
- Lemon juice
- Olive oil
- A small pinch of salt

---

## What to avoid

- Avoid using a wet spoon inside the jar.
- Do not store the honey in direct sunlight or near high heat.
- Avoid overheating honey during cooking for long periods.
- Keep the lid tightly closed after use.
- Honey is not recommended for infants under 12 months old.
