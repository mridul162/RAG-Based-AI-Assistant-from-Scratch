<section id="price-drivers">

## Price Drivers

Authentic Sundarbans Kholisha honey costs more than generic supermarket honey for real, structural reasons — not branding.

<table>
<thead><tr><th>Cost Factor</th><th>Why It Matters</th></tr></thead>
<tbody>
<tr><td>Forest access permits</td><td>Collectors (Mouwalis) must purchase seasonal permits from the Bangladesh Forest Department before entering the Sundarbans. These are limited and add a direct cost before any honey is collected.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Dangerous collection work</td><td>Mouwalis travel deep into the forest by boat and collect from wild hives in an active tiger and crocodile habitat. That occupational risk is reflected in fair collector earnings.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Short harvest window</td><td>Kholisha flowers bloom for only 6–8 weeks a year, between February and April. The entire year's supply comes from this one window — there is no way to produce more if demand increases.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Low, unpredictable yield</td><td>Wild collection depends entirely on what nature provides each season. Collectors cannot increase output by adding managed hives or supplemental feeding.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Transport from a remote location</td><td>Honey travels by boat from deep forest collection points to depots in Mongla or Khulna, then overland to Narayanganj. Each step carries a real logistics cost.</td></tr>
<tr><td>Raw handling</td><td>We source honey that has not been heated or pasteurised. Keeping it raw requires more careful storage and faster stock turnover — both cost more than heat-treating the product to artificially extend shelf life.</td></tr>
</tbody>
</table>

## Value Justification

We understand ৳320 for a 250g jar feels like more than you'd pay for honey at a local shop. Here is what that price gets your family — plainly stated.

**What you get with Hasanah Mart's Kholisha honey:**

- **One clear floral source.** Your honey comes from Kholisha bloom specifically, not a blend of whatever was cheapest to collect that season.
- **Raw and unheated.** Naturally occurring enzymes and pollen are intact. Most commercial honey is pasteurised, which destroys these.
- **No added sugars or fillers.** Cheap honey is frequently cut with sugar syrup, rice syrup, or glucose solution. What we sell is just honey.
- **A traceable origin.** In a market where adulteration is widespread, knowing your honey came from a real, documented source in the Sundarbans has genuine value.

**What it is not:**

Honey is food, not medicine. We will not make health claims we cannot back up. What we can say honestly is that your family is getting a clean, natural, minimally processed food product from one of Bangladesh's most protected ecosystems — and that is worth a fair price.
