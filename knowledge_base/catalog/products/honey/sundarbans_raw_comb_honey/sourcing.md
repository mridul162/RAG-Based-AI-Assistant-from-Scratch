# Sourcing
## Geographic origin

<section>

**Sundarbans Raw Comb Honey (সুন্দরবনের চাকের মধু)** originates from the Sundarbans, the world's largest mangrove forest, located in the southwestern coastal region of Bangladesh.

The honey is produced by wild honeybee colonies that build natural hives throughout the forest ecosystem. Bees forage on nectar from a diverse range of mangrove and coastal flowering plants, giving Sundarbans honey its distinctive flavor, aroma, and character.

Key characteristics of the sourcing region:

* Origin: Sundarbans Mangrove Forest, Bangladesh
* Ecosystem: Natural mangrove and coastal forest
* Honey source: Wild bee colonies rather than managed apiaries
* Floral diversity: Mixed seasonal nectar sources from forest vegetation
* Product type: Wild-harvested raw comb honey

Because the honey comes from a natural forest environment rather than commercial beekeeping farms, each harvest may vary slightly in color, taste, aroma, and texture.

</section>

## Harvest method

<section>

### Traditional wild honey collection

Sundarbans Raw Comb Honey is collected by experienced **Moualis** (traditional honey collectors) who enter designated forest areas during the annual honey harvesting season.

The general harvesting process includes:

1. Locating active wild bee colonies within the forest.
2. Carefully accessing the hive while minimizing disturbance.
3. Removing selected portions of the honey-filled comb.
4. Transporting the comb from the forest to collection points.
5. Sorting and preparing comb sections for distribution.

### Minimal processing

Unlike extracted honey, raw comb honey retains the original honeycomb structure.

The product is typically:

* Harvested directly from natural hives
* Cut into comb sections
* Minimally processed
* Packaged with the comb intact

As a result, the honey may naturally contain:

* Beeswax
* Bee pollen traces
* Propolis traces
* Naturally occurring particles associated with raw honey

No artificial flavoring, coloring, or sweeteners are added during sourcing.

</section>

### Limited supply

Raw comb honey is generally available in smaller quantities than extracted honey because:

* Only selected comb sections are harvested.
* Wild hive availability is limited.
* Harvesting is restricted to specific seasons.
* Collection requires specialized skills and access to forest areas.

## Supply chain

<section>

### From forest to consumer

The sourcing and distribution chain generally follows these stages:

1. **Wild bee colonies** produce honey within natural comb structures.
2. **Moualis** harvest comb sections during the approved collection season.
3. Honeycomb is transported from forest collection points.
4. Comb sections are inspected and sorted.
5. The raw comb honey is packaged with minimal handling.
6. Finished products are distributed to retailers and consumers.

### Quality preservation

To maintain authenticity and freshness:

* Honeycomb is handled carefully to preserve its natural structure.
* Excessive heating is avoided.
* Minimal processing is performed.
* Packaging is designed to protect the comb during storage and transport.

### Sustainability considerations

Responsible sourcing aims to balance honey collection with long-term forest conservation and sustainable use of natural resources.

Key principles may include:

* Harvesting during approved collection periods
* Following local forest regulations
* Avoiding unnecessary destruction of bee colonies
* Supporting traditional livelihoods of Mouali communities
* Encouraging continued regeneration of natural bee habitats

As sourcing conditions can vary between harvests and suppliers, specific sustainability practices may differ from one collection season to another.

</section>

> **Note:** Sundarbans Raw Comb Honey is a naturally harvested seasonal product. Variations in color, flavor, aroma, comb structure, and honey consistency are normal characteristics of authentic wild forest honey and do not indicate quality issues.
