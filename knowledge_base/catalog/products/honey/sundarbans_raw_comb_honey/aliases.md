## Regional Names

These variants capture specific colloquialisms, phonetic spelling variations, and phonetic English-script renderings (Banglish) used regionally in Bangladesh.

### Bengali Script Regionalisms

* **চাক কাটা মধু:** Literally "honey cut directly from the hive," emphasizing traditional extraction methods.
* **প্রাকৃতিক চাকের মধু:** "Natural hive honey," used by consumers looking to differentiate wild hives from cultivated apiary boxes.
* **খাঁটি চাকের মধু / খাঁটি মধু:** "Pure hive honey" / "Pure honey," highly trusted descriptors in local bazaars.
* **জংলি মধু:** "Jungle honey," a regional term used to describe honey sourced from deep, uncultivated wild forest terrains.
* **র চাকের মধু / র মধু:** Phonetic Bengali adaptations of the English word "Raw" joined with local terms.

## Scientific / Trade Names

These are strict industry-specific terms, scientific markers, or regulatory classifications relevant to this product.

* **Apis dorsata Mangrove Honey:** The biological designation identifying the giant Asian honey bee (*Apis dorsata*) as the specific non-domesticated species that builds these wild forest hives.
* **Wild Mangrove Honey / Raw Forest Honey:** Industry trade names identifying the strict geographic and ecological biome (unmanaged tidal mangrove ecosystem) from which the floral nectar is gathered.
* **Unpasteurized Crushed-Comb Honey:** Technical trade category indicating the product has not undergone thermal processing and contains suspended elements of native wax, propolis, and bee pollen.
