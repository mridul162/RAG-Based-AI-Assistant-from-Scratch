# Pricing
## Price Drivers

The pricing of Sundarbans Raw Comb Honey is strictly determined by its unique wild origin, harvesting complexity, and seasonal availability rather than industrial manufacturing metrics.

* **High-Risk Manual Harvesting:** This honey is not produced in managed apiaries or boxes. It is collected deep inside the mangrove forests of the Sundarbans by traditional honey hunters known as *Moualis*. Navigating the terrain involves extreme physical risks, including encounters with saltwater crocodiles and Bengal tigers, which restricts the labor force and raises sourcing costs.
* **Strict Seasonality and Natural Yields:** Harvesting is limited to a narrow window every year (primarily between April and June). Annual yields depend entirely on rainfall, mangrove blossoming patterns, and natural conditions, making the supply highly volatile.
* **Intact Natural Matrix:** The product includes actual pieces of the wild honeycomb, rich in temperature-sensitive bee pollen, propolis, and royal jelly. Preserving these elements requires tedious manual extraction and coarse straining instead of high-speed centrifugal filtration, increasing production time per unit.

## Value Justification

The value of Sundarbans Raw Comb Honey rests on its absolute purity and therapeutic grade, positioning it as a premium functional food product rather than a standard commercial sweetener.

* **100% Wild Sourced:** Unlike regular honey sourced from commercial boxes placed near agricultural fields, this honey comes entirely from *Apis dorsata* bees nesting naturally in wild mangrove canopies.
* **Zero Thermal Processing:** Commercial honey is frequently heated to prevent crystallization and thin it down for ultra-filtration. This product is kept completely raw and unpasteurized, ensuring that natural plant enzymes, organic acids, and antioxidants from mangrove flowers (such as Khalisa and Goran) remain entirely intact.
* **Fair Trade Sourcing:** A significant portion of the product premium directly supports the livelihood of the indigenous Mouali communities, compensating them fairly for their risky forest expeditions and promoting sustainable harvesting methods that do not destroy the bee populations.
