> **Note on Seasonal and Batch Variation:** Wild Sundarbans honey is harvested manually by Moualis from *Apis dorsata* (giant honey bee) hives. Due to its multi-floral origin (primarily Khalisa, Goran, Geor, and Passur mangroves) and natural harvesting methods, minor variations in moisture content, pollen density, and sugar ratios will occur between batches and seasons.

## Key Nutrients

Sundarbans raw comb honey is distinct from commercial honey due to the preservation of temperature-sensitive bioactive compounds.

* **Bioactive Enzymes:** Contains active diastase, invertase, and glucose oxidase, which naturally produces trace amounts of hydrogen peroxide, giving the honey its powerful antibacterial properties.
* **Antioxidants and Polyphenols:** Rich in flavonoids and phenolic acids unique to mangrove flora, contributing to its dark amber color and high free-radical scavenging capacity.
* **Bee Pollen and Propolis Co-Factors:** Because the honey is raw and contains actual comb fragments, it retains trace amounts of amino acids, vitamins (complex B, Vitamin C), and minerals (potassium, magnesium, calcium, iron, and zinc) deposited by the bees.
* **Glycemic Index (GI):** Typically ranges between **55 and 65** (Moderate GI). While it causes a lower glycemic spike than refined sugar due to its high fructose-to-glucose ratio, it should still be consumed mindfully by individuals managing blood glucose levels.
