## Visual Indicators

<section id="visual">

| Feature | Authentic | Suspicious / Fake |
|---|---|---|
| **Comb structure** | Natural hexagonal wax cells, intact or partially broken; cells vary slightly in size | Uniform, machine-perfect cells; or no comb at all in a "comb honey" product |
| **Honey color** | Deep amber to dark golden-brown; color varies by season and floral source | Uniformly pale yellow or unnaturally bright — may indicate sugar syrup or blending |
| **Clarity** | Naturally cloudy or slightly opaque; may have visible pollen particles and fine air bubbles | Crystal-clear like filtered commercial honey — raw comb honey is never perfectly clear |
| **Crystallization** | Will crystallize partially or fully over time (weeks to months at room temperature) — this is a sign of purity, not spoilage | Stays liquid indefinitely at room temperature — may indicate added glucose syrup |
| **Wax capping** | Many cells sealed with natural beeswax caps (white to pale tan); some may be uncapped with honey visible | No wax caps, or wax appears artificially added after the fact |
| **Bee inclusions** | Occasional presence of bee pollen granules, trace propolis (dark sticky residue), or very rarely a fragment of wax — all normal | Completely sterile appearance with no natural inclusions |
| **Packaging** | Sold in clean jars with visible comb pieces submerged in honey | Comb floating loosely on top with thin, watery liquid below — may indicate dilution |

**Note on color:** Sundarbans honey is typically darker than farmed honey due to the diverse mangrove and forest flora. Light-colored honey claiming to be from the Sundarbans warrants extra scrutiny.

</section>

## Taste & Aroma Indicators

<section id="sensory">

**Aroma:**
- Authentic Sundarbans comb honey has a **complex, wild floral scent** with earthy, woody undertones from the mangrove environment.
- A faint fermented or smoky note is normal — the honey is raw and unprocessed.
- Absence of aroma, or an artificially sweet/caramel smell, suggests heating or sugar adulteration.

**Taste:**
- Initial taste is intensely floral, followed by a **slow, lingering sweetness** — not an immediate sharp sugar hit.
- There is a mild bitterness or astringency in the finish, characteristic of mangrove-source honey.
- The flavour profile is notably more **complex and robust** than farmed or blended honey.
- Raw comb honey may produce a very slight tingling or warming sensation on the tongue due to natural enzymes and pollen.

**Texture:**
- Honey from the comb is slightly viscous — it flows slowly. If it runs like water, suspect dilution.
- The wax comb itself is soft and chewable; it slowly releases residual honey and then forms a waxy ball that can be spat out — this is the expected experience of eating comb honey.

**Simple home test (spoon drop):**
- Place a small drop on a cold plate. Authentic raw honey holds its shape as a firm dome for several seconds. Adulterated honey spreads immediately into a thin puddle.

</section>

## Lab / Certification Checks

<section id="certification">

Bangladesh does not yet have a standardized certification mark exclusively for Sundarbans honey, but buyers can request or look for the following:

| Check | What to look for |
|---|---|
| **BSTI certification** | Bangladesh Standards and Testing Institution approval on the label — confirms basic food safety standards are met |
| **Moisture content** | Should be **≤ 20%** (ideally 17–19% for well-ripened comb honey). Higher moisture risks fermentation and indicates premature harvesting or dilution. Can be measured with a honey refractometer. |
| **HMF (Hydroxymethylfurfural)** | Should be **< 40 mg/kg** for raw honey. High HMF indicates overheating or adulteration with inverted sugar. |
| **Diastase activity** | Raw honey has active enzymes (diastase number ≥ 8 Schade units). Heated or fake honey loses enzyme activity. |
| **Pollen analysis** | A lab pollen test (melissopalynology) can confirm the presence of mangrove and Sundarbans-specific pollen (e.g., *Heritiera fomes*, *Excoecaria agallocha*). This is the most definitive origin verification. |
| **Sugar profile** | Authentic honey: fructose > glucose, minimal sucrose (< 5%). High sucrose = added cane or beet sugar. |
| **Seller traceability** | Reputable sellers can provide harvest batch information, Mouali (honey collector) region, and approximate collection season (typically March–May for the primary harvest). |

</section>

## Common Fakes to Watch For

<section id="fakes">

### 1. Sugar Syrup Honey
**What it is:** Plain sugar dissolved in water, sometimes with added color or flavor.
**How to spot it:** No crystallization over time; overly sweet with no complexity; spreads immediately on a cold surface; zero aroma; moisture > 22%.

---

### 2. Heated / Processed Honey Relabeled as Raw
**What it is:** Commercially pasteurized honey (often imported or farmed) packaged with a "raw" or "Sundarbans" label.
**How to spot it:** Crystal-clear appearance; no pollen or wax particles; no crystallization; bland taste; fails enzyme activity test.

---

### 3. Farmed Honey Sold as Wild Sundarbans Honey
**What it is:** Honey from managed beehives (often *Apis mellifera* colonies) blended or passed off as wild-harvested Sundarbans honey.
**How to spot it:** Lighter color and milder taste than genuine wild honey; pollen analysis will not show Sundarbans-specific flora; may lack the earthy, mangrove-specific finish.

---

### 4. Comb Honey with Added Wax
**What it is:** Artificial wax comb (sometimes foundation wax) inserted into the jar with thin or diluted honey poured over it.
**How to spot it:** Comb structure looks too uniform or machine-made; wax does not release honey when chewed; honey layer is thin and watery; overall weight seems low for the jar size.

---

### 5. Blended Honey
**What it is:** A mix of genuine Sundarbans honey (small %) with cheaper farmed or imported honey to increase volume.
**How to spot it:** Inconsistent flavor; color may be lighter than expected; pollen test will show mixed or non-Sundarbans pollen sources; price significantly below market rate.

</section>
