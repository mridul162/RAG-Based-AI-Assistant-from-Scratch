## When to Choose This Product

<section id="choose-this">

**Sundarbans Raw Comb Honey is the right choice when:**

- **You want an authentic Bangladeshi wild honey experience.** This is the only honey that comes directly from the wild hives of the Sundarbans mangrove ecosystem — a UNESCO World Heritage site. The terroir is unique and irreproducible.

- **You value the complete raw product.** The presence of natural comb, bee pollen, propolis, and royal jelly means you are consuming honey in its most complete, unaltered form. No farmed or commercial honey offers this in the same way.

- **You are buying as a premium gift.** Sundarbans comb honey is one of Bangladesh's most recognizable food gifts. The visual presentation of comb in honey is distinctive and carries cultural significance.

- **You prioritize local origin and supporting traditional livelihoods.** Purchase directly supports Mouali honey collectors — a traditional community whose livelihood depends on sustainable wild harvest.

- **You want the richest, most complex flavor profile** among locally available honeys. The diversity of the Sundarbans flora (mangrove, sundari, gewa, and many more) produces a depth of flavor that farmed single-source honey cannot replicate.

- **You are comparing against imported premium honey on price.** At BDT 1,500/kg, this product is significantly more affordable than genuine Sidr or Manuka honey of comparable quality, while offering a comparable — and arguably more locally relevant — premium experience.

## When a Competitor May Be Better

<section id="choose-competitor">

**Consider an alternative if:**

- **You need year-round, consistent supply.**
  Sundarbans honey is seasonal (primary harvest: approximately March–May). Stock may be unavailable or limited outside this window. Farmed raw honey or commercial honey is available year-round without supply interruption.

- **You need a budget everyday sweetener.**
  At BDT 400/250g, this product is priced as a premium. For daily cooking, baking, or high-volume use, commercial processed honey (BDT 100–250/250g) is far more economical, with acceptable taste for these applications.

- **You need clinically rated therapeutic honey.**
  Manuka honey (New Zealand) has internationally standardized UMF/MGO ratings that are independently verified for specific antibacterial activity (methylglyoxal concentration). If you need honey for a clinical application — wound dressing, H. pylori management, or similar — Manuka with a verified UMF rating is the more defensible choice, despite higher cost.

- **You or a recipient has bee pollen or propolis allergy.**
  Because this product is raw and unfiltered, pollen and propolis cannot be avoided. A filtered commercial honey may be safer for sensitive individuals.

- **You want a milder, less assertive flavor.**
  The Sundarbans honey flavor is bold, earthy, and complex. For people who prefer a light, neutral sweetness (e.g. in tea or on delicate foods), a mild farmed honey or commercial processed honey may be more suitable.

- **You cannot verify source authenticity.**
  If you are purchasing from an unverified seller and cannot confirm origin, a well-known commercial brand with consistent packaging and brand accountability carries lower authenticity risk than a premium wild honey that is widely faked.

</section>
