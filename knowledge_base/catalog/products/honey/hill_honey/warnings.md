# Warnings
## Allergen Information | অ্যালার্জি সম্পর্কিত তথ্য

<section>

Hill Honey (পাহাড়ি মধু) is a natural bee product and may contain trace amounts of pollen, bee-derived compounds, and naturally occurring plant materials collected from wild forest environments.

### Potential Allergens

| Allergen Source | Notes                                                          |
| --------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Bee products    | May affect individuals with known bee-product allergies        |
| Pollen          | Wild honey may contain small amounts of natural pollen         |
| Plant compounds | Derived from diverse wildflowers, herbs, and forest vegetation |

### Possible Allergy Symptoms

Individuals with honey, pollen, or bee-product sensitivities may experience:

* Itching or irritation of the mouth or throat
* Skin rash or hives
* Swelling of the lips, tongue, or face
* Digestive discomfort
* Allergic reactions ranging from mild to severe

If any signs of an allergic reaction occur, discontinue use and seek medical advice.

</section>

## Who Should Avoid | কারা এড়িয়ে চলবেন

<section>

### Infants Under 12 Months

**Honey should never be given to infants younger than 12 months of age.**

Honey may contain naturally occurring bacterial spores that can cause infant botulism, a rare but serious condition affecting young babies.

### Individuals with Honey or Bee Product Allergies

People with known allergies to:

* Honey
* Bee pollen
* Beeswax
* Royal jelly
* Bee stings (especially if associated with severe allergic reactions)

should exercise caution and consult a healthcare professional before consuming honey.

### Individuals with Strict Sugar Restrictions

Honey is a natural sweetener but still contains significant amounts of naturally occurring sugars. Individuals following medically prescribed low-sugar diets should consider total sugar intake before use.

</section>

## Drug/Condition Interactions | ওষুধ ও শারীরিক অবস্থার সাথে সম্পর্ক

<section>

There are no widely recognized major drug interactions associated with normal food-level consumption of honey. However, certain individuals should use caution.

### Diabetes and Blood Sugar Management

* Honey can raise blood glucose levels.
* People with diabetes or impaired glucose tolerance should monitor intake carefully.
* Portion control is important, even when using natural sweeteners.

### Weight Management Programs

* Honey contains calories and natural sugars.
* Excessive consumption may contribute to increased calorie intake.

### Medical Conditions Requiring Dietary Monitoring

Individuals managing the following conditions should consider honey consumption within their overall dietary plan:

* Diabetes mellitus
* Prediabetes
* Metabolic syndrome
* Obesity management programs
* Certain medically supervised low-carbohydrate diets

### Pregnancy and Breastfeeding

Honey is generally considered safe for healthy adults during pregnancy and breastfeeding when consumed as a food. Individuals with specific medical concerns should consult their healthcare provider.

### Medication Use

No significant interactions are known with common medications when honey is consumed in typical dietary amounts. However, individuals with complex medical conditions or specialized treatment plans should seek professional advice if uncertain.

</section>

## Safe Usage Limits | নিরাপদ গ্রহণের সীমা

<section>

Hill Honey is intended as a food product and should be consumed in moderation as part of a balanced diet.

### General Serving Guidance

| Age Group               | Suggested Intake                                             |
| ----------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Adults                  | 1–2 tablespoons (15–30 g) per day is a common dietary amount |
| Children over 1 year    | Smaller portions appropriate to age and dietary needs        |
| Infants under 12 months | Not recommended                                              |

### Important Usage Notes

* Honey is a concentrated source of natural sugars.
* Excessive intake may contribute to excess calorie and sugar consumption.
* Daily intake should be considered alongside other dietary sources of sugar.
* Individuals with diabetes or other metabolic conditions may require lower amounts based on medical advice.

### Storage Safety

* Store in a clean, dry, tightly sealed container.
* Keep away from direct sunlight and excessive heat.
* Use a dry spoon to prevent moisture contamination.
* Natural crystallization is normal and does not indicate spoilage.

### Product Safety Reminder

Hill Honey is a natural food product and is **not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any disease**. Any health-related use should be discussed with a qualified healthcare professional when appropriate.

</section>
