## Comparison Table — তুলনামূলক সারণি

<section id="comparison-table">

The three alternatives compared here are:

- **Mustard Honey (সরিষার মধু)** — The most widely available farmed honey in Bangladesh, harvested from managed Apis mellifera hives placed in mustard fields. Found in most grocery stores and online shops.
- **Sundarban Honey (সুন্দরবনের মধু)** — Wild honey from the Sundarbans mangrove forest, harvested by Mawali communities. A recognized premium wild honey with a distinct single-floral profile (primarily Khalsi flower).
- **Commercial Processed Honey (বাজারি প্রক্রিয়াজাত মধু)** — Packaged, branded honey (domestic or imported) sold in supermarkets, typically filtered, pasteurized, and sometimes blended.

## When to Choose Hill Honey — পাহাড়ি মধু কখন বেছে নেবেন

<section id="choose-hill-honey">

Choose Hill Honey if one or more of the following applies to you:

**You want raw, unprocessed honey with maximum natural properties.**
Hill honey is harvested with minimal intervention. Enzymes, antioxidants, and pollen are preserved in a way that commercially processed honey cannot match.

**You prefer a complex, bold flavor.**
If mild, uniform sweetness is not what you are looking for — if you want honey that tastes like it came from somewhere specific — hill honey delivers a layered flavor that reflects its diverse jungle forage.

**You value origin traceability.**
This honey comes from identifiable regions: Bandarban, Rangamati, or Sylhet. That is a narrower origin claim than "processed in Bangladesh" or "blended from multiple sources."

**You are buying as a gift or for someone who values quality.**
The dark color, thick texture, complex aroma, and premium positioning make it a strong gifting choice compared to generic supermarket honey.

**You want multi-floral wild honey at a more accessible price than Sundarban honey.**
Hill honey provides a comparable raw wild honey experience at roughly ৳1,000/kg, versus ৳1,200–৳1,800/kg for Sundarban honey.

**You are comfortable with natural batch variation.**
Each harvest differs slightly. If you appreciate that variation as a mark of authenticity rather than find it frustrating, this product suits you well.

</section>

## When a Competitor May Be Better — প্রতিযোগী পণ্য কখন ভালো হতে পারে

<section id="choose-competitor">

Be honest with yourself about these scenarios before choosing Hill Honey:

---

**Choose Mustard Honey if:**
- You need honey year-round without worrying about seasonal stock-outs
- You primarily use honey as a cooking ingredient or daily sweetener where subtle flavor differences are not important
- Budget is a priority — mustard honey at ৳400–৳700/kg is significantly cheaper and widely available
- You prefer a milder, less assertive flavor that does not dominate food or drinks
- You want consistent color and texture across every purchase

---

**Choose Sundarban Honey if:**
- You specifically want the smoky, bold, mangrove-forest flavor profile unique to Sundarbans Khalsi honey — it is genuinely distinct from hill honey and not interchangeable
- You are buying for someone who already knows and loves Sundarban honey specifically
- You prioritize a honey with longer-standing cultural recognition and a more established identity in Bangladesh's premium honey market
- The higher price point (৳1,200–৳1,800/kg) is not a barrier

---

**Choose Commercial Processed Honey if:**
- Convenience and year-round availability matter most to you
- You want a BSTI-certified or internationally labeled product for institutional, commercial, or food-business use where documentation is required
- You or someone in your household is sensitive to strong flavors, high pollen content, or has pollen-related allergies (processed honey has most pollen removed)
- You are using large volumes for baking, cooking, or industrial preparation where raw enzyme content is irrelevant

---

> **Summary note:** No honey type is universally "best." Hill honey is the right choice for buyers who value raw quality, origin, and flavor complexity, and who can work within seasonal availability. It is not the right choice for buyers who need consistent year-round supply, mild flavor, or a low price point.

</section>
