## Key Nutrients | প্রধান পুষ্টি উপাদানসমূহ

* **Antioxidants & Polypolyphenols:** Dark amber wild honey from the Chittagong Hill Tracts contains significantly higher concentrations of phenolic compounds and flavonoids than commercial, single-source farmed honey.
* **Trace Minerals:** Contains bioavailable amounts of Iron, Zinc, Potassium, Magnesium, and Manganese absorbed from undisturbed hill soil and native medicinal flora.
* **Active Enzymes:** Raw and unpasteurized processing preserves heat-sensitive beneficial enzymes including diastase, invertase, and glucose oxidase.

> **Glycemic Index (GI) Note:** Wild hill honey typically ranges between a moderate GI of 55 to 58. Due to its higher fructose-to-glucose ratio compared to commercial honey, it causes a more gradual rise in blood sugar, but should still be consumed mindfully by individuals managing diabetes.

## Batch & Seasonal Variations | ব্যাচ এবং ঋতুভিত্তিক পরিবর্তন

* **Dry Season Harvest (Winter/Spring):** Typically yields honey with lower moisture levels (thicker consistency), darker coloration, and a more concentrated sweetness.
* **Monsoon Harvest (Pre/Post Rainy Season):** The honey may display a slightly higher moisture content (up to 19.5%) and a lighter amber hue due to the active blooming of soft rain-fed hill flora.
* **Crystallization:** Due to the natural sugars present in unheated honey, minor crystallization or cloudiness may occur over time—especially in cooler winter temperatures. This is a definitive sign of raw, unadulterated honey and does not compromise nutritional value.
