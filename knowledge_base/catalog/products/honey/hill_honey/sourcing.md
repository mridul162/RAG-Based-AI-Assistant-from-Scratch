# Sourcing
## Geographic Origin | ভৌগোলিক উৎস

<section>

Hill Honey (পাহাড়ি মধু) is sourced from the forested and mountainous regions of Bangladesh, particularly the Chittagong Hill Tracts and selected hill ecosystems where wild bee populations naturally thrive.

### Primary Sourcing Regions

| Region                 | Characteristics                                                               |
| ---------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Bandarban              | Dense forests, diverse wildflowers, medicinal plants, and mountain vegetation |
| Rangamati              | Mixed hill forests and natural flowering trees                                |
| Chittagong Hill Tracts | Rich biodiversity and varied nectar sources throughout the flowering season   |
| Sylhet                 | Forested hills, native flowering plants, and natural bee habitats             |

The bees forage across a wide range of wild flora, including forest flowers, shrubs, herbs, flowering trees, and seasonal mountain vegetation. Because nectar comes from multiple natural sources rather than a single cultivated crop, Hill Honey is generally classified as a multifloral wild honey.

The exact floral composition may vary between regions and harvesting seasons, contributing to natural differences in flavor, aroma, and color from one batch to another.

</section>

## Harvest Method | সংগ্রহ পদ্ধতি

<section>

Hill Honey is primarily collected from naturally occurring bee colonies found in forest and hill environments. Unlike commercial monoculture honey production, wild hill honey depends heavily on natural ecosystems and seasonal flowering patterns.

### Typical Harvest Process

1. Local honey collectors identify active wild bee colonies in forested hill areas.
2. Honeycombs are harvested during periods when honey stores have matured.
3. Collected combs are transported from remote collection points.
4. Honey is extracted through filtering and separation from wax and natural debris.
5. The honey is then stored, inspected, and packaged for consumer use.

### Natural Characteristics of Wild Harvesting

* Bees collect nectar freely from diverse forest plants.
* No artificial flavoring or sweetening is involved.
* Floral sources change naturally according to local biodiversity.
* Harvest volumes vary depending on weather conditions and flowering intensity.

Because the product originates from wild environments, natural variation between harvests is expected and considered a normal characteristic of authentic hill honey.

</section>

## Season & Timing | মৌসুম ও সংগ্রহকাল

<section>

Hill Honey is a seasonal product, with availability depending on flowering cycles, rainfall patterns, and environmental conditions within the hill regions.

| Factor              | Influence on Harvest                       |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------ |
| Flowering season    | Determines nectar availability             |
| Rainfall            | Affects bee activity and nectar production |
| Forest biodiversity | Influences flavor and aroma profile        |
| Weather conditions  | Impacts harvest volume and timing          |

Unlike year-round agricultural products, wild hill honey production cannot be fully controlled. Harvest quantities may differ significantly from season to season.

As a result, premium-quality Hill Honey is often available only during specific collection periods and may have limited stock throughout the year.

</section>

## Supply Chain | উৎস থেকে ভোক্তা পর্যন্ত যাত্রা

<section>

### Source to Consumer Journey

1. **Wild Forest Collection**
   Honey is gathered from naturally occurring bee colonies in hill and forest regions.

2. **Initial Handling**
   Honeycombs are transported from collection sites to processing points.

3. **Extraction & Filtration**
   Honey is separated from wax and filtered to remove visible impurities while preserving its natural character.

4. **Quality Assessment**
   The honey is checked for cleanliness, appearance, and packaging suitability.

5. **Packaging**
   Honey is packed into food-grade containers for storage and distribution.

6. **Distribution to Consumers**
   Packaged honey is supplied to retailers and customers.

### Sustainability & Ethical Considerations

* Sourcing often supports local hill communities and traditional honey collectors.
* Wild honey collection depends on maintaining healthy forest ecosystems.
* Responsible harvesting practices help protect bee populations and encourage long-term sustainability.
* Seasonal harvesting reduces pressure on natural bee habitats and follows natural production cycles.

Because Hill Honey is closely connected to natural biodiversity, the preservation of forests and flowering habitats plays an important role in maintaining future honey production.

</section>
