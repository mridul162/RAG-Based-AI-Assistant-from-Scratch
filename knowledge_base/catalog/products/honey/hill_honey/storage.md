# Storage
## Ideal Conditions | আদর্শ সংরক্ষণের পরিবেশ

Pure, raw wild hill honey is naturally self-preserving due to its high sugar concentration and low moisture content. However, to maintain its active therapeutic enzymes, medicinal properties, and complex forest flavor profile, it must be stored according to the following environmental conditions:

* **Temperature:** Store at room temperature, ideally between **18°C and 25°C**. Avoid exposure to direct sunlight, cooking appliances, or heat sources.
* **Refrigeration Warning:** Do not refrigerate. Refrigeration does not spoil honey, but it rapidly accelerates the natural process of crystallization, making the honey solid, grainy, and difficult to pour.
* **Humidity Management:** Honey is highly hygroscopic, meaning it actively absorbs moisture from the surrounding air. In the high-humidity climate of Bangladesh, the container lid must remain tightly sealed whenever not in use to prevent atmospheric moisture from diluting the honey, which can trigger unwanted fermentation.

---

## Container Recommendations | পাত্র ব্যবহারের নির্দেশনা

The type of storage container used directly affects the quality and shelf life of raw hill honey:

* **Recommended (Glass Jars):** High-quality glass is the ideal storage medium. It is non-reactive, completely impermeable to oxygen and moisture, and does not leach chemicals into the honey over extended storage periods.
* **Acceptable (Food-Grade Plastic):** High-density polyethylene (HDPE) or PET plastic bottles are acceptable for short-to-medium-term use, provided they are stored away from light and heat.
* **Strictly Prohibited (Metal Containers):** Never store raw honey in unlined metal containers (such as iron, copper, aluminum, or low-grade stainless steel). The natural organic acids present in wild hill honey can slowly react with the metal, oxidizing the container, altering the honey's flavor, and introducing harmful chemical compounds.

## Shelf Life | মেয়াদকাল

Because this product is unpasteurized and pure, its baseline shelf life relies heavily on proper storage habits:

| Packaging State | Expected Shelf Life | Storage Guideline |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Unopened Bottle** | Practically indefinite (labeled as **2–3 years** for regulatory standards) | Store in a cool, dark pantry or cupboard away from direct sunlight. |
| **Opened Bottle** | **1–2 years** (retaining optimal nutrient potency) | Ensure the lid is screwed on tightly after every single use to block moisture and airborne yeast. |

## Signs of Spoilage & Quality Shifts | নষ্ট হওয়া বা গুণগত পরিবর্তন চেনার উপায়

It is critical to distinguish between natural physical alterations and genuine spoilage in raw wild honey.

### 1. Natural Alterations (Safe to Consume)

* **Crystallization:** The honey becomes cloudy, thick, or forms granular sugar crystals. This is a natural physical reaction of raw glucose separating from the water and is a definitive marker of 100% pure honey. To reverse it, place the jar in a warm water bath (under **40°C**) until it liquifies. Never microwave it, as excessive heat destroys the live enzymes.
* **Layering / Foam:** A fine white froth or bubble layer at the top of the bottle is simply trapped micro-air bubbles or wild pollen particles rising. This is safe and common in raw, unfiltered jungle honey.

### 2. Genuine Spoilage (Do Not Consume)

Due to its origin in the humid Chittagong Hill Tracts, if the honey's moisture content rises above 20% from unsealed storage, it can ferment:

* **Fermentation:** Indicated by a distinct, sharp, alcohol-like or sour vinegar smell.
* **Active Bubbling:** Continuous, aggressive bubbling or a bulging container lid caused by gas pressure from active wild yeast fermentation.
* **Sour Taste:** A prominent, unpleasant sour or acidic taste replacing the natural herbal sweetness.
