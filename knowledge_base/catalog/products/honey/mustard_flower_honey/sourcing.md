# Sourcing
## Geographic origin

<section>

Mustard Flower Honey (সরিষা ফুলের মধু) is sourced from agricultural regions of Bangladesh where mustard is widely cultivated as a winter crop. During the flowering season, extensive mustard fields provide abundant nectar for honeybees, making this one of the country's most important seasonal honey varieties.

Common sourcing regions may include:

* North Bengal agricultural zones
* Rajshahi region
* Dinajpur region
* Rangpur region
* Other mustard-growing farming areas across Bangladesh

The specific floral characteristics of the honey are influenced by local climate conditions, soil quality, flowering density, and the surrounding agricultural environment.

Because mustard fields often cover large continuous areas during the flowering season, bees can collect nectar predominantly from mustard blossoms, resulting in honey with a distinctive aroma, flavor, and appearance.

</section>

---

## Harvest method

<section>

### Nectar collection by honeybees

* Honeybees forage on blooming mustard flowers during peak flowering periods.
* Nectar collected from mustard blossoms is transported to the hive and naturally converted into honey through enzymatic activity and moisture reduction.

### Beekeeping practices

* Beekeepers commonly place managed bee colonies near mustard cultivation areas to take advantage of seasonal nectar flow.
* Colonies remain in the fields throughout the flowering period to maximize nectar collection.

### Honey extraction

* Once honey-filled combs are capped by bees, frames are removed from the hive.
* Honey is typically extracted using centrifugal extraction methods that separate honey from the comb without destroying the wax structure.
* The extracted honey is then filtered to remove wax particles and natural debris.

### Minimal processing

* Pure Mustard Flower Honey is generally processed with the goal of preserving its natural characteristics.
* Filtration may be performed for cleanliness, while excessive heating is generally avoided to help retain natural flavor, aroma, and quality attributes.

</section>

## Supply chain

<section>

### Step 1: Mustard cultivation

Farmers grow mustard crops during the winter season across agricultural regions of Bangladesh.

### Step 2: Hive placement

Beekeepers position honeybee colonies near flowering mustard fields to allow efficient nectar collection.

### Step 3: Honey production

Bees collect nectar from mustard flowers and convert it into honey within the hive.

### Step 4: Harvest and extraction

Honey-filled frames are harvested and extracted using standard beekeeping methods.

### Step 5: Filtering and quality handling

The honey is filtered to remove natural impurities such as wax fragments while preserving its natural composition.

### Step 6: Packaging

Honey is packed into clean, food-grade containers of various sizes suitable for retail sale.

### Step 7: Distribution

The packaged honey is supplied to retailers, online stores, distributors, and end consumers.

</section>

## Sustainability & ethical sourcing considerations

<section>

### Pollination support

* Honeybees contribute to pollination activities within agricultural ecosystems.
* Bee activity can support crop productivity and biodiversity in farming landscapes.

### Support for rural livelihoods

* Honey production provides seasonal income opportunities for beekeepers and farming communities.
* Local sourcing helps support agricultural economies in mustard-growing regions.

### Responsible harvesting

* Sustainable beekeeping practices aim to maintain healthy bee colonies while harvesting surplus honey.
* Responsible hive management helps ensure continued honey production across future seasons.

### Natural seasonal production

* Mustard Flower Honey is produced according to natural flowering cycles rather than year-round artificial production systems.
* Seasonality is an important indicator of its agricultural and floral origin.

</section>
