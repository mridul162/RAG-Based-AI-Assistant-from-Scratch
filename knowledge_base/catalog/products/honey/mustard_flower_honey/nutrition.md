## Key Nutrients

* **High Glucose-to-Fructose Ratio:** Mustard honey naturally contains a higher ratio of glucose compared to many other honey varieties. This specific chemical composition causes it to **crystallize quickly** in cooler temperatures (a natural, authentic characteristic of pure *sorisha modhu*).
* **Antioxidants and Flavonoids:** Rich in phenolic compounds and organic acids derived directly from mustard (*Brassica*) nectar. These elements contribute to its distinct slightly pungent aroma and wellness benefits.
* **Trace Minerals:** Contains bioavailable trace amounts of Iron, Zinc, Potassium, and Magnesium which assist in overall metabolic functions.
* **Enzymes:** Features live enzymes such as diastase, invertase, and glucose oxidase, introduced naturally by the bees during honey processing.

---

## Glycemic Index (GI)

* **Estimated GI Range:** 55 – 62 (Moderate)
* **Context:** Because of its higher glucose concentration, Mustard Flower Honey has a slightly higher glycemic index compared to high-fructose honeys (like Sundarban or Litchi honey). It provides a rapid, clean energy source, making it excellent for post-workout recovery or winter warming properties, but it should be consumed mindfully by individuals managing blood sugar levels.
