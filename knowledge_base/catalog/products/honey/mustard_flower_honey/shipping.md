# Shipping & Logistics Protocol: Mustard Flower Honey (সরিষা ফুলের মধু)

This document provides logistics data, protective packaging requirements, temperature thresholds, and regulatory guidelines for processing and shipping orders of Mustard Flower Honey.

---

## Temperature Requirements

Proper transit environment management is necessary to maintain the physical state and cosmetic appeal of raw honey:

* **Transit Temperature Zone:** Maintain between **15°C to 30°C** during transit.
* **Winter Transit Dynamics:** In transit during winter months, the product is highly susceptible to rapid solidification and uniform crystallization due to its naturally high glucose profile. This is a safe physical transition and does not disrupt product safety or trigger shipping insurance liabilities.
* **Thermal Protection:** Avoid exposing transport vehicles or sorting facilities to extreme heat zones above 40°C. Prolonged heat exposure thinning the honey will break down active health enzymes, alter the delicate floral aroma, and increase risk of structural seal expansion.

## Packaging Notes

Because our product variants (250g, 500g, 1kg) use glass jars as the primary container material to preserve chemical purity, strict shatter-proofing and leak-prevention protocols must be followed during sorting and sorting assembly:

* **Primary Seal Inspection:** Every jar must have an airtight, induction-sealed foil inner cap below the screw lid to prevent leakage from volumetric expansion or atmospheric pressure changes in transit.
* **Cushioning Requirements:**
* Wrap each primary glass container individually in a minimum of **3 layers of industrial bubble wrap** or specialized corrugated paper sleeves.
* Pack jars tightly into heavy-duty corrugated cardboard master cartons. Fill any empty internal space completely using biodegradable void fill or packing peanuts to prevent internal movement.


* **Orientation and Fragility Labeling:** Master shipping cartons containing glass variants must display clear external warning indicators:
* `FRAGILE: HANDLE WITH CARE`
* `THIS SIDE UP` (Directional arrows indicating proper vertical orientation)
* `KEEP AWAY FROM DIRECT HEAT`

## Regulatory Considerations

Domestic transport and inventory movement within Bangladesh must strictly adhere to regular food transportation standards:

* **Product Classification:** Classified as a Non-Hazardous, Non-Perishable, Processed Agricultural Food Commodity.
* **Mandatory Documentation (Domestic Freight):** Inter-district bulk distribution requires standard commercial invoices confirming regional origin (e.g., North Bengal, Rajshahi, Dinajpur harvesting hubs) along with batch testing certificates verifying the absence of heavy metal contamination and sugar-syrup adulteration.
* **Seasonal Shipping Deadlines:** No absolute delivery lockouts exist, but transit timelines should prioritize efficient winter dispatch windows when demand is highly elevated and internal stock volumes are peak active.
