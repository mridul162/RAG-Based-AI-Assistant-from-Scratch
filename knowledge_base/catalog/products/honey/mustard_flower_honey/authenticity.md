# Mustard Flower Honey — Authenticity Guide
**Product:** Mustard Flower Honey (সরিষা ফুলের মধু)
**Product ID:** mustard_flower_honey
**Last Updated:** 2026-06-11

> This document helps buyers, support agents, and QA teams verify that a batch of Mustard Flower Honey is genuine. All indicators below are based on the natural properties of honey collected from blooming mustard (*Brassica* spp.) fields in Bangladesh.

---

## Visual Indicators

<section id="visual">

Mustard flower honey has a distinct appearance that sets it apart from multifloral or other single-origin honeys.

| Indicator | Genuine | Suspect |
|---|---|---|
| **Color** | Light golden to pale amber when liquid; creamy white to pale yellow when crystallized | Unusually dark brown or water-clear transparency |
| **Crystallization** | Crystallizes readily — often within weeks of harvest, especially in cool weather. Fine, smooth, almost butter-like texture | Stays permanently liquid for many months (may indicate added glucose syrup or heating) |
| **Clarity** | Slightly hazy or opaque when fresh; not perfectly clear | Perfectly clear like water (over-filtered or diluted) |
| **Foam/Bubbles** | Small natural foam layer may appear on top shortly after collection | Excessive or persistent large bubbles (fermentation from high moisture) |
| **Consistency** | Thick, slow-dripping flow; forms a ribbon when poured | Watery, flows quickly like syrup |

**Crystallization note:** Because mustard honey has a high glucose-to-fructose ratio, crystallization is normal and expected — especially during and after Bangladesh's winter season (November–February). Crystallized mustard honey is not spoiled. Buyers should treat rapid crystallization as a positive authenticity signal, not a defect.

</section>

## Taste & Aroma Indicators

<section id="sensory">

Mustard flower honey has a strong sensory profile that is difficult to replicate with cheap adulterants.

**Aroma**
- Characteristic pungent, slightly sharp floral scent — noticeably different from mild multifloral honey
- Warm, earthy undertone associated with mustard blossoms
- Should not smell fermented, sour, or chemical

**Taste**
- Initial sweetness followed by a mild pungent or slightly spicy finish (from mustard flower nectar compounds)
- Full-bodied, not thin or watery on the palate
- Slight warming sensation in the throat — this is the characteristic most buyers associate with its traditional use in cough and cold relief
- Aftertaste lingers pleasantly; does not taste of plain sugar or corn syrup

**Texture in the mouth**
- Smooth and coating when liquid
- Fine-grained and creamy when crystallized — melts evenly on the tongue without grittiness

> If the honey tastes only of plain sweetness with no pungent or floral note, the mustard flower content may be low or absent.

</section>

## Lab / Certification Checks

<section id="certification">

For bulk buyers, institutional procurement, or quality-sensitive use cases, the following checks are recommended.

**Pollen analysis (Melissopalynology)**
- A lab microscopy test of the honey sample should show a dominant presence of *Brassica* (mustard) pollen grains
- This is the most reliable method to confirm floral origin
- Genuine mustard flower honey from Bangladesh typically shows >45% mustard pollen in the pollen profile

**Moisture content**
- Should be ≤20% by weight (measured via refractometer)
- Higher moisture (>21%) increases fermentation risk and may indicate dilution with water

**HMF (Hydroxymethylfurfural)**
- Should be <40 mg/kg for fresh honey
- Elevated HMF (>80 mg/kg) indicates overheating or old/adulterated stock

**Diastase activity**
- Natural honey contains active enzymes; diastase number should be ≥8 (Schade scale)
- Very low diastase suggests the honey was overheated or heavily processed

**Brix / Sugar profile**
- Natural mustard honey: predominantly glucose + fructose
- Presence of sucrose >5% or detectable high-fructose corn syrup (C4 sugars via SCIRA isotope test) is a red flag

**Bangladesh Standards and Testing Institution (BSTI)**
- Look for a valid BSTI certification mark on packaged honey if purchasing from a commercial seller
- Certification indicates the product met national food safety standards at time of testing

</section>

## Common Fakes to Watch For

<section id="adulteration">

Mustard flower honey is one of Bangladesh's most affordable and popular honeys, which also makes it a frequent target for adulteration. Below are the most commonly encountered methods.

**1. Sugar syrup dilution**
- Plain sugar (sucrose) or glucose syrup dissolved in water and mixed with a small amount of real honey
- *How to spot it:* Honey feels thin, flows quickly, and lacks the pungent mustard aroma. A refractometer will show low Brix. The water test (a drop in a glass of water) may show it dispersing quickly rather than sinking as a mass.

**2. Rice syrup or corn syrup blending**
- Industrial syrups added to bulk up volume
- *How to spot it:* Taste is flat and one-dimensionally sweet. Honey does not crystallize naturally. Lab SCIRA isotope testing can confirm C4 sugar contamination.

**3. Blending with cheaper multifloral honey and labeling as mustard**
- Real honey, but not from mustard flowers — the premium floral origin is falsely claimed
- *How to spot it:* Pollen microscopy will show a mixed or unrelated pollen profile. Aroma and taste will be milder, lacking the characteristic mustard pungency.

**4. Overheating to prevent crystallization**
- Genuine honey heated above 60°C to extend liquid shelf life and improve appearance
- *How to spot it:* Honey that never crystallizes despite being months old. HMF levels will be elevated in lab testing. Enzyme activity will be reduced or absent.

**5. Artificial flavoring**
- Cheap honey or syrup with added mustard or floral flavoring compounds
- *How to spot it:* Aroma is sharp or chemical rather than naturally warm and floral. Taste does not have the characteristic throat-warming finish. Pollen test will show no mustard pollen.

### Quick At-Home Checks

| Test | Method | Genuine result |
|---|---|---|
| **Thumb test** | Place a small drop on your thumb | Stays in place, does not spread or drip immediately |
| **Water test** | Drop a small amount into a glass of still water | Sinks and stays as a lump; does not dissolve immediately |
| **Crystallization check** | Store at room temperature for 2–4 weeks in winter | Should show signs of granulation or full crystallization |
| **Aroma check** | Open container and smell before tasting | Should have a noticeable, slightly pungent floral mustard scent |
| **Burn test** | Dip a cotton wick in honey and attempt to light it | Pure honey will burn; water-diluted honey will not ignite |

> Note: At-home tests can indicate problems but are not conclusive. For high-stakes purchasing decisions, request a lab report from the supplier.

</section>
